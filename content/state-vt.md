# Vermont Solar Incentives & Policy

Vermont offers one of the more complete state-level solar frameworks in the Northeast: **net metering** for systems up to **500 kW**, a **Standard Offer** program for larger renewable projects, a **sales-tax exemption**, a **100% property-tax exemption** on the added value, and utility programs — notably **Green Mountain Power's (GMP) battery / Bring-Your-Own-Device (BYOD)** offerings. This guide covers both **residential** and **commercial** solar where the rules diverge.

> **Educational use only.** Program terms, rates, and statutes change frequently. Verify every figure below against official Vermont sources and [DSIRE](https://www.dsireusa.org/) before you rely on it. Today's date for this guide is **2026-06-30**.

## Overview

Vermont's value stack for solar includes:

- **Net metering** (PUC-regulated) for systems **up to 500 kW**.
- The **Standard Offer** program (part of the former SPEED framework) providing **long-term fixed-price contracts** for renewable facilities **up to ~2.2 MW**, administered by **VEPP Inc.**
- A **sales-tax exemption** and a **100% property-tax exemption** on the added value of a system.
- Strong **utility storage programs**, especially from **Green Mountain Power (GMP)**, which serves roughly three-quarters of Vermont customers.

Regulation runs through the **Vermont Public Utility Commission (PUC)** and the **Department of Public Service (DPS)**.

## Net Metering / Net Billing

Vermont allows **net metering for solar projects of 500 kW or less.** See [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md) for the general mechanics.

- **Compensation:** The PUC sets **net-metering rates by category**, combining the utility's **blended residential rate** with **REC adjustments/adjustors** that can add to or subtract from the credit. These are **updated periodically** (a biennial update process is ongoing in 2026).
- **Reported values (verify):** The statewide blended residential rate has been cited around **$0.18–$0.21/kWh** across 2024–2026; GMP month-end excess credits have been described near retail (~$0.14/kWh in some materials). **These figures move with rate cases — confirm the current category rates at the PUC.**
- **Group (virtual) net metering:** Under **Act 179**, starting **2026**, group net metering is **limited to on-site or adjacent-parcel systems** — a meaningful change for community/aggregated arrangements. **Verify current group rules.**

> **Commercial note:** Larger commercial arrays approaching or exceeding 500 kW should evaluate the **Standard Offer** (below) or a power-purchase structure rather than net metering.

## Standard Offer

The **Standard Offer** program (rooted in Vermont's **SPEED** framework) provides **long-term, fixed-price contracts** from utilities for qualifying renewable facilities **up to ~2.2 MW**, administered by **VEPP Inc.** on behalf of the PUC.

- Targets **commercial / developer-scale** projects, not typical rooftop residential.
- Capacity is allocated in blocks with periodic solicitations; **verify current availability and pricing** with VEPP and the PUC.

## SREC / RECs

Vermont operates under a **Renewable Energy Standard (RES)** rather than a classic tradable retail SREC market. RECs from net-metered and Standard-Offer systems are handled under program rules (RECs may be retained or adjusted for in net-metering compensation).

- Do not assume a separate **retail SREC cash stream** the way New Jersey/Massachusetts historically offered; confirm **who owns the RECs** in your specific arrangement.
- For how tradable-certificate markets work in general, see [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md).

## State Tax Credits

Vermont offers **no state income-tax credit** for solar for residential or commercial taxpayers. Its state-level benefits are the **sales-tax** and **property-tax** exemptions (below), plus utility programs.

## Federal Tax Credits

- **Residential (§25D):** **Ended for systems placed in service after December 31, 2025** under the **One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA)**. **2026 residential buyers receive no federal §25D credit.**
- **Commercial (ITC §48 / tech-neutral §48E):** Businesses may still claim the **commercial ITC** on qualifying projects, subject to current begin-construction / placed-in-service and phase-out rules, plus possible bonus adders and depreciation.

See [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md).

## Utility Incentives / Rebates

Vermont's utility programs — especially **Green Mountain Power (GMP)** — are a distinctive strength:

- **GMP Bring-Your-Own-Device (BYOD):** Customers who add a qualifying **battery** to their solar can receive an **upfront incentive (reported up to ~$10,500)** in exchange for making stored energy available to GMP during peak events (typically a **10-year** commitment). Reported enrollment deadline around **Sept 30, 2026** — **verify current cap and deadline.**
- **GMP Energy Storage System (ESS) Lease:** Lease **home batteries (e.g., two Powerwalls)** for a fixed monthly or upfront cost over ~10 years, with GMP peak dispatch rights. Reported terms have included roughly **$55/month or a ~$5,500 upfront** option — **verify current pricing.**
- **Commercial:** GMP and other utilities offer **business storage / peak-shaving** programs; terms differ from residential BYOD. Confirm current commercial offerings.

> **Verify all storage terms directly with GMP** — caps, deadlines, and dispatch rules change each program year.

## Sales Tax Exemption

Vermont **exempts solar energy systems from the state sales tax**, saving on the equipment portion of installed cost (Vermont's sales tax is ~6%). Applies to residential and commercial purchases. **Verify current scope** with the Vermont Department of Taxes.

## Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

Vermont provides a **property-tax exemption on the added value** of a solar system — the improvement generally does **not** raise your assessed value/property taxes. This is a **statewide** treatment covering residential and commercial systems.

> **Verify** eligible system types and any municipal filing requirements with the Vermont Department of Taxes / your town.

## Permitting & Interconnection Notes

- **Permitting / siting:** Vermont uses a **Certificate of Public Good (CPG)** process through the **PUC** for generation projects, including net-metered solar (with streamlined registration paths for small residential systems). This is distinct from many states' purely municipal permitting.
- **Interconnection:** Governed by **PUC rules** and utility procedures; small systems use expedited review, larger systems trigger studies.
- **CPG rules interact with net-metering category and group-net-metering changes** — confirm the current CPG/registration path for your system size.

## Bottom Line

- **Residential:** A strong stack — **net metering + sales-tax exemption + 100% property-tax exemption + GMP storage incentives** — even though **§25D ended for 2026 buyers** and there's no state income-tax credit. Pairing solar with a battery via **GMP BYOD/ESS** can materially improve economics.
- **Commercial:** Same exemptions, plus access to the **federal commercial ITC/§48E + depreciation**, **net metering up to 500 kW**, and the **Standard Offer** for larger projects. Model group-net-metering limits (Act 179) carefully.
