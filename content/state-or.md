# Oregon Solar Incentives & Policy

Oregon offers one of the friendlier rooftop-solar climates on the West Coast despite modest sunshine: **full 1-for-1 net metering**, **no state sales tax** (nothing to exempt), a **statutory property-tax exemption** for the added value of a solar system, and **rebate-based** incentives delivered through the Energy Trust of Oregon and the Oregon Department of Energy rather than a state income-tax credit. This guide summarizes the current landscape for both residential and commercial solar as of mid-2026. Programs change frequently — confirm live figures with the administering agency and the **Database of State Incentives for Renewables & Efficiency ([DSIRE](https://www.dsireusa.org/))** before modeling a project.

For background on how export compensation works, see [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md). For the federal side, see [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md). For neighboring-state and cross-state comparisons, see [More State Programs](incentive-more-states.md) and the [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md).

## At-a-Glance Summary

| Policy Lever | Residential | Commercial |
|---|---|---|
| **Net metering** | Full **1:1 retail-rate** (PGE, Pacific Power); ~25 kW cap | Net metering up to **2 MW** |
| **SREC / REC market** | None (no solar carve-out; RECs via WREGIS) | None tradable at retail |
| **State income-tax credit** | **None** (RETC ended 2017) | None |
| **Cash rebates** | Energy Trust incentives + ODOE Solar+Storage rebate *(funds limited — verify)* | Energy Trust solar-for-business/nonprofit |
| **Sales-tax exemption** | **N/A — Oregon has no state sales tax** | N/A |
| **Property tax** | **100% exemption** on added value (ORS 307.175) | Same statute applies |

> **Framing:** Oregon replaced its old solar *tax credit* with *rebate* programs. Because rebate pools (especially ODOE's) are small and can exhaust in days, timing and confirmation of live funding are essential.

## Overview

Oregon's two large investor-owned utilities — **Portland General Electric (PGE)** and **Pacific Power** — cover most customers and fund the **Energy Trust of Oregon (ETO)**, a ratepayer-financed nonprofit that administers most residential and commercial solar incentives in their territories. State energy policy and rebates are set by the **Oregon Public Utility Commission (OPUC)** and the **Oregon Department of Energy (ODOE)**. Oregon has **no state solar income-tax credit** — the former Residential Energy Tax Credit ended in 2017 — so incentive value comes through net metering, ETO/ODOE rebates, the property-tax exemption, and federal treatment.

## Net Metering / Net Billing

Oregon requires its investor-owned utilities to offer **net metering at the full retail rate**, under **OAR Chapter 860, Division 39** (administered by the OPUC).

- **Full 1-for-1 retail-rate credit** for exported kWh. Excess credits roll over month to month.
- **Annual true-up is March 31.** Any credit balance remaining at year-end is **not paid to the customer** — it is donated (at the utility's avoided-cost rate) to the utility's low-income assistance program. Sizing a system to avoid a large stranded surplus matters here.
- **Capacity limits:** Residential net-metering systems are generally capped around **25 kW**; **non-residential up to 2 MW**.
- **PGE's legacy Solar Payment Option** (a feed-in-style program) is being wound down as 15-year contracts expire; affected customers transition into standard net metering.

> A commonly repeated rumor that PGE plans to cut net-metering credit value by 20–30% has **no formal OPUC filing** as of mid-2026. Treat it as unconfirmed and **verify** against current OPUC dockets and Pacific Power Schedule 135 before relying on any change. See [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md) for the mechanics.

## SREC / RECs

**Oregon has no tradable SREC market.** Its Renewable Portfolio Standard has **no solar carve-out**, so there is no dedicated solar-REC price signal. Generic RECs are tracked through the **Western Renewable Energy Generation Information System (WREGIS)** and retired by utilities for compliance; residential owners generally cannot monetize them meaningfully. Contrast this with the compliance markets in our [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md).

Oregon's RPS (SB 1547, 2016) requires the large IOUs to reach **≥50% renewable by 2040**, with an interim **27% by 2025** milestone; smaller utilities have lower tiers. *Verify current milestone status at the OPUC/ODOE.*

## State Tax Credits

**None.** Oregon's **Residential Energy Tax Credit (RETC)** ended **December 31, 2017**, and ODOE's tax-credit programs were **not replaced** by another credit — they were replaced by the rebate programs below. There is no Oregon state income-tax credit for residential or commercial solar today.

## Federal Tax Credits

Federal incentives are national, not Oregon-specific, but they drive project economics:

- **Residential (§25D):** The federal Residential Clean Energy Credit **ended after December 31, 2025** under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA). Systems placed in service in 2026 and later generally **cannot claim §25D**. Confirm with a tax professional.
- **Commercial (ITC / §48E):** The technology-neutral **Clean Electricity Investment Credit (§48E)** generally remains available for commercial and utility-scale projects, subject to phase-out schedules, wage/apprenticeship rules, and bonus adders. See [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md).

## Utility Incentives / Rebates

Oregon's core cash incentives are **rebates**, not tax credits:

- **Energy Trust of Oregon (residential):** Upfront solar incentives for PGE and Pacific Power customers. Standard-market incentives have historically been in the low hundreds of dollars per kW (often cited around a flat ~$2,500 per system or ~$0.20/W DC). **Beginning in 2026, ETO incentive rates step down quarterly**, so any figure is time-sensitive — **verify the current-quarter rate sheet.**
- **Energy Trust "Solar Within Reach" (income-qualified):** Substantially higher incentives (installer sources cite up to ~$0.90/W, capped in the low-$5,000s per home). *Verify current amounts and eligibility.*
- **Energy Trust battery storage:** Storage incentives (reported starting around $400/kWh, capped ~$5,000). *Verify.*
- **Energy Trust commercial:** Separate **solar-for-business**, **solar-for-nonprofit**, multifamily, and agricultural tracks. Per-watt amounts are published in ETO's periodic Solar Status Report and move with the quarterly step-downs. *Verify current commercial rates.*
- **ODOE Oregon Solar + Storage Rebate Program (OSSRP):** State rebate paid to approved contractors. **Homeowners: up to $5,000 for solar + up to $2,500 for storage. Low-income service providers (nonprofits, tribes, municipalities): up to $30,000 for solar + $15,000 for storage.** Funding is **small and exhausts quickly** — a June 2026 reopening was funded with only ~$1.1 million and warned of running out in as little as a day. **Confirm the program is open and reserved before relying on it.**

> Energy Trust and ODOE rebates serve **only PGE and Pacific Power** territories; customers of consumer-owned utilities (e.g., EWEB, many PUDs/co-ops) should check their own utility's programs. **Verify.**

## Sales Tax Exemption

**Not applicable — Oregon has no statewide sales tax.** Solar equipment is not subject to state sales tax, so there is no exemption to claim. (A handful of localities levy narrow taxes, but there is no general Oregon sales tax.)

## Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

**Oregon exempts 100% of the added value** of a qualifying alternative-energy system from property tax under **ORS 307.175**. Installing solar (or geothermal, wind, water, fuel-cell, or methane systems) does **not raise your assessed value**, for both residential and commercial end-user systems that are net-metered or primarily offset on-site load.

> The statute carries a scheduled sunset (Public.Law's text references property tax years beginning after **July 1, 2029**); systems installed before the sunset retain the exemption. The Legislature has extended this exemption before — **verify the current sunset date** at ORS 307.175 or DSIRE.

## Permitting & Interconnection Notes

- **Interconnection:** OPUC standards under **OAR Chapter 860, Division 82**. Equipment must meet **UL 1741 / IEEE 1547**. Systems under ~25 kW typically get expedited review; larger systems may require engineering studies.
- **Automated permitting (SolarAPP+):** Oregon encourages, but does not statewide-mandate, local adoption of **SolarAPP+** for instant residential permit approval. Adoption is **jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction** — **verify** whether the specific building department uses it.
- **Consumer-owned utilities** set their own interconnection tariffs.

## Bottom Line

For **Oregon homeowners**, economics rest on **full 1:1 net metering**, **no sales tax**, a **100% property-tax exemption**, and whatever **rebates** are currently funded through Energy Trust and ODOE — plus the reality that §25D federal credit ended after 2025. The ODOE rebate can be meaningful (up to $5,000 solar + $2,500 storage) but frequently runs out of money, so confirm availability first. For **commercial and nonprofit projects**, Energy Trust's solar-for-business/nonprofit incentives plus the federal §48E ITC and depreciation are the core drivers; low-income service providers can access ODOE's larger rebate tier. Watch ETO's **quarterly step-downs** — timing affects the incentive you actually receive.
