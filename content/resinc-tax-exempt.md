# Property & Sales-Tax Exemptions for Homeowners

Two of the most common — and most overlooked — residential solar incentives are **tax exemptions**: a **property-tax exemption** so your solar system doesn't raise your annual property-tax bill, and a **sales-tax exemption** so you don't pay sales tax when you buy the equipment. Neither is a check in your pocket, but together they can save thousands over a system's life. This page explains both, the wide state variation, and roughly how much they save.

> **Exemptions are common but not universal, and their structure varies.** Some states offer a full, permanent exemption; others a partial or time-limited one; some leave it to the county or municipality. Always confirm availability for **your state and locality** on [DSIRE](https://www.dsireusa.org/).

## Property-Tax Exemptions (Assessment Exclusions)

Adding solar typically **increases a home's value**. Without an exemption, a higher assessed value would mean a **higher annual property-tax bill**. A **solar property-tax exemption** (also called an **assessment exclusion**) prevents that: the added value from the qualifying system is **excluded** from your property-tax assessment.

| Structure | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Full, permanent exemption** | 100% of added value excluded for the life of the system |
| **Partial exemption** | A percentage (or capped dollar amount) of added value excluded |
| **Term-limited exemption** | Exclusion applies for a set number of years, then expires |
| **Local option** | State authorizes it, but each county/municipality decides |

> **What it does NOT do.** A property-tax exemption doesn't lower your existing taxes — it **prevents an increase** caused by the solar system. You still pay property tax on the home's underlying value.

### How much a property-tax exemption saves

```
  System adds ~$15,000 to assessed home value
  Local effective property-tax rate ~1.2%

  Annual tax that would otherwise be added:
      $15,000 x 1.2% = ~$180/yr
  Over 20 years (undiscounted) ......... ~$3,600 avoided
```

The savings scale with your **local tax rate** and the **value added** — in high-tax jurisdictions this can be one of the larger lifetime incentives.

## Sales-Tax Exemptions

A **sales-tax exemption** removes state (and sometimes local) **sales tax** from the purchase of solar equipment, lowering your **upfront** cost. It applies at the point of purchase/installation and requires no filing by the homeowner in most cases — the installer handles it.

| Structure | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Full exemption** | No state sales tax on qualifying solar equipment |
| **Equipment-only** | Panels/inverters exempt; labor may be taxed differently |
| **Local variation** | State exempts its portion; local sales tax may still apply |

### How much a sales-tax exemption saves

```
  Equipment portion of a system ~$18,000
  State sales-tax rate ~6.5%

  Sales tax avoided:
      $18,000 x 6.5% = ~$1,170 upfront
```

In states with no sales tax at all (e.g., Oregon, Montana, New Hampshire, Delaware), this "exemption" is moot — there's no sales tax to exempt.

## Wide State Variation — Examples

The details differ dramatically. A few illustrative patterns (**verify current specifics on [DSIRE](https://www.dsireusa.org/)**):

| Pattern | Illustrative examples (verify) |
|---|---|
| **Both** property- and sales-tax exemptions | Several states pair a property-tax exclusion with a sales-tax exemption (e.g., New York, New Jersey, Florida-style approaches) |
| **Property-tax exemption only** | Many states exclude added solar value but still charge sales tax |
| **Sales-tax exemption only** | Some states waive sales tax but reassess property value |
| **Local option** | The state enables the exemption but each county opts in |
| **Neither at state level** | Some states leave it entirely to net metering / other incentives |

> Because these are governed by **state statute and local assessors**, two neighboring counties can treat the same system differently. Confirm with your **county assessor** (property tax) and your **installer / state revenue department** (sales tax).

## How These Stack With Other Incentives

Tax exemptions are **independent** of tax credits and bill savings — they stack cleanly:

- They do **not** reduce your federal-credit basis the way a **cash rebate** does. (An exemption lowers what you're charged; it isn't a payment to you.) See [Residential Federal Solar Tax Credit (§25D)](resinc-federal.md) — noting §25D ended for homeowner-owned systems placed in service after Dec 31, 2025.
- They combine with [State Residential Tax Credits & Rebates](resinc-state.md), [SRECs](resinc-srec.md), [utility rebates](resinc-utility.md), and your [net-metering / net-billing](resinc-net-metering.md) savings.

```
  Example lifetime value of exemptions alone:
    Sales-tax exemption (upfront) ......... ~$1,170
    Property-tax exemption (20 yr) ........ ~$3,600
    Combined ~$4,770 in avoided taxes,
    entirely separate from credits and bill savings.
```

## How to Confirm Your Exemptions

1. **Look up your state on [DSIRE](https://www.dsireusa.org/)** and filter for "Property Tax Incentive" and "Sales Tax Incentive."
2. **Call your county assessor** to confirm the property-tax exclusion applies and whether you must **file** anything (some require an application).
3. **Ask your installer** whether they apply the sales-tax exemption at purchase and what documentation is needed.
4. **Keep records** — assessment exclusions sometimes must be reaffirmed or can lapse if the property changes hands.
