# Montana Solar Incentives & Policy

Montana offers **full-retail net metering**, **no statewide sales tax at all**, and a **property-tax exemption** for renewable-energy systems. Its once-notable **35% commercial renewable-energy investment tax credit was repealed** and is no longer available for new projects — a key correction for anyone relying on older guides. This guide covers both **residential** and **commercial** solar where the rules diverge.

> **Educational use only.** Program terms, rates, and statutes change frequently. Verify every figure below against official Montana sources and [DSIRE](https://www.dsireusa.org/) before you rely on it. Today's date for this guide is **2026-06-30**.

| **SRECs / RECs** | No retail SREC market | No retail SREC market | Montana has an RPS but no homeowner SREC market |

## Overview

Montana's solar economics rest on:

- **Full-retail net metering** — a genuinely strong compensation mechanism.
- The absence of any **state sales tax**, so equipment purchases avoid sales tax entirely (no exemption needed).
- A **property-tax exemption** for renewable-energy systems (subject to value caps and a term).
- **Federal commercial incentives** for businesses (residential §25D having ended for 2026 buyers).

Regulation runs through the **Montana Public Service Commission (PSC)** and the **Montana Department of Environmental Quality (DEQ)**. **NorthWestern Energy** is the dominant regulated utility; **Montana-Dakota Utilities** and rural cooperatives serve other areas.

## Net Metering / Net Billing

Montana provides **full-retail net metering** for customer-generators — every exported kWh offsets a consumed kWh at the **full retail rate.** See [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md) for the mechanics.

- **Reported retail value (verify):** around **$0.13/kWh** in recent materials — moves with rate cases; **confirm your utility's current retail rate.**
- **System-size caps (verify):** **NorthWestern Energy** has limited net-metered systems to about **50 kW**, and Montana **does not allow aggregate or virtual net metering** — each meter/account stands alone. Cooperatives set their own terms.
- **Rollover / true-up:** Excess credits carry forward; confirm the utility's true-up handling.

> **Commercial note:** The ~50 kW cap (at NorthWestern) and the lack of virtual/aggregate net metering limit how large a behind-the-meter commercial system can be while staying in the net-metering program. Larger commercial projects need a different offtake/interconnection structure — confirm with the utility and PSC.

## SREC / RECs

Montana has a **Renewable Portfolio Standard**, so systems generate **RECs**, but there is **no active retail SREC cash market** for homeowners. REC ownership in commercial deals is negotiated. See [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md) for how such markets work elsewhere.

## State Tax Credits

> **Important correction — the 35% commercial credit is gone.**
> Montana's **Alternative Energy Investment Tax Credit** — historically **35% of eligible costs, up to a large cap (commonly cited near $5 million)** for commercial/net-metering renewable investments of **$5,000+** — was **repealed by the 2021 Legislature.** It **cannot be claimed for new investments after Tax Year 2021.** Taxpayers with pre-repeal credits may still use remaining **carryforward** (historically up to 7 years). **Do not model this credit for a new 2026 project.** Verify at the Montana Department of Revenue / DSIRE.

- **Residential:** Montana historically offered a **small residential alternative-energy income-tax credit** (a few hundred dollars). **Verify current availability and amount** with the Montana Department of Revenue, as several energy credits have been changed or repealed.

## Federal Tax Credits

- **Residential (§25D):** **Ended for systems placed in service after December 31, 2025** under the **One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA)**. **2026 residential buyers receive no federal §25D credit.**
- **Commercial (ITC §48 / tech-neutral §48E):** Businesses may still claim the **commercial ITC** on qualifying projects, subject to current begin-construction / placed-in-service and phase-out rules, plus possible bonus adders and depreciation. With the state 35% credit gone, **federal commercial incentives now carry the commercial case.**

See [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md).

## Utility Incentives / Rebates

- No standing statewide upfront PV rebate.
- **DEQ Alternative Energy Revolving Loan Program (AERLP):** low-interest loans for renewable-energy and efficiency projects — **verify current rates, caps, and eligibility** with Montana DEQ.
- **NorthWestern Energy** and co-ops may run efficiency/demand programs; check current offerings.

## Sales Tax Exemption

**Montana has no statewide general sales tax**, so there is **no sales tax on solar equipment** and **no exemption is needed.** (Certain local resort-area taxes exist but generally don't affect PV equipment; verify locally if relevant.)

## Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

Montana **exempts the value of renewable-energy systems from property tax** for a period after installation.

- **Reported terms (verify):** residential exemption up to a **capped added value (commonly cited around $20,000)** for roughly **10 years**; commercial systems have a **separate (higher) cap.** Exact caps and term should be **confirmed with the Montana Department of Revenue**, as figures vary across sources.

## Permitting & Interconnection Notes

- **Permitting:** Local building/electrical permits via city or county; confirm AHJ and adopted electrical code.
- **Interconnection:** Governed by **PSC** rules and the serving utility's procedures. Net-metered systems (within size caps) use the standard interconnection path; larger systems require studies and separate agreements.

## Bottom Line

- **Residential:** Strong **full-retail net metering**, **no sales tax**, and a **property-tax exemption** make Montana attractive on the state side — but **§25D is gone for 2026 buyers** and the residential state credit is small (verify). Watch the **~50 kW cap** and the **no-virtual-net-metering** rule.
- **Commercial:** The **35% state investment credit is repealed** — the commercial case now rests on the **federal ITC/§48E + depreciation**, plus the property-tax exemption. Size systems around the utility's net-metering cap or use an alternative offtake structure.
