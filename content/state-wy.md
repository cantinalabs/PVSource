# Wyoming Solar Incentives & Policy

Wyoming is a **minimal-incentive** state: it mandates **net metering for systems up to 25 kW**, has **no state income tax** (so income-tax credits/deductions are irrelevant), and offers few dedicated solar programs. Sales-tax and property-tax treatment are **partly unsettled and should be verified**. This guide covers both **residential** and **commercial** solar where the rules diverge.

> **Educational use only.** Program terms and statutes change frequently. Verify every item below against official Wyoming sources and [DSIRE](https://www.dsireusa.org/) before you rely on it. Today's date for this guide is **2026-06-30**.

## Overview

Wyoming's solar economics rest almost entirely on:

- The **statewide net-metering mandate** (capped at **25 kW**), overseen by the **Wyoming Public Service Commission (PSC)**.
- The **federal commercial ITC** for businesses (residential §25D having ended for 2026 buyers).

Wyoming has **no state income tax**, **no RPS**, **no state solar rebate**, and **no retail SREC market**. Its sales-tax and property-tax exemption status is the main area needing verification. The dominant regulated utility is **Rocky Mountain Power (PacifiCorp)**; municipal utilities and cooperatives also serve parts of the state.

## Net Metering / Net Billing

Wyoming law **requires utilities to offer net metering for customer-generators with systems up to 25 kW.** See [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md) for how these mechanisms compare.

- **Credit basis:** Monthly **net energy generated (NEG)** appears as a bill line; net excess generation **rolls over month to month.**
- **Annual true-up:** At the end of the billing year, utilities are generally required to purchase remaining excess at an **avoided-cost rate** (well below retail) — **verify current true-up rate.**
- **The 25 kW cap is a real ceiling.** Larger residential or **commercial** systems exceeding 25 kW fall **outside** the mandated net-metering program and must negotiate other arrangements (e.g., separate purchase/interconnection terms) with the utility.

> **Commercial note:** Because the mandate stops at 25 kW, mid-size and large commercial arrays cannot rely on statewide net metering. Confirm the utility's tariff, buyback terms, and interconnection process for systems above the cap.

## SREC / RECs

Wyoming has **no Renewable Portfolio Standard** and **no retail SREC market.** Do not count on certificate revenue. See [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md) for how such markets function in states that have them.

## State Tax Credits

Wyoming has **no state income tax**, so **there are no state solar income-tax credits or deductions** for residential or commercial taxpayers. The relevant tax levers here are **sales tax** and **property tax** (below) and **federal** incentives.

## Federal Tax Credits

- **Residential (§25D):** **Ended for systems placed in service after December 31, 2025** under the **One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA)**. **2026 residential buyers receive no federal §25D credit.**
- **Commercial (ITC §48 / tech-neutral §48E):** Businesses may still claim the **commercial ITC** on qualifying projects, subject to current begin-construction / placed-in-service and phase-out rules, plus possible bonus adders and depreciation.

See [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md).

## Utility Incentives / Rebates

- No standing statewide upfront PV rebate.
- **Rocky Mountain Power** and local co-ops/municipals may run occasional programs (e.g., storage, demand-side, or wattsmart-type offerings) — **check current utility program pages.**

## Sales Tax Exemption

Wyoming's sources indicate a **sales/use-tax exemption for renewable-energy equipment** exists (DSIRE lists a Wyoming renewable sales/use-tax exemption), but the **exact scope and current status for rooftop PV should be verified.**

> **Verify at DSIRE/state site.** Confirm with the **Wyoming Department of Revenue** and DSIRE whether your specific equipment qualifies before assuming an exemption. Do not budget an exemption you have not confirmed.

## Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

Wyoming's property-tax treatment of solar is **contradictory across sources** — some report a residential renewable-energy property-tax exemption (with references to a ~2022 enactment); others state Wyoming lacks a broad solar property-tax exemption (particularly for utility-scale).

> **Verify — this is genuinely unsettled.** Confirm the current statute and any residential vs. utility-scale distinction with the **Wyoming Department of Revenue**, your county assessor, and [DSIRE](https://www.dsireusa.org/) before relying on any property-tax exemption.

## Permitting & Interconnection Notes

- **Permitting:** Local city/county building and electrical permits; confirm AHJ requirements and adopted electrical code.
- **Interconnection:** Governed by **PSC** rules and the serving utility's procedures. Systems **≤ 25 kW** use the net-metering interconnection path; **larger systems** require separate interconnection review and buyback negotiation.

## Bottom Line

- **Residential:** Value is essentially **net metering (≤ 25 kW) + a possible sales-tax exemption**, with **no state income-tax benefit** and **no federal §25D for 2026**. Property-tax exemption status is uncertain — verify. Wyoming's excellent solar resource helps, but the incentive stack is thin.
- **Commercial:** The **federal commercial ITC/§48E + depreciation** is the primary driver; the **25 kW net-metering cap** means larger systems must negotiate buyback/interconnection directly with the utility.
