# Massachusetts — SMART

The **Solar Massachusetts Renewable Target (SMART)** program is the Commonwealth's primary state solar incentive. It pays a **per-kilowatt-hour (`$/kWh`) production incentive** — a declining-block **tariff** funded through utility rates — for every kWh a qualifying **Solar Tariff Generation Unit (STGU)** produces, for a fixed term. SMART runs **on top of** net metering, and commercial projects typically stack it with the [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md) and any [State Tax Credits & Incentives](state-tax-credits.md).

> **Program administrator:** Massachusetts **Department of Energy Resources (DOER)** sets policy; the **SMART Solar Program Administrator** (utility-side, via Eversource/National Grid/Unitil) administers enrollment and payments.
> **Eligible utilities:** **Eversource**, **National Grid**, and **Unitil** — you must be a customer of one of these to participate.
> **Official site:** [SMART Program (Mass.gov / DOER)](https://www.mass.gov/info-details/smart-30-program-details)

---

## How SMART Works

A SMART STGU earns a **compensation rate** (`$/kWh`) on its production for the program term. The compensation is the **Base Compensation Rate** plus any qualifying **Compensation Rate Adders**, with **subtractors/fees** for certain land uses.

> **Key point:** SMART is a **production-based tariff**, not an upfront rebate or a tradable certificate. You are paid per kWh generated, and the rate **locks for the full term** at qualification.

### Term

- **Larger systems (> ~25 kW AC):** **10-year** tariff term (historically), with longer terms cited for certain SMART 3.0 categories — **verify the term for your STGU size/category** in the current DOER guideline.
- **Small systems (≤ 25 kW AC) and Low-Income:** **20-year** term, paid as a flat incentive rate per kWh.

### Compensation rate types

- **Net-metered STGUs** (most behind-the-meter commercial): paid an **incentive on top of net-metering savings** — net metering offsets the bill; SMART pays a separate per-kWh incentive.
- **Stand-alone / qualifying facility (alternative on-bill credit) STGUs:** compensated through a value-of-generation mechanism rather than retail net metering.

## SMART 3.0 — Current Program Structure (2025–2026) — VERIFY

DOER **substantially revised** SMART into **"SMART 3.0,"** which changed the core mechanics:

> **Major change:** SMART 3.0 **replaced the old declining capacity-block ladder (Blocks 1–8)** with a structure where **Base Compensation Rates and Adders are pre-set and reset annually** by Program Year. Capacity is allocated per Program Year by utility rather than stepping through eight sequential blocks. **Confirm the current Program Year rates and capacity at the DOER site before pricing.**

### Capacity allocation by utility

DOER establishes a statewide capacity target each Program Year and splits it across the three utilities. Reported allocations:

| Program Year | Statewide capacity | Allocation (approx.) |
|--------------|--------------------|----------------------|
| **PY2025** | — | Eversource ~49%, National Grid ~45%, Unitil ~6% |
| **PY2026** | ~600 MW | National Grid ~270.9 MW (others per DOER) |

> **VERIFY current capacity/block status:** Per-utility capacity and whether a Program Year is open changes; confirm at [SMART 3.0 Program Details (DOER)](https://www.mass.gov/info-details/smart-30-program-details).

### Base Compensation Rate (PY2026 — verify)

| Category | Reported Base Rate |
|----------|--------------------|
| **Standard** | `$0.03/kWh` |
| **Low-income qualified** | `$0.06/kWh` |

> Base rates are **reset annually** and are bounded by a guardrail: a year-over-year change to any Base Rate or Adder generally cannot exceed **20%** of the prior year's value for that category, **or `$0.01/kWh`, whichever is greater**. Verify the current-year figures.

### Compensation Rate Adders

Adders **increase** the per-kWh rate for projects that deliver targeted policy value. Reported SMART 3.0 adder magnitudes (`$/kWh`, **verify**):

| Adder | Reported value | Purpose |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| **Energy storage** | ~`$0.04/kWh` | Paired battery storage meeting SMART storage specs |
| **Solar canopy** | ~`$0.03/kWh` | Carport/canopy-mounted arrays |
| **Low-income** | ~`$0.03–0.06/kWh` | Low-income facilities/properties & community shared solar serving LMI |
| **Public entity** | per guideline | Municipal/public/government-owned |
| **Community shared solar** | per guideline | Qualifying community shared solar |
| **Location / land-use** | per guideline | Brownfield, landfill, dual-use agriculture, building-mounted |

> Adders overall have been cited in the **~`$0.0025`–`$0.08/kWh`** range across categories. Confirm exact current values and stacking rules in the DOER guideline.

### Subtractors / land-use mitigation

> **Greenfield mitigation:** SMART 3.0 **replaced the old "greenfield subtractor"** with a **project-specific Mitigation Fee** for ground-mounted projects **over 250 kW AC sited on undeveloped land** without a qualifying location adder (e.g., brownfield, landfill, dual-use ag). The fee is calculated per acre based on habitat/farmland/carbon-risk GIS layers, with a portion due at SOQ application and the balance at Final SOQ (refundable if the project is cancelled or the site reclaimed). Verify the current methodology.

## Interaction with Net Metering

> **SMART and net metering run together, not as alternatives.** A behind-the-meter commercial STGU offsets its own consumption and exports via **net metering** (bill credits), **and separately** earns the **SMART per-kWh incentive** on its total production. Model both revenue streams.

- **Net-metering caps and class** still matter — Massachusetts net-metering eligibility, caps, and credit values depend on system size, facility class, and utility. Larger commercial/standalone projects may be on different net-metering or alternative-credit terms.
- Confirm the project's net-metering classification with the utility, because it affects the **compensation rate type** assigned under SMART.

## Legacy Context: SREC-I / SREC-II

Before SMART, Massachusetts incentivized solar through **tradable Solar Renewable Energy Certificates (SRECs)** under **SREC-I** and then **SREC-II**, where systems earned certificates (1 SREC per MWh) sold into a market with a floor/ceiling.

> **SMART replaced the SREC programs for new projects.** SREC-I/II are **closed to new entrants**; existing SREC-qualified systems continue earning certificates for their remaining qualification term. New commercial projects use **SMART 3.0**, which fixes the per-kWh value at qualification instead of exposing it to a fluctuating certificate market.

## How a Commercial Project Monetizes SMART

```
Behind-the-meter savings:  Net metering offsets the utility bill
SMART incentive:           Base Rate + Adders ($/kWh) on ALL production, for the term
Federal:                   ITC + bonus depreciation  →  see federal-tax-credits.md
```

1. **Qualify the STGU** (Statement of Qualification / SOQ) under the current Program Year and utility allocation.
2. **Lock the rate** — Base Rate + applicable Adders − any Mitigation Fee — fixed for the term.
3. **Stack net metering** for self-consumption/export savings.
4. **Layer federal ITC and depreciation** on the capital side.

## Commercial Cheat Sheet — Massachusetts

| Decision point | Guidance |
|----------------|----------|
| **Utility** | Must be Eversource, National Grid, or Unitil; capacity is allocated per utility per Program Year. |
| **Structure** | SMART 3.0 uses **pre-set annual** Base Rates/Adders (no Blocks 1–8 ladder). Verify current PY rates. |
| **Adders** | Storage (~`$0.04`), canopy (~`$0.03`), low-income, public entity, community shared, location/land-use. |
| **Land use** | Ground-mount > 250 kW AC on undeveloped land → **Mitigation Fee**; favor roofs, canopies, brownfields, dual-use. |
| **Net metering** | Runs **alongside** SMART — model both bill credits and the per-kWh incentive. |
| **Term** | Confirm 10- vs 20-year term for your size/category; rate locks at qualification. |
| **Stacking** | SMART + net metering + federal ITC/depreciation. |
