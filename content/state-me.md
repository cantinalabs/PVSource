# Maine Solar Incentives & Policy

Maine pairs a favorable **Net Energy Billing (NEB)** framework with a strong **community solar** market and a statewide **property-tax exemption** for renewable-energy equipment. There is **no state solar rebate or state solar income-tax credit** — the value stack is bill credits plus federal commercial incentives. This guide covers both **residential** and **commercial** solar where the rules diverge.

> **Educational use only.** Program terms, rates, and statutes change frequently. Every dollar figure and rate below should be re-verified against the official Maine agency pages and [DSIRE](https://www.dsireusa.org/) before you rely on it. Today's date for this guide is **2026-06-30**.

## Overview

Maine has no state income-tax credit for solar and no statewide upfront PV rebate. Instead, the economics rest on:

- **Net Energy Billing (NEB)** — Maine's version of net metering, run by the **Maine Public Utilities Commission (MPUC)**.
- A robust **community solar** sector that lets renters and rooftop-constrained customers subscribe to off-site arrays.
- A **statewide property-tax exemption** for renewable-energy equipment.
- **Federal** incentives — now **commercial-only** for new projects after the residential §25D credit ended.

The two regulated utilities are **Central Maine Power (CMP)** and **Versant Power**; NEB mechanics and reported credit values differ slightly by territory.

## Net Metering / Net Billing

Maine uses **Net Energy Billing** rather than classic "net metering." See the sitewide primer on [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md) for how these mechanisms differ.

There are two NEB tracks:

- **kWh-basis NEB** — the customer banks **kilowatt-hour credits** valued at (approximately) the retail volumetric rate; typical for **residential** rooftop and small systems.
- **Tariff-rate / monetary NEB** — a **$/kWh** credit set by the MPUC; increasingly relevant for **community solar** and larger **commercial** projects.

> **Reported credit values (verify):** Rooftop NEB has been described as approximately retail-value, with per-kWh figures cited around **$0.27/kWh (CMP)** and **$0.32/kWh (Versant)** in 2026 marketing material. These track retail rates and change with rate cases — **confirm the current effective rate at the MPUC and on your utility's tariff.**

Key mechanics to verify for your project:

- **Credit rollover** — unused credits generally carry forward on a rolling ~12-month basis, then expire.
- **System-size / account caps** — NEB eligibility and the number of accounts a project may serve are set by MPUC rule.
- **LD 1777 (effective Jan 1, 2024)** changed compensation for **new community solar** projects to a **PUC tariff rate (below retail)**; reporting indicates the **rooftop residential NEB program was not cut** by this change. **Verify current NEB rules** at the MPUC before pricing any project.

> **Commercial note:** Larger commercial arrays more often use **tariff-rate NEB** and may negotiate offtake as community-solar hosts. Model the tariff value, not the retail rate.

## SREC / RECs

Maine has a **Renewable Portfolio Standard (RPS)**, so solar systems generate **Renewable Energy Certificates (RECs)**. However:

- There is **no active retail SREC cash market** for homeowners the way New Jersey or Massachusetts once ran. RECs are typically retained by the program or the offtaker.
- For **commercial** developers, REC ownership and any monetization are deal-specific — confirm who holds the RECs in your NEB/community-solar agreement.

For how tradable-certificate markets work in states that have them, see [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md).

## State Tax Credits

**Maine offers no state income-tax credit for solar** for either residential or commercial installations. Do not assume a state credit exists — it does not. Federal credits are covered below.

## Federal Tax Credits

- **Residential (§25D):** The **25% → 30% residential clean-energy credit ended for systems placed in service after December 31, 2025** under the **One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA)**. **Homeowners buying solar in 2026 receive no federal §25D credit.**
- **Commercial (ITC §48 / tech-neutral §48E):** Businesses may still claim the **commercial Investment Tax Credit** for qualifying projects, subject to the current placed-in-service / begin-construction timelines and phase-out rules. Bonus adders (domestic content, energy community) and depreciation may stack.

See [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md) for eligibility, timing, and stacking details. Depreciation is covered separately at the site level.

## Utility Incentives / Rebates

- **Efficiency Maine** — Maine's efficiency administrator — **does not offer a PV rebate.** Its incentives target **heat pumps, weatherization, heat-pump water heaters, and EVs.** (Verify current program list at Efficiency Maine.)
- **CMP / Versant** — no standing statewide upfront PV rebate; value comes through **NEB**. Check each utility's current tariffs and any storage or demand programs.

> **Bottom line on rebates:** Budget for **no upfront state or utility PV rebate** in Maine. If a program appears, verify it directly.

## Sales Tax Exemption

Maine's general sales-tax rate is **5.5%**. The **sales-tax treatment of PV equipment is unsettled in current sources** — some list a solar exemption, others state that the 5.5% tax applies.

> **Verify at MRS/official site.** Confirm the current sales-tax treatment of solar equipment with **Maine Revenue Services** (and your installer) before assuming an exemption. Do not budget an exemption you have not confirmed.

## Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

Maine provides a **statewide property-tax exemption for renewable-energy equipment**, so the added assessed value of a qualifying solar system is generally not taxed. This is a **statewide** treatment (not merely a local option), but it is typically **application-based**.

- **Residential & commercial:** Both may qualify; file the required exemption application with your municipal assessor per **Maine Revenue Services** guidance (see Property Tax Bulletin No. 29).
- **Verify:** Exact eligible equipment, deadlines, and forms — confirm current bulletin at Maine Revenue Services.

## Permitting & Interconnection Notes

- **Permitting:** Handled at the **municipal** level (building/electrical). Maine has adopted the statewide **Maine Uniform Building and Energy Code (MUBEC)**; local jurisdictions administer inspections.
- **Interconnection:** Governed by **MPUC interconnection rules**; CMP and Versant each publish application processes and technical requirements. Small residential systems use expedited/simplified review; larger commercial systems may trigger studies.
- **Community solar** projects have their own MPUC program requirements and queue considerations.

## Bottom Line

- **Residential:** No state credit, no state PV rebate, likely **no confirmed sales-tax exemption** — value is **NEB bill credits + statewide property-tax exemption**, and (for 2026 buyers) **no federal §25D**. Community solar subscription is a strong option for those who can't install.
- **Commercial:** Same NEB/property-tax framework, plus access to the **federal commercial ITC/§48E** and depreciation, and opportunities as a **community-solar host**. Model against the **tariff-rate NEB**, not retail.
