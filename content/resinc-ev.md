# EV Charging + Solar Incentives at Home

Pairing a home EV charger with solar and storage is one of the highest-value combinations in residential energy: you generate power, store it, and use it to drive. The incentives, though, come from different places and are on different clocks. This article covers the federal EV-charger credit's status, utility EVSE rebates and managed-charging programs, and how solar + storage + EV stack.

> **Federal EV-charger credit status (verify — dated 2026):** The federal **Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Credit (§30C)** — the home **EV charger** credit — was cut short by the OBBBA. Its termination date was moved from December 31, 2032 to **June 30, 2026.** For a charger at your **main home**, the credit is generally **30% of cost, up to $1,000**, and — critically — the property must be in an **eligible (low-income or non-urban) census tract** to qualify. Because the placed-in-service window closes **June 30, 2026**, and today is late June 2026, treat this credit as **expiring immediately** and confirm current status with the IRS before relying on it.

Note this is a **separate** credit from the home solar credit (§25D), which already ended after December 31, 2025 — see [Residential Solar Credit: Timeline & History](resinc-timeline.md).

## The federal §30C charger credit — the details that trip people up

| Feature | Home (individual) rule |
|---|---|
| Credit amount | **30% of cost, up to $1,000 per item** (charger + qualifying installation) |
| Covered costs | Charging equipment and installation labor/electrical work |
| **Location requirement** | Property must be in an **eligible census tract** (low-income community or non-urban area) — this excludes many suburban addresses |
| Placed-in-service deadline | **June 30, 2026** (moved up by OBBBA from 2032) |
| How to claim | IRS **Form 8911** |

> **Two reasons a homeowner may not get this credit even before it expires:** (1) the **census-tract** requirement — many addresses are ineligible; and (2) the **June 30, 2026** deadline. Check your address against the IRS's eligible-tract guidance and confirm the current expiration before assuming you qualify. See the IRS [Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Credit for Individuals](https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/alternative-fuel-vehicle-refueling-property-credit-for-individuals).

## Utility EVSE rebates and managed-charging programs

With the federal charger credit expiring, **utility programs are the more durable incentive** for most homeowners. These fall into a few types:

- **Upfront EVSE rebates** — a flat rebate toward a qualifying Level 2 charger (often requiring a Wi-Fi-connected/networked charger).
- **Managed charging / demand response** — you let the utility shift or pause charging during peak periods in exchange for enrollment payments or bill credits.
- **EV time-of-use (TOU) rates** — a special rate plan with very low overnight prices for EV charging; the "incentive" is the cheaper electricity rather than a check.
- **Panel/wiring (make-ready) rebates** — help with the electrical upgrade needed to add a charger.

| Program type | What it does | Typical structure (verify locally) |
|---|---|---|
| EVSE rebate | Cuts charger purchase cost | Flat rebate toward a qualifying networked Level 2 charger |
| Managed charging / DR | Pays you to charge off-peak or pause on demand | Enrollment bonus + recurring bill credits |
| EV TOU rate | Cheap overnight charging | Discounted per-kWh price in defined night hours |
| Make-ready rebate | Offsets electrical upgrade | Rebate toward panel/circuit work |

> **These vary enormously by utility.** A charger rebate that exists in one service territory may not exist next door. Look up your **specific utility** and confirm the charger model qualifies. General roundups: ENERGY STAR and the DOE Alternative Fuels Data Center (below).

Representative examples of utilities that have run residential EV programs include **PG&E** and **Southern California Edison** (California), among many others; check each program's current status and terms directly.

## How solar + storage + EV stack

The three technologies reinforce each other:

1. **Solar** generates daytime power. On a TOU plan, midday solar can offset expensive grid energy.
2. **Storage** shifts solar energy into the evening/overnight window and can hold cheap off-peak grid power — useful for charging the car and for backup. See [Residential Battery & Whole-Home Backup](residential-battery-backup.md) and [Residential Storage Incentives](resinc-storage.md).
3. **EV + managed charging / TOU** lets you charge when power is cheapest (or self-generated), turning the car into the largest flexible load in the home.

Practical stacking notes:

- **TOU alignment matters most.** The biggest EV savings usually come from **charging during the cheapest hours**, whether that's midday solar or an overnight EV rate — see [Utility Residential Rebates & Programs](resinc-utility.md).
- **Storage can bridge solar to nighttime charging**, but batteries are often more valuable for backup and rate arbitrage than purely for charging a car; size deliberately.
- **VPP programs and EV managed-charging** may **overlap or conflict** — enrolling a battery in a VPP and a car in managed charging can be great, but confirm the utility allows both together.
- **Financing** for the charger and electrical work can often be folded into a broader solar/storage loan — see [Homeowner Financing 101](residential-financing.md).

## A sensible sequence for a homeowner

1. Confirm whether your address qualifies for the expiring **§30C** credit and whether your install can be completed **before June 30, 2026**; if not, don't count on it.
2. Look up **your utility's** EVSE rebate, managed-charging program, and **EV TOU rate**.
3. Decide charging strategy first (overnight TOU vs. daytime solar), then size any battery.
4. Choose a **networked Level 2 charger** if utility programs require one.
5. Coordinate any panel upgrade with make-ready rebates.
6. Check whether battery **VPP** enrollment ([Residential Storage Incentives](resinc-storage.md)) and EV managed charging can coexist.
