# Low-Income & Equity Solar Programs

Solar's biggest barrier for many households is the **upfront cost** — and with the [federal §25D credit ended after 2025](resinc-federal.md), that barrier grew. A set of **low- and moderate-income (LMI) and equity programs** aims to close the gap with enhanced rebates, no-cost installations, and community-solar subscriptions for income-qualified households. This page covers the major federal and state programs, eligibility basics, and how to access them.

> **Availability is highly local and changing fast.** LMI program funding, eligibility thresholds, and even program existence vary by state, utility, and year — and one major federal program (Solar for All) is in legal flux (below). Confirm current offerings with your **state energy office**, your **utility**, and [DSIRE](https://www.dsireusa.org/).

## Federal: Solar for All (GGRF) — Status in Flux

**Solar for All** was a **$7 billion** federal program funded through the **Greenhouse Gas Reduction Fund (GGRF)** and administered by the **EPA**, awarding grants to states, tribes, and nonprofits to bring residential solar to **low-income and disadvantaged communities**.

> **Critical status (verified June 2026):** On **August 7, 2025**, the EPA announced it would **no longer implement** the Solar for All program, and subsequent legislation **repealed EPA's authority** to administer it and **rescinded remaining funds**. **Multiple lawsuits** challenging the termination were filed in late 2025 and were still working through the courts as of mid-2026. The practical takeaway: **do not assume Solar for All funding is available** — check the current status of your **state's specific award** with your state energy office before relying on it. Source: [EPA — Greenhouse Gas Reduction Fund](https://www.epa.gov/aboutepa/greenhouse-gas-reduction-fund).

Because many awards flowed to **states**, some state-run LMI programs seeded by Solar for All may continue under state funding or pending litigation. The situation is fluid — verify locally.

## California: DAC-SASH and SASH

California operates two long-running LMI residential solar programs, administered in part by **GRID Alternatives**:

| Program | Who it serves | Key facts (verified 2026) |
|---|---|---|
| **SASH** (Single-family Affordable Solar Homes) | Income-qualified single-family homeowners | Long-standing program; model for DAC-SASH |
| **DAC-SASH** (Disadvantaged Communities – SASH) | Homeowners in **disadvantaged communities** meeting income limits | ~**$8.5 million/yr** in incentives; runs **through 2030**; can provide **no-cost or low-cost** systems |

California is also implementing a state **Solar for All** grant (~**$250 million** from EPA, performance period through **April 30, 2029**) via the CPUC — again, subject to the federal funding uncertainty above. See [CPUC — State of California Solar for All](https://www.cpuc.ca.gov/industries-and-topics/electrical-energy/demand-side-management/customer-generation/state-of-california-solar-for-all-program).

## Illinois Solar for All

**Illinois Solar for All (ILSFA)** is a **state-funded** program (distinct from the federal Solar for All) that provides **no-upfront-cost** solar to income-eligible households and organizations, with strong **consumer protections** and a requirement that participants see **guaranteed savings**. It operates alongside the state's [Illinois Shines / Adjustable Block Program](incentive-srec-markets.md).

- Sub-programs cover **residential rooftop**, **community solar**, and **nonprofit/public** facilities.
- Households generally qualify at or below **80% of area or state median income**.
- Access is through **approved vendors** listed by the program.

## GRID Alternatives

**GRID Alternatives** is a national nonprofit that **installs solar for income-qualified households**, often at **no cost**, and administers several of the programs above (DAC-SASH, and historically Solar for All-related work). It combines LMI installations with **job training**. Households typically apply through GRID's regional offices or partner programs. See [GRID Alternatives](https://gridalternatives.org/).

## State LMI Carve-Outs and Enhanced Incentives

Beyond named programs, many state incentive frameworks include **LMI carve-outs** — a reserved budget or **higher incentive rate** for income-qualified participants:

- **Higher $/watt or $/kWh** incentives for LMI customers within a state's main program (e.g., adders in Illinois Shines, Massachusetts SMART LMI adders, New York's affordable-solar tracks).
- **Reserved capacity blocks** so LMI projects aren't crowded out by market-rate demand.
- **Bill-credit guarantees** ensuring participants actually save money.

Verify what your state offers on [DSIRE](https://www.dsireusa.org/) and through program-specific pages like [MA SMART](incentive-ma-smart.md) and [NY-Sun & VDER](incentive-ny-sun-vder.md).

## Community Solar LMI Subscriptions

For renters, condo dwellers, and homes with unsuitable roofs, **community solar** offers LMI access **without rooftop ownership**: you subscribe to a share of an off-site array and receive **bill credits**, often with a **guaranteed discount** for income-qualified subscribers and **no upfront cost or credit check**.

- Many states **mandate an LMI subscriber share** in community-solar programs.
- LMI subscribers frequently get a **larger guaranteed discount** (e.g., 10–20% off the credited value).
- No equipment, no roof work, and typically **cancel-anytime** terms.

See [Community Solar for Households](residential-community-solar.md) for how subscriptions work and what to check before signing.

## Eligibility and How to Access — Quick Guide

| Step | What to do |
|---|---|
| **1. Check income eligibility** | Most programs use **% of AMI or SMI** (commonly ≤80%) or participation in another assistance program (LIHEAP, SNAP, etc.) |
| **2. Identify programs in your state** | Use [DSIRE](https://www.dsireusa.org/), your **state energy office**, and your **utility** |
| **3. Confirm current funding** | Ask whether the program is **open** (funding can pause mid-year; Solar for All is in flux) |
| **4. Use approved vendors** | LMI programs require **program-approved installers/subscription providers** |
| **5. Verify the savings guarantee** | Legitimate LMI programs guarantee net savings — be wary of anything that doesn't |

> **Consumer-protection note.** Income-qualified households are frequent targets of predatory solar sales. Work only through **program-listed vendors**, insist on written **savings guarantees**, and verify the program directly with the administrator before signing anything.
