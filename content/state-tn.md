# Tennessee Solar Incentives & Policy

Tennessee is unusual: nearly the entire state is served by the **Tennessee Valley Authority (TVA)** and its ~150+ **local power companies (LPCs)**, and there is **no statewide net-metering mandate**. Instead, solar export compensation runs through **TVA programs** at **avoided-cost (wholesale) rates** that are far below retail. Tennessee has **no state personal income tax** on wages, a **green-energy property-tax assessment cap** for certified facilities, and a **sales-tax exemption pathway** aimed at certified green-energy production facilities rather than at typical home systems.

This guide covers both **residential** and **commercial** solar. For core concepts, see [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md) and [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md).

Commercial projects can access a **green-energy property-tax assessment cap** and a **sales-tax exemption** by becoming **certified green-energy production facilities**.

## Overview

The **Tennessee Valley Authority (TVA)**, a federal utility, is the wholesale power provider for essentially all of Tennessee. Power is delivered to end customers by **more than 150 local power companies (LPCs)** — for example, KUB (Knoxville), MLGW (Memphis), Nashville Electric Service, and BrightRidge (Johnson City). Because TVA is federal and LPCs are local, **there is no state-level net-metering mandate** and each LPC can set its own interconnection and program details within TVA's framework.

Tennessee has **no enforceable Renewable Portfolio Standard**, hence **no SREC compliance market**. Tennessee also levies **no personal income tax on wages** (the former Hall tax on investment income was fully phased out), so state income-tax credits are not a relevant lever for individuals.

## Net Metering / Net Billing

**Tennessee does not offer statewide net metering, and it has no standardized statewide interconnection rules.** Compensation for exported solar energy flows through **TVA programs** administered with the local power company:

- **TVA Green Connect** — TVA's program framework for connecting **behind-the-meter** distributed generation (such as rooftop solar). Program terms, eligibility, and any export compensation are set by TVA and implemented through the LPC.
- **Dispersed Power Production (DPP)** — TVA's long-standing program that pays its **avoided-cost rate** for delivered solar energy. This rate fluctuates monthly and is **generally only a few cents per kWh**, i.e., **well below the retail rate** customers pay for grid electricity.

> Because export compensation is near **wholesale avoided cost** (often ~2–3¢/kWh vs. ~12¢/kWh retail — *verify current values with TVA/your LPC*), the winning residential strategy is to **size for self-consumption** and offset your own usage rather than to export.

**Residential vs. commercial:**
- **Residential:** No retail net metering. Value comes from **direct offset** of on-site consumption. Batteries and load-shifting improve economics because they raise self-consumption.
- **Commercial:** Larger customers may pursue TVA programs and, for utility-scale or dedicated generation, TVA procurement/PPA structures. Because there is no retail netting, commercial project modeling must reflect **actual coincident on-site load, demand charges, and any negotiated TVA/LPC terms.**

Always confirm the **specific LPC's** current rules — see also [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md).

## SREC / RECs

Tennessee has **no RPS and no SREC compliance market**. RECs generated by Tennessee solar are **voluntary-market** instruments only. Note that under some TVA program structures, **TVA may retain the RECs/environmental attributes** associated with generation it compensates — read program terms carefully before assuming you own or can sell the RECs.

## State Tax Credits

Tennessee has **no state personal income tax on wages** and **no residential solar income-tax credit** — there is no state income-tax mechanism through which individuals would claim a solar credit. For businesses, the relevant state-level benefits are the **property-tax assessment** and **sales-tax** provisions below rather than an income-tax credit.

## Federal Tax Credits

- **Residential (§25D):** The residential clean-energy credit **ended after December 31, 2025** under the **One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA)**. Home systems placed in service on or after **January 1, 2026** do **not** qualify for the 30% residential credit.
- **Commercial (§48 / §48E):** Businesses may still claim the **commercial Investment Tax Credit** under the technology-neutral **§48E** framework, subject to prevailing-wage/apprenticeship requirements, bonus adders (domestic content, energy community), and OBBBA timing/phase-down rules.

See [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md) for current commercial ITC rules and deadlines. Confirm all dates against the IRS.

## Utility Incentives / Rebates

There is **no broad statewide solar rebate** in Tennessee. TVA's earlier residential incentive program (Green Power Providers) **closed to new applicants at the end of 2019**. Any current incentives are:

- **LPC-specific:** A minority of local power companies run their own efficiency or DER programs. Availability varies widely.
- **TVA program-driven:** Current offerings are structured through TVA's distributed-generation and procurement programs rather than upfront rebates.

> **Verify current TVA and LPC program availability** directly — offerings change and are not uniform across the 150+ local power companies.

## Sales Tax Exemption

Tennessee's sales-tax picture for solar is **nuanced and source-dependent — verify before relying on it**:

- **Certified Green Energy Production Facilities:** Tennessee provides a **sales-and-use-tax exemption/credit pathway** for machinery and equipment used to **produce electricity in a Certified Green Energy Production Facility** (certified by the Tennessee Department of Environment and Conservation, TDEC). Qualifying projects may **take a credit, request a refund, or make tax-exempt purchases.** This is oriented toward **commercial/utility production facilities**, not ordinary rooftop residential systems.
- **Typical residential systems:** Generally **do not** benefit from a blanket solar sales-tax exemption; standard sales tax may apply. *Verify current treatment at the Tennessee Department of Revenue and DSIRE.*

## Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

Tennessee's key property-tax benefit is the **Green Energy Property Tax Assessment** (certified green-energy facilities):

- Certified solar facilities are assessed at **12.5% of installed cost** for property-tax purposes — sharply limiting the taxable value added by the system.
- This assessment cap is most relevant to **commercial/production** facilities that pursue certification. Residential treatment and any local variation should be confirmed. *Verify current percentage and eligibility with TDEC / the Tennessee Comptroller and DSIRE.*

## Permitting & Interconnection Notes

- **No statewide interconnection standard** — each **local power company** sets its own interconnection application, fees, and technical requirements within TVA's framework. Obtain the LPC's DER interconnection packet early.
- **TVA program enrollment** (Green Connect / DPP) is coordinated through the LPC; confirm current program terms, capacity caps, and REC ownership up front.
- **Permitting** is handled at the **local (city/county) level** and varies. Coordinate electrical and structural requirements with the authority having jurisdiction (AHJ).
- Commercial projects seeking the **green-energy property-tax assessment or sales-tax exemption** must complete **TDEC certification** — build that lead time into the schedule.

## Bottom Line

- **Residential:** No retail net metering and no §25D after 2025 make Tennessee a **self-consumption** state. Size systems to offset on-site load, consider storage to raise self-use, and don't count on meaningful export revenue at avoided-cost rates. There is no state income-tax credit (and no wage income tax to credit against).
- **Commercial:** The **federal §48E ITC** is the main driver. Pursuing **Certified Green Energy Production Facility** status can unlock the **sales-tax exemption** and the **12.5% property-tax assessment cap**. Model export compensation conservatively at TVA avoided cost and verify REC ownership under your TVA/LPC program.
