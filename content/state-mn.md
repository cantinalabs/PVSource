# Minnesota Solar Incentives & Policy

Minnesota combines one of the country's strongest **1:1 net metering** laws, a mature **community solar garden** program (the first of its kind in the U.S.), a utility production incentive from Xcel Energy, and full **sales- and property-tax exemptions** for solar equipment. There is **no statewide solar income-tax credit**. This guide covers both residential and commercial systems, noting where the rules diverge.

> **Note (as of 2026-06-30):** Program budgets, incentive rates, and rate-structure details change frequently. Dollar figures below should be treated as directional and **verified at [DSIRE](https://www.dsireusa.org/) and the official Minnesota agency links** in Sources before you rely on them.

## Overview

Minnesota's solar policy rests on several statutory pillars: mandatory net metering, a nationally recognized community solar garden program, the Xcel Energy Solar\*Rewards production incentive, and sales/property tax exemptions. The state does **not** operate a compliance SREC market like the Mid-Atlantic states, so most of the value stack comes from **bill offset (net metering)** plus the **federal ITC (commercial)** and, where available, **Solar\*Rewards** and **community solar** credits.

For the cross-cutting mechanics of how bill credits work, see [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md).

## Net Metering / Net Billing

Minnesota law requires investor-owned utilities (such as **Xcel Energy**), and many cooperatives and municipal utilities, to offer **net metering**. Key points:

- Residential and small systems generally receive **1:1 credit at the retail rate** for exported energy.
- The commonly cited statutory threshold for standard net metering is **up to 40 kW**; systems above that size fall under different tariffs and may face negotiated or avoided-cost compensation. **Verify current size thresholds and true-up treatment** at the Minnesota Department of Commerce and DSIRE.
- **Commercial systems** above the small-system threshold are typically compensated under separate interconnection agreements and may not receive full retail 1:1 credit.

1:1 retail net metering is a major economic driver in Minnesota and is one of the more favorable structures nationally. Read more on structural differences in [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md).

## SREC / RECs

Minnesota does **not** run a tradable compliance **SREC** market. Instead:

- **Renewable Energy Credits (RECs)** associated with a system may be **conveyed to the utility** as a condition of participating in incentive programs such as Solar\*Rewards or community solar.
- Owners who retain their RECs may sell them in **voluntary** markets, but pricing is thin and not a reliable revenue stream.

For how compliance SREC markets work elsewhere (Mid-Atlantic, Illinois), see [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md) and [Illinois Shines & Commercial Solar](illinois-shines.md).

## State Tax Credits

Minnesota has **no statewide solar income-tax credit** for either residential or commercial installations. Value comes from net metering, tax exemptions, utility incentives, and (for commercial) the federal ITC.

## Federal Tax Credits

- **Residential (§25D):** The 30% Residential Clean Energy Credit **ended after December 31, 2025** under the **One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA)**. Systems placed in service on or after **January 1, 2026** do **not** qualify for the residential credit.
- **Commercial (ITC / §48E):** Businesses may still claim the **Clean Electricity Investment Credit (§48E)**, subject to statutory **begin-construction** deadlines and phase-out rules. Combined with **MACRS accelerated depreciation**, this remains a substantial value driver for commercial and third-party-owned systems.

See [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md) for eligibility, timelines, and depreciation interaction.

## Utility Incentives / Rebates

- **Xcel Energy Solar\*Rewards** is a **production-based incentive** paid on top of net metering. Recent program design pays a per-kWh incentive over roughly **10 years**, with separate **standard** and **income-qualified** residential tracks that carry their own annual budgets and occasional **waitlists**. **Verify the current $/kWh rate and remaining budget** at Xcel/DSIRE before quoting to a customer.
- Some **commercial and industrial (C&I)** tracks and small-business options exist; availability and terms change annually.
- Other municipal utilities and cooperatives may offer their own rebates — check locally.

## Sales Tax Exemption

Minnesota **exempts solar energy systems from state sales tax** (state rate 6.875%). The exemption broadly covers PV panels, inverters, racking, wiring, and integrated system components. This applies to both **residential and commercial** purchases. Confirm scope (including storage) at the Minnesota Department of Revenue.

## Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

- The **added market value** of a qualifying solar energy system is **excluded from property tax assessment** under Minn. Stat. §272.02 — installing solar does not raise your assessed property value.
- **Commercial/utility-scale caution:** Minnesota imposes a **Solar Energy Production Tax** (approx. **$1.20/MWh**) on systems with AC nameplate capacity **exceeding 1 MW**. Systems **1 MW or less are exempt**. Large commercial and community-solar projects should model this production tax. **Verify the current rate** at the Minnesota Department of Revenue.

## Permitting & Interconnection Notes

- Interconnection with Xcel and other IOUs follows the **Minnesota Distributed Energy Resources (DER) interconnection process**; smaller residential systems use a simplified/fast-track path.
- Local **AHJ permitting** (building/electrical) applies; requirements vary by municipality.
- Community solar garden subscriptions require **no rooftop installation** — subscribers receive bill credits from an off-site array administered through the Minnesota Department of Commerce (for Xcel territory).

## Bottom Line

- **Residential:** Strong 1:1 net metering + sales/property tax exemptions + (where available) Xcel Solar\*Rewards make Minnesota attractive — but the **federal §25D credit is gone after 2025**, changing payback math for 2026 installs.
- **Commercial:** Net metering (size-dependent), the **§48E ITC + MACRS**, and tax exemptions drive economics; watch the **>1 MW production tax** and negotiated export terms.
- **Community solar** remains a leading no-rooftop option for renters, businesses, and shaded properties.
