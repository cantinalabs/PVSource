# New York — NY-Sun & VDER

New York runs solar incentives on **two parallel tracks** that a commercial project must understand together:

- **NY-Sun (NYSERDA's MW Block program)** — a one-time, declining **capacity incentive** paid in `$/W` to buy down installed cost.
- **VDER ("Value of Distributed Energy Resources" / the "Value Stack")** — the ongoing **compensation mechanism** that replaced traditional net metering for most commercial, demand-metered, remote, and community-solar systems.

A commercial developer typically stacks **both**: an upfront NY-Sun incentive *plus* monthly VDER Value Stack bill credits, on top of the [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md) and any [State Tax Credits & Incentives](state-tax-credits.md).

> **Program administrator:** New York State Energy Research and Development Authority (**NYSERDA**) for NY-Sun; compensation tariffs are set by the **NY Public Service Commission (PSC)** and administered by each utility (Con Edison, National Grid, NYSEG, RG&E, O&R, Central Hudson; PSEG Long Island runs a parallel structure).
> **Official sites:** [NY-Sun](https://www.nyserda.ny.gov/All-Programs/NY-Sun) · [Value Stack / VDER](https://www.nyserda.ny.gov/All-Programs/NY-Sun/Contractors/Value-of-Distributed-Energy-Resources)

## Part 1 — NY-Sun MW Block (Capacity Incentive)

### How the MW Block works

NY-Sun divides the state into **three incentive regions**, each with separate "blocks" of megawatt capacity. As each block fills, the per-watt incentive **steps down** to the next (lower) block. Incentives are denominated in `$/W` (typically `$/W-DC` for the residential/small side and tracked per `W-DC` or `W-AC` depending on sector). When a region's standard block is exhausted, incentives for that sector **close** until/unless new capacity is authorized.

The three regions:

| Region | Utilities covered |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Con Edison** | New York City + Westchester |
| **Upstate** | National Grid, NYSEG, RG&E, O&R, Central Hudson |
| **Long Island** | PSEG Long Island / LIPA service territory |

> **Key point:** The MW Block is a **declining incentive**, not a fixed rate. The `$/W` a project locks in depends on which block is open on the date a complete application is accepted. Always confirm the live block before pricing a project.

### Sectors

NY-Sun separates **Residential/Small (≤25 kW)** from **Nonresidential / Commercial / Community Distributed Generation (CDG)**. Commercial projects sit in the **Nonresidential** track and, if structured as community solar, in the **CDG** track.

### Current block status (2025–2026) — VERIFY before pricing

The standard-income MW Block has **largely wound down** as New York approaches its 10 GW distributed-solar goal:

- **Con Edison** — standard-income MW Block reported **closed (mid-2025)**.
- **Upstate** — standard-income MW Block reported **closed (late 2025)**.
- **Long Island** — Megawatt Block reported **expired**.

What generally **remains active** for commercial/community projects (subject to change):

| Incentive | Notes |
|-----------|-------|
| **Low-income (≤80% AMI) base incentive** | Reported around `$0.80/W` (Con Ed & Upstate); reduced on Long Island — verify. |
| **Inclusive Community Solar Adder (ICSA)** | For nonresidential CDG serving LMI subscribers / affordable housing / disadvantaged communities; allocated by dedicated block (e.g., a Con Ed ICSA block). |
| **Large-project / SEEF adders** | Con Ed nonresidential ≥1 MWac reported to carry a small adder (≈`$0.20/W-DC`); a Solar Energy Equity Framework (SEEF) residual block may open after the standard block closes. |

> **VERIFY current block/tariff:** Standard MW Block capacity changes constantly and several sectors are closed. Confirm the live dashboard before committing a project at [NY-Sun Dashboards and Incentives](https://www.nyserda.ny.gov/All-Programs/NY-Sun/Contractors/Dashboards-and-incentives) and the current [NY-Sun Program Manual](https://www.nyserda.ny.gov/-/media/Project/Nyserda/Files/Programs/NY-Sun/Contractor-Resources/program-manual.pdf).

### Eligibility (commercial & community solar)

- **Commercial / nonresidential on-site:** Interconnected to a participating NY utility; installed by a **NY-Sun Participating Contractor**; meets program manual requirements.
- **Community Distributed Generation (CDG):** Off-site arrays serving subscribers (10+ members; with low-income/affordable-housing provisions for ICSA). CDG projects are compensated through VDER (below), not retail net metering.

## Part 2 — VDER and the "Value Stack"

### Why VDER exists

New York is **transitioning away from volumetric (kWh) net metering** toward a value-based tariff. Under **VDER Phase One**, **demand-metered**, **remote net-metered**, and **community net-metered** projects are compensated through the **Value Stack** — a set of components that pay for *when* and *where* energy is exported, credited as **dollars ($) rather than kWh**.

> **Mass-market vs. demand-metered (critical distinction):**
> - **Mass-market** customers (residential and *small* commercial — generally those **not** on a demand meter) historically remained on **traditional/legacy net metering or a transitional successor tariff** while the PSC develops the mass-market successor (Rate Design / "successor tariff" working groups under **VDER Phase Two**).
> - **Demand-metered** customers (most mid-to-large commercial), plus **community solar** and **remote net-metered** projects, are compensated on the **Value Stack** with a **monetary** credit.
>
> Verify which tariff applies to a specific account — the meter type and account class drive it.

### The Value Stack components

| Component | What it pays for | Basis |
|-----------|------------------|-------|
| **Energy Value (LBMP)** | Wholesale energy | Hourly day-ahead **NYISO zonal LBMP** (`$/kWh`, varies by hour & zone) |
| **Capacity Value** | Avoided generation capacity | ICAP-based; varies by election (e.g., hourly vs. monthly peak) |
| **Environmental Value** | Clean attributes | Set to the higher of the Tier 1 REC price or the SCC-based value (`$/kWh`) |
| **Demand Reduction Value (DRV)** | Reducing utility peak demand | Utility-specific `$/kWh` (applied to peak hours) |
| **Locational System Relief Value (LSRV)** | Relief in grid-constrained areas | Utility-/location-specific, often `$/kW-year`; only in qualifying areas |
| **Community Credit** | Adder for qualifying CDG projects | `$/kWh` set by tariff |

> **Illustrative 2025 values (Con Ed Value Stack — VERIFY, they update):** DRV ≈ `$0.85360/kWh` (peak), LSRV ≈ `$140.76/kW-year`, Community Credit = `$0.12000/kWh`. Energy/LBMP and Capacity are market-driven and change hourly/seasonally. Confirm with the current utility VDER credit statement and the NYSERDA Value Stack Calculator.

### Phase One vs. Phase Two

- **Phase One** — established the Value Stack for demand-metered, remote, and community net-metered DERs (the framework in use today).
- **Phase Two** — extends/refines compensation for **mass-market** (residential + small commercial) systems; ongoing PSC working groups on the mass-market **successor tariff** and Value Stack improvements.

## How a Commercial Project Monetizes NY-Sun + VDER

```
Upfront:  NY-Sun MW Block / ICSA / low-income $/W  →  reduces installed cost
Ongoing:  VDER Value Stack $ credits each month   →  offsets the bill (demand-metered)
                                                      or pays community-solar subscribers
Federal:  ITC + bonus depreciation                →  see federal-tax-credits.md
```

1. **On-site demand-metered commercial:** Self-consumed energy avoids retail rates; **net exports** earn **monetary Value Stack credits** that roll forward to offset future bills. Model the credit using the **NYISO zonal LBMP** plus DRV/LSRV/capacity for the project's utility and location.
2. **Community Distributed Generation (CDG):** The host array earns Value Stack credits (often including the **Community Credit**); the project allocates those `$` credits to subscribers' bills, typically at a discount to their retail rate (the developer's margin sits between Value Stack revenue and subscriber discount).
3. **Stacking the capital incentive:** Layer any open **NY-Sun** `$/W` (low-income, ICSA, large-project adder) on top of the recurring VDER revenue.

> **Modeling tip:** Use NYSERDA's [Value Stack Calculator](https://www.nyserda.ny.gov/All-Programs/NY-Sun/Contractors/Value-of-Distributed-Energy-Resources/Value-Stack-Calculator) with the correct utility, zone, and in-service year. Value Stack revenue is **not** a flat `$/kWh` — it is hour- and location-specific, so a Bronx-constrained-feeder project can value very differently from an upstate rural one.

## Commercial Cheat Sheet — New York

| Decision point | Guidance |
|----------------|----------|
| **Capacity incentive** | Check live NY-Sun dashboard; standard blocks largely closed — low-income/ICSA/large-project adders may remain. |
| **Compensation tariff** | Demand-metered commercial & community solar → **VDER Value Stack** (monetary credit), not 1:1 net metering. |
| **Best locations** | Grid-constrained areas with **DRV/LSRV** uplift dramatically improve Value Stack revenue. |
| **Community solar** | CDG + **Community Credit** + potential **ICSA**; revenue allocated to subscribers as bill credits. |
| **Modeling** | Use NYSERDA Value Stack Calculator with correct zone/utility/year — never a flat `$/kWh`. |
| **Stacking** | NY-Sun `$/W` + VDER `$` credits + federal ITC/depreciation. |
