# New Mexico Solar Incentives & Policy

New Mexico pairs abundant sun with an unusually complete incentive stack: a **refundable 10% state income-tax credit** (up to $6,000), **retail-rate net metering**, a **gross-receipts tax deduction** for solar (and paired storage) equipment, and a **property-tax exclusion** for the added value of residential systems. This guide summarizes the current landscape for both residential and commercial solar as of mid-2026. Programs change frequently — confirm live figures with the administering agency and the **Database of State Incentives for Renewables & Efficiency ([DSIRE](https://www.dsireusa.org/))** before modeling a project.

For background on how export compensation works, see [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md). For the federal side, see [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md). For neighboring-state comparisons, see [More State Programs](incentive-more-states.md) and the [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md).

## At-a-Glance Summary

| Policy Lever | Residential | Commercial |
|---|---|---|
| **Net metering** | **Retail-rate** (PNM, EPE, Xcel/SPS) | Same; larger systems on FERC SGIP tiers |
| **SREC / REC market** | None tradable (PNM REC incentive ended 2022) | REPTC per-kWh credit for utility-scale |
| **State income-tax credit** | **10% up to $6,000** (NSMDTC), **refundable** | Same 10%/$6,000; REPTC for large generation |
| **Utility rebate** | PNM: essentially none for rooftop | None confirmed for rooftop |
| **Gross-receipts (sales) tax** | **Deduction** for solar + paired storage equipment | Deduction applies |
| **Property tax** | Added value **excluded** from assessment | See statute |

> **Framing:** New Mexico's state credit is **refundable** (any excess over tax liability is paid out) — a strong feature — but it draws on a **$30 million annual aggregate pool** that can exhaust. Confirm live funding before relying on it.

## Overview

New Mexico's investor-owned utilities are **Public Service Company of New Mexico (PNM)**, **El Paso Electric (EPE)** in the south, and **Xcel Energy / Southwestern Public Service (SPS)** in the east. Tax credits are administered by the **Energy, Minerals and Natural Resources Department (EMNRD)**, Energy Conservation & Management Division; tax matters by the **NM Taxation and Revenue Department (TRD)**; and utility policy by the **NM Public Regulation Commission (PRC)**.

## Net Metering / Net Billing

All three IOUs still offer **net metering** under PRC rule; there is no statewide net-billing conversion as of mid-2026.

- **PNM:** Full **1:1 retail-rate** credits; excess rolls over.
- **El Paso Electric:** Retail-rate credits within the month, but **month-end surplus is credited at a lower avoided-cost/purchased-power rate** — the key non-1:1 nuance.
- **Xcel/SPS:** 1:1 monthly credits; excess valued at the fuel/purchased-power adjustment rate.

> Aggregators mention PNM rate changes in April 2026 that could affect solar value — **verify** current tariff values at each utility and the PRC. See [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md).

## SREC / RECs

**New Mexico has no tradable residential SREC market.** The **PNM REC purchase incentive ended December 31, 2022**, so there is no ongoing per-kWh SREC revenue stream for rooftop owners. Utilities track and retire RECs for RPS compliance under PRC rule. A separate **Renewable Energy Production Tax Credit (REPTC)** is a per-kWh credit aimed at **utility-scale/commercial generation** (historically fully subscribed) — relevant to large projects, not rooftops. Contrast with our [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md).

New Mexico's **Energy Transition Act (2019)** sets the RPS: **50% renewable by 2030 and 100% zero-carbon by 2045** for IOUs (co-ops by 2050). *Verify interim milestones.*

## State Tax Credits

**New Solar Market Development Income Tax Credit (NSMDTC) — active.** Administered by EMNRD:

- **10% of equipment, materials, and labor costs, capped at $6,000** per taxpayer per tax year.
- **Refundable:** any portion exceeding tax liability is **refunded** to the taxpayer.
- **Annual aggregate cap: $30 million per year**, first-come first-served; once exhausted for a year, no further credits issue that year. **Verify live subscription levels** at EMNRD's Solar Market Dashboard.
- **Sunset:** the current statute allows the credit for tax years **prior to January 1, 2032** (i.e., through tax year 2031). **Verify the exact sunset.**
- **Applies to both residential and commercial/agricultural** taxpayers.
- **Process:** install → building-code electrical inspection → apply via EMNRD portal → receive certificate → claim on the NM return (TRD Form TRD-41406).

> **Verify:** 2026 legislation (SB 55) proposed raising the credit to **30%** and the cap to **$15,000**. As of mid-2026 that change was **not confirmed enacted** — do not rely on 30% until verified.

## Federal Tax Credits

Federal incentives are national, not New Mexico-specific, but they drive project economics:

- **Residential (§25D):** The federal Residential Clean Energy Credit **ended after December 31, 2025** under OBBBA. Systems placed in service in 2026 and later generally **cannot claim §25D**. Confirm with a tax professional.
- **Commercial (ITC / §48E):** The technology-neutral **Clean Electricity Investment Credit (§48E)** generally remains available for commercial and utility-scale projects, subject to phase-out schedules, wage/apprenticeship rules, and bonus adders. See [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md).

## Utility Incentives / Rebates

- **PNM** does **not** offer solar rebates/discounts for rooftop systems and does not partner with installers on incentives. The **PNM REC purchase program ended December 31, 2022.** PNM does run a subscription-based **Community Solar** program (separate from rooftop).
- No active PNM residential solar or standalone-storage cash rebate was confirmed in mid-2026. **Verify** any newly launched storage/demand programs at the utility.

## Sales Tax Exemption

New Mexico's sales-tax equivalent is the **Gross Receipts Tax (GRT)**. Solar equipment qualifies for a **GRT deduction**, applied at the point of sale by the installer (no homeowner paperwork).

- As of **July 1, 2024**, the deduction was **expanded to cover energy-storage equipment paired with solar/wind**, in effect **through July 1, 2034**.
- **Verify** the exact statutory citation and whether the deduction covers labor as well as equipment, with the TRD and DSIRE.

## Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

New Mexico **excludes the added value of a residential solar energy system from the property's assessed value**, so installing solar does not raise the property-tax bill (effectively a 100%-of-added-value exclusion). **Verify** the exact statutory citation and mechanics (full exemption vs. valuation exclusion) with the TRD Property Tax Division and DSIRE.

## Permitting & Interconnection Notes

- Residential/small systems use a **fast-tracked, size-based interconnection process** under PRC rules; PNM follows the **FERC Small Generator Interconnection Procedures** (0–20 MW).
- A local **building-code electrical inspection is required** and is the trigger event for NSMDTC eligibility — the credit's fund year is set by the date of successful inspection.
- **Community Solar** is under active PRC rulemaking in 2026 (program cap ~500 MW total, allocated by utility territory) — affects community, not rooftop, solar. *Verify current rule status.*

## Bottom Line

For **New Mexico homeowners**, the stack is strong: **retail-rate net metering** (watch EPE's lower surplus rate), a **refundable 10% state credit up to $6,000**, a **GRT deduction** on equipment and paired storage, and a **property-tax exclusion** — though the state credit draws on a $30M annual pool and §25D federal credit ended after 2025. For **commercial and agricultural projects**, the same 10%/$6,000 credit applies (with REPTC available for utility-scale generation), plus the GRT deduction and federal §48E ITC and depreciation. Confirm the **NSMDTC funding level, its sunset, and the SB 55 status** before committing.
