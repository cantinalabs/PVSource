# Wisconsin Solar Incentives & Policy

Wisconsin has **no statewide net metering mandate** — crediting terms are set utility-by-utility in tariffs approved by the Public Service Commission (**PSC**). The state's standout incentive is the **Focus on Energy** rebate for residential and non-residential solar, backed by permanent **sales tax** and **property tax** exemptions. There is **no state income tax credit** and **no active SREC market**.

## At-a-Glance Summary

| Incentive / Policy | Residential | Commercial |
|---|---|---|
| Net metering | Utility-specific (no state mandate) | Utility-specific |
| SREC market | None | None |
| State income tax credit | None | None |
| Sales & use tax exemption | 100% state (Wis. Stat. § 77.54) | Applies to qualifying equipment |
| Property tax exemption | 100% of added value (§ 70.111(18)) | 100% of added value |
| Focus on Energy rebate | $600/kW, up to $2,400 | Tiered, up to $25,000 (verify) |
| Administering agency | PSC / Focus on Energy | PSC / Focus on Energy |

> The key variable in Wisconsin is **your specific utility's net metering tariff** — caps and credit rates vary widely, so who serves you can matter as much as the incentives.

## Overview

Wisconsin lacks a single statewide net metering law fixing rates. The obligation traces to a 1982 PSC order requiring regulated utilities to file net metering tariffs (historically for systems up to 20 kW), but each utility sets its own terms subject to PSC approval. This makes Wisconsin genuinely **utility-specific** — We Energies, Alliant/WPL, Madison Gas & Electric, and Xcel each operate differently. The state's most impactful incentive is the **Focus on Energy** rebate program, which was notably increased for 2026.

## Net Metering / Net Billing

There is no statewide mandate; terms come from each utility's PSC-approved tariff:

- **System caps** commonly sit around **20 kW** for retail-rate-based net metering, though larger behind-the-meter thresholds have been raised for commercial DG.
- **Credit treatment varies** — some utilities credit monthly excess at or near retail; others use avoided/wholesale cost. Annual leftover credits are commonly cashed out (often January 1) at a wholesale/avoided-cost rate.

> **Actively evolving.** The PSC has an open/reopened investigation into parallel generation (net metering) and whether Wisconsin should undertake a Value of Solar Study. We Energies has moved toward single bidirectional metering. Because tariffs differ by utility and are under review, confirm the current caps and credit rate with **your specific utility's tariff** — verify at DSIRE/official site.

For how these crediting structures affect payback, see [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md).

## SREC / RECs

Wisconsin has **no active SREC market** and **no solar carve-out**. The state's Renewable Portfolio Standard (roughly 10% renewable, met years ago) uses general RECs from any renewable source, with no ongoing statewide REC incentive driving residential solar. See the [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md) for how such markets operate elsewhere.

## State Tax Credits

**Wisconsin offers no state income tax credit for residential or commercial solar.**

## Federal Tax Credits

Federal incentives are separate from state policy:

- **Residential (§25D):** The 30% federal residential clean energy credit **ended after December 31, 2025** under OBBBA. Systems placed in service in 2026 and later generally do **not** qualify.
- **Commercial (ITC / §48E):** Business-owned solar may still qualify for the commercial investment tax credit and the technology-neutral **§48E** credit, subject to current rules.

See [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md).

## Utility Incentives / Rebates

**Focus on Energy** is Wisconsin's statewide energy-efficiency and renewables program and the state's primary solar cash incentive. Per the 2026 program year:

- **Residential solar PV: $600 per kW, up to a maximum of $2,400** per single-family home. (This was increased for 2026.)
- **Multifamily: up to $25,000.**
- **Non-residential:** reported at $600/kW up to $2,400, then a lower per-kW rate for additional capacity, up to a **$25,000** maximum.

Key requirements: the site must be served by a **participating** Focus on Energy electric utility (most Wisconsin utilities participate; some municipals/co-ops do not); the system must be grid-tied, behind-the-meter, appropriately oriented, minimally shaded, and installed by a licensed Wisconsin contractor. One rebate per site (residential) or per meter (non-residential) per year.

> **Verify current terms.** Some third-party sources claim the Focus rebate cannot be combined with the federal tax credit; the official program materials do not clearly state that exclusion. Confirm eligibility, deadlines, and the non-residential tier structure against the official 2026 Focus on Energy program documents before relying on them. Battery/storage rebate amounts were not verifiable statewide — do not assume one exists.

## Sales Tax Exemption

Wisconsin provides a **100% state sales and use tax exemption** for qualifying solar and renewable energy products (panels, inverters, racking, wiring) under **Wis. Stat. § 77.54**. Eligibility is tied to producing usable energy from renewable resources.

> The exemption covers the 5% state sales tax; confirm whether separate local/county taxes apply and the exact statutory subsection against Wisconsin DOR guidance.

## Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

Under **Wis. Stat. § 70.111(18)**, the **added assessed value** attributable to a solar (or other qualifying renewable) energy system is **100% exempt** from general property tax. This applies to **both residential and commercial** property. The exemption covers the value added by the system, not the underlying real estate.

## Permitting & Interconnection Notes

- Interconnection is governed by **Wis. Admin. Code ch. PSC 119**, providing uniform statewide standards for DG facilities up to 15 MW. Revised rules effective May 2024 incorporate IEEE 1547-2018.
- Small systems (under 20 kW) receive streamlined/expedited review; larger systems may require engineering studies.
- Local building/electrical permits and utility approval are required; installers must hold a valid Wisconsin contractor's license.

## Bottom Line

Wisconsin's solar value proposition is a combination of the **Focus on Energy rebate** (up to $2,400 residential in 2026), permanent **sales and property tax exemptions**, and **utility-specific net metering** — which means your utility's tariff can materially change the math. There is **no state income tax credit** and **no SREC market**. With the residential federal §25D credit gone after 2025, the Focus on Energy rebate and utility bill savings carry more weight for homeowners in 2026, while businesses can still pursue the commercial ITC/§48E. Confirm your utility's current net metering terms and the current Focus on Energy rebate rules before proceeding.

> **Educational use only.** This page is a general reference, not tax, legal, or financial advice. Incentive values, tariff rates, and program rules change frequently. Verify all figures with the PSC, Focus on Energy, the Wisconsin Department of Revenue, your utility, and a qualified professional before making decisions.
