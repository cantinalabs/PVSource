# Pennsylvania Solar Incentives & Policy

Pennsylvania offers **full-retail net metering** (a strong point) and a **tradable SREC market** that is currently **oversupplied and low-priced**. There is **no state solar income-tax credit**, **no statewide sales-tax exemption**, and property-tax treatment is handled **locally**. The historic **Pennsylvania Sunshine** rebate is closed. This guide covers residential and commercial systems and where they diverge.

> **Note (as of 2026-06-30):** PA SREC prices are volatile and market-driven; net-metering and tax details can change. Treat figures below as directional and **verify at the Pennsylvania PUC, PA DEP, and [DSIRE](https://www.dsireusa.org/)** before relying on them.

## At-a-Glance Summary

| Policy area | Residential | Commercial |
|---|---|---|
| Net metering | **Full retail**, monthly carryover, annual true-up | Full retail (size limits apply) — *verify* |
| SREC market | **Tradable but oversupplied / low** (~$20–$40, volatile) | Same market; more RECs per project |
| State tax credit | None | None |
| Federal tax credit | §25D **ended after 12/31/2025** (OBBBA) | §48E ITC — see [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md) |
| Utility rebate | PA Sunshine **closed**; limited local programs | Limited / grant-based — *verify* |
| Sales tax exemption | **No** statewide exemption (6% applies) | Manufacturing/resale exemptions may apply — *verify* |
| Property tax | **Local** — no uniform statewide exemption | Local treatment |

## Overview

Pennsylvania's distributed-solar value stack is driven mainly by **full-retail net metering** plus income from selling **SRECs** into the state's compliance market — though that market has been **chronically oversupplied**, keeping prices low. Unlike some neighbors, PA has **no statewide sales-tax exemption** and **no state income-tax credit**, and its property-tax handling is **local**. The **Pennsylvania Sunshine Solar Program** (a 2009-era rebate) is **historical/closed**.

## Net Metering / Net Billing

Pennsylvania has one of the region's more favorable net-metering rules:

- **Full retail-rate** credit for exported energy.
- Credits **carry forward monthly** and are **trued up annually** (excess typically settled at a price-to-compare/generation rate at true-up).
- Net metering is **mandatory for investor-owned utilities** — including **PECO, PPL, and FirstEnergy** (Met-Ed, Penelec, Penn Power, West Penn Power).
- **System-size caps and customer-class rules** apply; larger commercial systems must confirm eligibility and any capacity limits with the utility/PUC.

See [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md) for how full-retail net metering compares to net billing.

## SREC / RECs

Pennsylvania has a **tradable compliance SREC market** tied to its **Alternative Energy Portfolio Standard (AEPS)** solar carve-out:

- **1 SREC = 1 MWh**; SRECs are sold separately from the electricity.
- The market is widely described as **oversupplied**, so prices are **low and volatile** — commonly cited in the **~$20–$40** range recently (some quotes lower). A typical residential system might earn on the order of a few hundred dollars per year, **but this is not guaranteed** and can fall further. **Verify current spot/contract pricing** with an SREC aggregator and DSIRE.
- **Commercial systems** generate proportionally more SRECs, so even a weak market can add up at scale — but revenue should be modeled conservatively.

For how tradable SREC markets work and how PA compares to stronger markets, see [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md).

## State Tax Credits

Pennsylvania has **no state solar income-tax credit** for residential or commercial systems.

## Federal Tax Credits

- **Residential (§25D):** The 30% Residential Clean Energy Credit **ended after December 31, 2025** under **OBBBA**. Systems placed in service on/after **January 1, 2026** do **not** qualify.
- **Commercial (ITC / §48E):** The **Clean Electricity Investment Credit (§48E)** remains available for businesses/TPO systems, subject to **begin-construction** deadlines and phase-outs, and pairs with **MACRS depreciation**.

Details and timelines: [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md).

## Utility Incentives / Rebates

- **Pennsylvania Sunshine Solar Program:** A **historical rebate** (funded ~2009 under the Alternative Energy Investment Act) that helped early adopters. It is **no longer accepting applications**; it is relevant today mainly because some older systems' SREC eligibility ties back to it.
- Current **utility rebates are limited**; occasional **grant programs** (e.g., DEP/Solar for Schools-type or federal-pass-through programs) may apply — **verify current availability**.

## Sales Tax Exemption

Pennsylvania has **no statewide sales-tax exemption for residential solar equipment** — the **6% state sales tax generally applies** (plus local add-ons in Allegheny County and Philadelphia).

- **Commercial note:** Businesses may access **manufacturing** or **resale** exemptions in specific circumstances, and equipment used directly in certain production processes can qualify — **confirm eligibility** with the PA Department of Revenue and a tax professional.

## Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

Pennsylvania has **no uniform statewide property-tax exemption** for solar. Treatment is **local**:

- Whether a rooftop system increases assessed value depends on the **county/municipal assessor**.
- Commercial and ground-mount projects should confirm local assessment practice, since added value can affect real-estate taxes.

**Verify local practice** with the county assessment office and DSIRE.

## Permitting & Interconnection Notes

- Interconnection follows **PA PUC / utility interconnection rules**; small residential systems use expedited review.
- Local **AHJ permitting** (building/electrical) varies by municipality.
- SREC participation requires **registering the system** with the AEPS program and a **certified SREC tracking/aggregation** service (e.g., PJM-GATS).

## Bottom Line

- **Residential:** **Full-retail net metering** is the main strength; **SREC income is a modest, uncertain bonus**; there is **no state credit, no sales-tax exemption, and only local property-tax treatment**. The **federal §25D credit is gone after 2025**, materially changing 2026 payback.
- **Commercial:** Net metering + **§48E ITC + MACRS** + at-scale SREC volume drive returns, but **model SREC revenue conservatively** given oversupply, and confirm any manufacturing sales-tax exemption and local property-tax impact.

For programs in other states, see [More State Programs](incentive-more-states.md).
