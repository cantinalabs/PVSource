# Nevada Solar Incentives & Policy

Nevada pairs abundant sun with a distinctive policy mix: **no state income tax** (so no state income-tax credit is even possible), a **tiered net-metering successor** administered by NV Energy, and a **utility-scale property- and sales-tax abatement** aimed at large projects rather than rooftops. This guide summarizes the current landscape for both residential and commercial solar as of mid-2026. Programs change frequently — confirm live figures with the administering agency and the **Database of State Incentives for Renewables & Efficiency ([DSIRE](https://programs.dsireusa.org/))** before modeling a project.

For background on how export compensation works, see [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md). For the federal side, see [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md). For neighboring-state comparisons, see [More State Programs](incentive-more-states.md) and [More State Programs (Round 2)](incentive-more-states-2.md).

## At-a-Glance Summary

| Policy Lever | Residential | Commercial |
|---|---|---|
| **Net metering** | NV Energy tiered NEM — **Tier 4 = 75%** of retail for excess | Small commercial same tiers; large C&I on DG/wholesale rules |
| **SREC / REC market** | None (RPS uses utility-held Portfolio Energy Credits) | None tradable at retail |
| **State income-tax credit** | None (no state income tax) | None (no state income/corporate tax) |
| **Utility rebate** | NV Energy storage incentive *(oversubscribed — verify)* | Commercial storage incentive *(verify)* |
| **Sales-tax exemption** | None for rooftop | Partial **abatement to 2.6%** only via RETA (large projects) |
| **Property tax** | N/A (added value generally not separately taxed — verify) | **55% abatement** for qualifying ≥10 MW facilities (RETA) |

> **Framing:** Nevada's headline incentives (RETA's 55% property-tax and reduced sales-tax abatements) are designed for **utility-scale and large commercial** projects, not single-family rooftops. Homeowner economics rest primarily on net metering plus the federal picture.

## Overview

Nevada's investor-owned utility, **NV Energy**, serves the large majority of customers across its northern (Sierra Pacific) and southern (Nevada Power) territories. State solar policy is set by the **Public Utilities Commission of Nevada (PUCN)** and the **Governor's Office of Energy (GOE)**. Because Nevada levies **no personal or corporate income tax**, the classic state solar income-tax credit does not exist here; value instead comes from net-metering credits, the RETA abatement program (large projects), and federal incentives covered on our federal pages.

## Net Metering / Net Billing

NV Energy moved off full retail net metering years ago to a **tiered "NEM 2.0" structure** in which each successive tier opened as installed capacity crossed statutory benchmarks and credited excess generation at a declining share of retail.

- **Current tier: Tier 4 — excess generation credited at 75% of the retail rate.** Tier 4 has no capacity cap; 75% holds unless changed by the legislature.
- **Rate lock:** A customer keeps their tier's credit rate for **20 years** at the original install location.
- **Prior tiers (historical):** Tier 1 = 95%, Tier 2 = 88%, Tier 3 = 81% — each filled as applied-for capacity hit an 80 MW benchmark.

**Recent PUCN rate-case changes (verify — some are litigated):**

- A **daily peak demand charge** for residential and small-business customers in **Southern Nevada** was approved to begin **April 2026**; it faces pending legal challenges. *Verify final status.*
- **Northern Nevada** customers installing solar **after Oct. 1, 2025** have excess generation netted on a **15-minute interval basis** (rather than monthly), which reduces the effective value of exports even though credits remain valued at 75% of retail.

**Residential vs. commercial:** Standard net metering applies to systems generally sized to on-site load (residential and small commercial). Larger commercial and industrial customers typically interconnect under **distributed-generation / wholesale** arrangements and separate rate schedules rather than retail net metering. *Verify exact commercial NEM size thresholds against the current tariff.*

> Nevada has **not** adopted a formal California-style net-billing successor tariff; the 15-minute netting and demand-charge changes are the closest analog. See [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md) for the mechanics.

## SREC / RECs

**Nevada has no tradable retail SREC market.** The state's Renewable Portfolio Standard operates on **Portfolio Energy Credits (PECs)**, which obligated utilities (chiefly NV Energy) hold and trade among themselves for compliance — homeowners and most commercial owners **cannot monetize SRECs** here. Contrast this with the Mid-Atlantic markets covered in our [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md).

## State Tax Credits

**None — and none possible in income-tax form.** Nevada has **no state personal or corporate income tax**, so there is no state solar income-tax credit. The only income-tax-based incentives available to Nevada projects are **federal**.

## Federal Tax Credits

Federal incentives are national, not Nevada-specific, but they drive project economics:

- **Residential (§25D):** The federal Residential Clean Energy Credit **ended after December 31, 2025** under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA). Systems placed in service in 2026 and later generally **cannot claim §25D**. Confirm your situation with a tax professional.
- **Commercial (ITC / §48E):** The technology-neutral **Clean Electricity Investment Credit (§48E)** generally remains available for commercial and utility-scale projects, subject to phase-out schedules, wage/apprenticeship rules, and bonus adders. See [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md) for current terms.

## Utility Incentives / Rebates

NV Energy does **not** directly rebate residential solar panels, but it has run a **storage (battery) incentive** and various efficiency/EV programs:

- **Residential Energy Storage Incentive:** When active, tiered by rate plan — roughly **$0.19/Wh (cap $3,000)** for time-of-use customers and **$0.095/Wh (cap $1,500)** for non-TOU customers, not to exceed 50% of equipment cost. The program has been **repeatedly oversubscribed and suspended**; its open/closed status as of mid-2026 is uncertain. **Verify directly with NV Energy before relying on it.**
- **Commercial Energy Storage Incentive:** A separate commercial storage incentive exists. *Verify current terms.*
- **PowerShift efficiency rebates:** Heat pumps, heat-pump water heaters, and related measures — first-come, first-served, historically exhausting mid-year. *Verify current availability.*
- **EV / managed charging:** Charger rebates and managed-charging programs (and a lower-income EV rebate).

## Sales Tax Exemption

**No broad statewide sales-tax exemption** exists for residential or small commercial solar equipment. Nevada's base state sales/use tax is **6.85%**, with combined local rates exceeding 8% in some counties; ordinary rooftop purchases are taxed normally.

The **only** sales/use tax relief is the **partial abatement under RETA** (see below), which reduces the applicable rate to **2.6%** on qualifying equipment for large projects — not available to homeowners or small commercial rooftop.

## Property Tax Exemption / Abatement — RETA

Nevada's flagship incentive is the **Renewable Energy Tax Abatement (RETA)** program, administered by the **Governor's Office of Energy** under **NRS 701A.300–701A.450**:

| RETA Benefit | Terms |
|---|---|
| **Property-tax abatement** | **55%** of taxes on real & personal property, for **20 fiscal years** |
| **Sales/use-tax abatement** | Rate reduced to **2.6%** on qualifying equipment, up to ~3 years (construction) |
| **Minimum capacity** | Generally **≥ 10 MW** production capacity; must operate in Nevada ≥ 10 years |

**Eligibility thresholds (location-dependent):**

- **Rural locations:** ≥ **$3,000,000** capital investment and ≥ **50** full-time construction employees.
- **Other/urban locations:** ≥ **$10,000,000** capital investment and ≥ **75** full-time construction employees.
- Green-industry hourly wage floors apply.

> **This is a utility-scale / large-commercial program.** Single-family and small commercial rooftop systems do **not** qualify for RETA. For most homeowners, "Nevada's property-tax break" is not a rooftop incentive. *Verify how your county treats the added value of a residential system.*

## Permitting & Interconnection Notes

- **Interconnection:** NV Energy governs interconnection through its **Net Metering and Energy Storage Interconnection Handbook**. Residential and small systems follow a standardized net-metering application; larger systems undergo distributed-generation interconnection studies. New Northern Nevada solar (post-Oct. 1, 2025) is subject to 15-minute interval metering (see above).
- **Automated permitting (SolarAPP+):** Adoption is **jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction**, not a statewide mandate. Clark County and the City of Las Vegas offer streamlined/online permitting for licensed contractors; fire-code rooftop setbacks apply. *Verify which specific AHJ has adopted an instant-permit tool.*

> A commonly cited "SB 178 automated-permitting" reference appears to be a **misattribution** (2023 SB 178 was an unrelated veterans-services bill that failed). Do not rely on it; **verify** the correct statute for any Nevada permitting mandate.

## Bottom Line

For **Nevada homeowners**, solar economics rest chiefly on **NV Energy net metering at 75% of retail (Tier 4)** — good but no longer full retail — plus whatever federal treatment applies (note §25D ended after 2025). The much-touted **55% property-tax abatement (RETA)** and reduced sales tax apply to **large commercial and utility-scale** projects, not rooftops. For **commercial developers of ≥10 MW facilities**, RETA plus the federal §48E ITC and depreciation are the core drivers. Watch the Southern Nevada demand charge and Northern Nevada 15-minute netting changes closely, as both erode distributed-solar value.
