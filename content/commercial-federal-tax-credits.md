# Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits

## Overview
The federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) is the primary federal incentive for commercial solar projects in the United States. Established under Section 48 of the Internal Revenue Code and significantly expanded by the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) of 2022.

## Current ITC Structure (Post-IRA)

### Base Investment Tax Credit (Section 48)
- **Base Rate**: 6% of qualified project costs
- **Bonus Rate**: 30% (5x bonus) if prevailing wage and apprenticeship requirements are met
- **Applicable Period**: Projects placed in service 2022-2032 (phases down after 2032)

### Prevailing Wage & Apprenticeship Requirements
To qualify for the 30% bonus rate (vs. 6% base):
- **Prevailing Wage**: All laborers/mechanics paid prevailing wage rates (Davis-Bacon Act)
- **Apprenticeship**: Minimum % of labor hours by qualified apprentices (10% in 2022, 12.5% in 2023, 15% 2024+)
- **Documentation**: Must maintain certified payroll records

### Energy Communities Bonus (Section 48(e))
Additional **10% bonus** (total 40%) for projects in:
- Brownfield sites
- Coal closure areas (coal mine/plant closure since 2009/2009)
- Statistical areas with high fossil fuel employment (≥0.17% employment, ≥50% county tax revenue)

### Domestic Content Bonus (Section 48(g))
Additional **10% bonus** (total 40%) if:
- 100% of steel/iron manufactured in US
- 40%+ of manufactured products mined/produced/manufactured in US (2024: 20%, 2025: 40%, 2026+: 55%)

### Low-Income Communities Bonus (Section 48(e))
Additional **10-20% bonus** for projects in low-income communities or serving low-income households (allocated via annual capacity allocation program).

### Stacking Potential
| Combination | Total ITC |
|-------------|-----------|
| Base only | 6% |
| Base + Prevailing Wage/Apprenticeship | 30% |
| Base + PWA + Energy Community | 40% |
| Base + PWA + Domestic Content | 40% |
| Base + PWA + Low-Income Community | 40-50% |
| **Maximum Stack (PWA + Energy Community + Domestic Content)** | **50%** |

## Section 48E (Clean Electricity Investment Credit) - Starting 2025
- Technology-neutral credit replacing Section 48 for projects placed in service after 2024
- 6% base / 30% bonus with PWA requirements
- Applies to zero-emission facilities (solar, wind, storage, nuclear, etc.)
- Phases down when US electricity sector hits 75% emissions reduction (2032 earliest)

## Section 45 (Production Tax Credit - PTC)
Alternative to ITC for commercial solar:
- **Rate**: $27.50/MWh (2024, inflation-adjusted) with PWA
- **Base**: $5.50/MWh without PWA
- **Term**: 10 years from placed-in-service
- **Election**: Choose ITC or PTC (not both) per project
- Better for high-capacity-factor projects in sunny regions

## Qualified Costs (ITC Basis)
- Solar PV modules, inverters, racking, tracking
- Balance-of-system (BOS) equipment
- Installation labor (with PWA)
- Sales/use tax on equipment
- Engineering, design, permitting fees
- **NOT eligible**: Land, fencing, grading beyond site prep, interconnection beyond point of interconnection, O&M contracts, developer fees

## Key Deadlines & Phase-Down
| Year Placed in Service | ITC Rate (with PWA) | PTC Rate (with PWA) |
|------------------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| 2022-2032 | 30% | $27.50/MWh (infl. adj.) |
| 2033 | 22.5% | $20.63/MWh |
| 2034 | 15% | $13.75/MWh |
| 2035+ | 0% | 0% |

*Section 48E phase-down triggers at 75% sector emissions reduction*

## Key Considerations for Commercial Solar EPC
1. **Prevailing Wage Compliance**: Critical for 30% vs 6% - track certified payroll weekly
2. **Domestic Content**: Document domestic content % from suppliers early
3. **Energy Communities**: Map project locations against DOE energy community maps
4. **Domestic Content Certifications**: Collect manufacturer certifications early
5. **Transferability (IRA Section 6418)**: Credits can be sold/transferred (one-time) - useful for tax appetite limitations
6. **Direct Pay (Elective Payment)**: Tax-exempt entities (nonprofits, governments, tribes) can receive direct payment
7. **Interconnection Costs**: Only up to point of interconnection eligible for ITC
8. **Storage**: Standalone storage now qualifies for ITC (standalone or co-located)
