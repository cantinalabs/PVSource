# Massachusetts Solar Incentives & Policy

Massachusetts is a top-tier solar market anchored by the **Solar Massachusetts Renewable Target (SMART)** program — now in its **SMART 3.0** iteration — paired with **net metering**, a **15% residential state income-tax credit (up to $1,000)**, a **100% sales-tax exemption**, and a **20-year property-tax exemption**. This page gives the full state picture and links to the dedicated SMART deep-dive for program mechanics.

> **For the full mechanics of the SMART program (declining blocks, base compensation rates, and adders), see the dedicated page:** [Massachusetts — SMART](incentive-ma-smart.md). This page covers the surrounding tax, permitting, and interconnection landscape for **residential** and **commercial** projects.

---

## Overview

Massachusetts solar policy is administered by the **Department of Energy Resources (DOER)**, with utility tariffs approved by the **Department of Public Utilities (DPU)** and administrative support from the **Massachusetts Clean Energy Center (MassCEC)**. **SMART** is a **declining-block, per-kWh production incentive** paid on top of net metering. In 2025–2026 the state launched **SMART 3.0**, a redesigned successor that operates on an **annual program-year** structure with rates and capacity reset by DOER.

| At a glance | Residential | Commercial |
|-------------|-------------|------------|
| **Primary state incentive** | SMART 3.0 (per-kWh, ~$0.03–$0.06/kWh flat) | SMART 3.0 (formula-based ~$0.06–$0.10/kWh + adders) |
| **Incentive term** | **20 years** | 20 years |
| **Export compensation** | Net metering | Net metering (subject to caps for larger systems) |
| **State income-tax credit** | **15% of net cost, up to $1,000** | Not applicable (see federal/depreciation) |
| **Sales-tax exemption** | **100%** (exempt from 6.25%) | **100%** |
| **Property-tax exemption** | **20 years** (added value exempt) | 20-year exemption |
| **Program administrator** | DOER / DPU / MassCEC | DOER / DPU / MassCEC |

> **2026 status flag:** **SMART 3.0** applications for the 2026 program year opened **January 1, 2026**, and DPU **approved the revised SMART 3.0 tariff on May 19, 2026**, directing utilities to file company-specific tariffs. Rates, blocks, and adder values reset annually — **verify current program-year values at [Mass.gov SMART](https://www.mass.gov/solar-massachusetts-renewable-target-smart) and [DSIRE](https://programs.dsireusa.org/).**

## Net Metering / Net Billing

Massachusetts retains **net metering**, which works **alongside** (not instead of) the SMART incentive.

- **Residential** systems generally earn **retail or near-retail net-metering credits** for exported energy, banked and rolled forward.
- **Commercial and larger systems** are subject to **net-metering caps and class rules** (public vs. private, system-size thresholds); credits for some classes are valued at a modified rate. Utilities: **Eversource, National Grid, and Unitil.**

> **Key point:** SMART pays you **per kWh generated** (whether consumed or exported) for **20 years**, while net metering credits your **exports** — the two stack. Model both together. See [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md).

## SREC / RECs

Massachusetts **retired its SREC / SREC-II certificate programs** to new projects when it transitioned to **SMART**.

- Legacy **SREC-I and SREC-II** systems continue earning under their original terms, but **new systems earn through SMART**, not a tradable certificate market.
- Under SMART, the renewable attributes are effectively monetized through the **per-kWh incentive** rather than sold as separate certificates.

For how tradable certificate markets differ (e.g., NJ, MD), see [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md).

## State Tax Credits

Massachusetts offers a **residential** solar income-tax credit.

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Credit** | **15% of net system cost** |
| **Cap** | **$1,000** maximum |
| **Applies to** | Owner-occupied residential (primary residence) |
| **Carryforward** | Unused credit may be carried forward **up to 3 years** |
| **Regulation** | 830 CMR 62.6.1 (Residential Energy Credit) |

> **Bold takeaway:** The **15% / $1,000** credit is a meaningful residential offset, claimable against MA state income tax and carried forward if it exceeds one year's liability.

**Commercial** taxpayers do **not** use the residential credit; the business value case comes from the **federal ITC and MACRS depreciation** — see below.

> **Verify** the current credit at the [Massachusetts Department of Revenue](https://www.mass.gov/orgs/massachusetts-department-of-revenue) and [DSIRE](https://programs.dsireusa.org/).

## Federal Tax Credits

> **Residential (§25D):** The **25% federal Residential Clean Energy Credit ended for systems placed in service after December 31, 2025** under OBBBA. **Cash and loan residential purchases in 2026 receive no §25D credit** in Massachusetts.

- **Commercial / business systems** remain eligible for the **federal ITC under §48 / §48E**, plus **MACRS accelerated depreciation** and potential **domestic-content, energy-community, and low-income adders**, with **transferability** and **direct-pay** options.

## Utility Incentives / Rebates

Massachusetts delivers its main incentive through **SMART** (a DOER program paid via the utility tariff) rather than standalone utility rebates.

- **SMART 3.0 adders** materially change project value: **storage**, **canopy/carport**, **community solar**, **low-income / affordable-housing**, and **land-use** categories. Adder values reported in the ~**$0.0025–$0.08/kWh** range.
- **Storage requirement:** Larger systems (reported **>1 MW**) generally must **incorporate storage** to qualify for the adder; systems **behind-the-meter under 250 kW** are excluded from the annual capacity block, preserving small-scale deployment.
- **Community solar** projects face stronger low-income participation requirements under SMART 3.0.

> **Verify** current adder values, block capacity, and storage rules at [Mass.gov SMART 3.0](https://www.mass.gov/info-details/smart-30-program-details) and [MassCEC](https://www.masscec.com/).

## Sales Tax Exemption

Massachusetts provides a **100% sales-and-use tax exemption** for solar/renewable-energy equipment.

- **Residential and commercial** equipment purchases are **fully exempt from the 6.25% state sales tax**.
- Covers solar panels, inverters, racking, and other qualifying components.

> **Verify** eligible equipment at the [Massachusetts Department of Revenue](https://www.mass.gov/orgs/massachusetts-department-of-revenue) and [DSIRE](https://programs.dsireusa.org/).

---

## Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

Massachusetts exempts the **added value** of a qualifying solar-energy system from local property tax.

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Benefit** | Exemption from the **increase in assessed value** from the solar system |
| **Term** | **20 years** |
| **Applies to** | Residential and (typically) commercial systems used to supply the property's energy |
| **Authority** | Massachusetts General Laws (property-tax exemption for solar/wind) |

> **Key point:** The **20-year** property-tax exemption means the added home/facility value from solar does **not** raise your property-tax bill during that window. Confirm local assessor treatment for commercial and larger systems.

## Permitting & Interconnection notes

- **Permitting:** Municipal building and electrical permits apply; Massachusetts follows the state building/electrical code. See [Residential Permitting](residential-permitting.md) and [Commercial Permitting](commercial-permitting.md).
- **Interconnection:** Governed by the **Massachusetts DPU interconnection tariff**, with tiered review (simplified/expedited/standard) by system size. Larger commercial systems may require **impact studies and upgrades**. See [Grid Studies & Queues](grid-studies-queues.md) and [IEEE 1547](grid-ieee1547.md).
- **SMART qualification:** Systems must be **qualified under SMART** with the administrator; incentive payments run **20 years** from qualification/energization.

## Bottom line

Massachusetts pairs a **20-year per-kWh SMART incentive** with **net metering**, a **15% / $1,000 residential income-tax credit**, a **100% sales-tax exemption**, and a **20-year property-tax exemption** — a strong, layered package. With **§25D federal residential credit gone after 2025**, residential economics now lean more heavily on SMART and the state credit. For **commercial** projects, **SMART 3.0's formula-based rate plus adders (especially storage)**, combined with the **federal §48E ITC and MACRS**, drive the return. The key action is to **lock in the current SMART program-year rate and applicable adders** before the annual reset.
