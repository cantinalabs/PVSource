# Arizona Solar Incentives & Policy

Arizona has one of the best solar resources in the United States and a favorable set of state-level incentives — including a **state income-tax credit**, a **sales-tax exemption**, and a **property-tax exemption** — though it has moved to **net billing** rather than full retail net metering. This guide covers the current landscape for **residential and commercial** systems, with pointers to verify time-sensitive values.

> **Educational reference only — not tax or legal advice.** Programs change frequently. Verify every value below with the relevant agency, your utility, and [DSIRE](https://programs.dsireusa.org/) before making decisions.

## At-a-Glance Summary

| Policy / Incentive | Status | Notes |
|--------------------|--------|-------|
| Statewide net metering | No — **Net Billing** | Export credits below retail; declining annually |
| SREC market / RPS solar carve-out | No SREC market | RES has a DG requirement met via utilities, not homeowner SRECs |
| State personal income-tax credit | Yes — **25%, max $1,000** | Residential; 5-year carryforward |
| State corporate income-tax credit | Yes (commercial) | Separate commercial solar tax credit; caps apply |
| Federal residential credit (§25D) | Ended | Expired after Dec 31, 2025 (OBBBA) |
| Federal commercial ITC (§48/§48E) | Yes | See federal pages; phase-out schedule applies |
| Utility rebates / storage incentives | Yes (varies) | APS, SRP, TEP storage/DR programs |
| Sales-tax exemption | Yes | Solar equipment exempt from state TPT |
| Property-tax exemption | Yes | Added value excluded from assessed value |
| Major utilities | APS, SRP, TEP + munis/co-ops | SRP is a public power district (not ACC-regulated) |

## Overview

Arizona has an exceptional solar resource — roughly **6.0 to 6.5+ peak sun-hours per day**, among the highest in the nation, with Phoenix and Tucson receiving abundant year-round sun. This makes Arizona one of the most productive states for rooftop PV per installed kW.

Utility structure:

- **Arizona Public Service (APS)** and **Tucson Electric Power (TEP)** are **investor-owned utilities regulated by the Arizona Corporation Commission (ACC)**.
- **Salt River Project (SRP)** is a large **public power district** serving the Phoenix metro — it is **governed by its own elected board, not the ACC**, and sets its own rate and export rules (often including demand charges for solar customers).
- Various **municipal utilities and electric cooperatives** set their own policies.

Key regulators/agencies: the **ACC** regulates APS and TEP; **SRP** is self-governed; the **Arizona Department of Revenue (ADOR)** administers the tax credits and exemptions.

> **Key point:** **SRP is not ACC-regulated** — its solar rate structure (often with demand charges) differs meaningfully from APS and TEP. Identify the exact utility first.

---

## Net Metering / Net Billing

Arizona **ended traditional retail-rate net metering** (via the ACC's 2016 "Value of Solar" decision) and moved to **net billing / export-rate crediting** for the ACC-regulated utilities.

- **APS and TEP** credit exported solar at an **export rate (the "Resource Comparison Proxy," RCP)** set below the retail rate. This rate is **reviewed and typically steps down annually**, with the ACC historically **capping annual decreases (e.g., ~10%)** to provide some predictability.
  - Recent illustrative export rates (verify current): **APS ~6.2¢/kWh**, **TEP ~5.7¢/kWh**. These change annually.
- **SRP** (not ACC-regulated) uses its own **solar/export price plans**, historically featuring **demand charges** and a modest export credit (recently around **~3.5¢/kWh** on its export plan — verify current).
- Customers are **grandfathered on the export rate in effect at interconnection for a set term** (often ~10 years for the export-rate lock), a key detail to confirm.

Because export credits are well below retail, **battery storage and self-consumption improve economics** in Arizona much as they do in other net-billing states.

For the general mechanics, see [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md).

> **Key point:** Arizona is a **net-billing state** with **annually declining export rates**. Confirm your utility's current export rate, demand-charge structure, and grandfathering term before sizing a system.

## SREC / RECs

Arizona has a **Renewable Energy Standard and Tariff (REST)** administered by the ACC, which historically included a **distributed-generation (DG) requirement**, but it **does not operate a tradable retail SREC market** the way SREC states do.

- **No homeowner-sellable SRECs** in Arizona.
- Utilities meet REST/DG obligations through their own procurement and programs, not through a homeowner certificate market.

For how SREC markets work where they exist, see [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md).

> **Key point:** **No retail SREC income in Arizona.** Value comes from the state tax credit, net-billing credits, and utility programs.

## State Tax Credits

Arizona is one of the states that **does** offer a state income-tax credit for solar.

- **Residential — Solar Energy Credit (A.R.S. §43-1083):** **25% of the system cost, capped at $1,000**, against Arizona personal income tax. Unused credit can be **carried forward up to five years**. Available for a taxpayer's residence (primary or secondary), with the cap applied per residence/year. **Verify current cap and rules with ADOR.**
- **Commercial/Corporate:** Arizona also offers **commercial solar tax incentives** (e.g., the Commercial/Industrial Solar Energy Tax Credit program), which are **credit-capped and subject to program allocation limits** administered through ADOR and (for certain programs) other state authorities. Availability and caps change; **verify current commercial credit status.**

See [Residential Incentives](residential-incentives.md) and [Commercial State Tax Credits](commercial-state-tax-credits.md).

> **Key point:** The **residential 25% / $1,000 credit** is a real, ongoing benefit with a 5-year carryforward. Commercial credits are separate and allocation-limited. Verify at [Arizona Department of Revenue](https://azdor.gov/) and [DSIRE Arizona](https://programs.dsireusa.org/system/program?state=AZ).

## Federal Tax Credits

- **Residential (§25D Residential Clean Energy Credit):** **ended after December 31, 2025** under OBBBA. Homeowner purchases placed in service in 2026 or later cannot claim the 30% federal residential credit (the Arizona 25% state credit is separate and continues). See [Residential Incentives](residential-incentives.md).
- **Commercial (§48 / §48E ITC and MACRS depreciation):** remains available in 2026, subject to OBBBA's phase-out timeline and begin-construction / placed-in-service deadlines. Domestic-content, energy-community, and low-income adders may apply. See [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md).

> Federal rules changed materially under OBBBA and are date-sensitive. Confirm with a tax professional and the [IRS](https://www.irs.gov/).

## Utility Incentives / Rebates

Arizona's major utilities run various **storage, demand-response, and time-of-use programs** rather than large upfront rooftop rebates:

| Utility | Program type (verify current) | Notes |
|---------|-------------------------------|-------|
| **APS** | Residential battery / demand-response and TOU rate options | Storage incentives and pilots change; check current offers |
| **SRP** | Battery storage incentive / demand-management programs | SRP has offered battery rebates; solar plans carry demand charges |
| **TEP** | Storage and TOU programs | Program terms vary |

- **SRP** has periodically offered **battery storage rebates** (e.g., a per-project or per-kWh incentive) — availability and amounts change, so verify.
- Because ACC-regulated utilities use **net billing with declining export rates**, **storage + TOU optimization** is increasingly central to Arizona solar value.

> **Key point:** Utility rebates in Arizona are **storage- and demand-management-focused and frequently changing.** Check your specific utility's current program page.

For storage design and economics, see [Battery Storage](battery-storage.md).

## Sales Tax Exemption

Arizona provides a **sales-tax (Transaction Privilege Tax, TPT) exemption for solar energy devices.**

- **Retail sales of qualifying solar energy devices are exempt from Arizona state TPT**, and the exemption is generally applied automatically at the point of sale by registered solar retailers/installers.
- This covers panels, inverters, batteries, solar water heaters, and other qualifying components.

> **Key point:** Arizona's **solar sales-tax exemption is real and typically automatic.** Confirm current scope with the [Arizona Department of Revenue](https://azdor.gov/) and [DSIRE](https://programs.dsireusa.org/).

---

## Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

Arizona provides a **property-tax exemption for the value added by solar energy devices (A.R.S. §42-11054 and related statutes).**

- **The added value of a solar energy device is excluded from the property's assessed (full cash) value** — installing solar **does not increase your property-tax bill** for the value the system adds.
- The exemption is generally applied by the county assessor; **installers or owners may need to file the appropriate solar-device valuation form** with the county. (This is sometimes described as covering the useful life of the system — commonly cited as effectively long-term / multi-decade coverage; **verify the exact treatment and any filing requirement with your county assessor.**)

> **Key point:** Solar's added value is **excluded from Arizona property tax.** Confirm any required form and current treatment with your **county assessor** and [DSIRE](https://programs.dsireusa.org/).

## Permitting & Interconnection Notes

- Arizona has a **moderate AHJ landscape** centered on major municipalities (Phoenix, Tucson, Mesa, Chandler, Scottsdale, etc.) plus counties; permitting processes vary by jurisdiction.
- Arizona is a **growing adopter of SolarAPP+** automated residential permitting in participating cities, which can enable same-day permit approval.
- **Interconnection** for APS and TEP follows ACC-jurisdictional interconnection rules; **SRP** administers its own interconnection process. Utility interconnection review can add time beyond the local permit.
- High summer temperatures make **thermal derating and equipment placement/ventilation** important design considerations statewide.

See [Residential Permitting](residential-permitting.md) and [Commercial Permitting](commercial-permitting.md).

---

## Bottom Line

Arizona's world-class sun, **25% / $1,000 residential state tax credit**, **sales-tax exemption**, and **property-tax exemption** make it a strong solar market — but the move to **net billing with annually declining export rates** (and, for SRP customers, demand charges) means **self-consumption and battery storage increasingly drive the economics**. There is **no retail SREC market**, and the federal residential credit ended after 2025, so value comes from the state credit, tax exemptions, net-billing credits, and (for commercial) the federal ITC and depreciation. Because export rates, utility programs, and credit caps change regularly, **verify current specifics with the ACC or SRP, ADOR, your utility, and DSIRE before committing.**
