# New Jersey Solar Incentives & Policy

New Jersey is one of the most mature and financially attractive solar markets in the country. Its incentive backbone is the **Successor Solar Incentive (SuSI)** program, which pays fixed-value **SREC-IIs** for **15 years**, layered on top of **true 1:1 retail net metering**, a **100% sales-tax exemption**, and a **100% property-tax exemption**. This page gives the full state picture and links to the dedicated SuSI deep-dive for program mechanics.

> **For the full mechanics of SuSI, the ADI vs. CSI tracks, and SREC-II values, see the dedicated page:** [New Jersey — SuSI](incentive-nj-susi.md). This page adds the surrounding tax, permitting, and interconnection landscape for **residential** and **commercial** projects.

---

## Overview

New Jersey's solar programs are run by the **New Jersey Board of Public Utilities (NJBPU)**, with registration and administration through **New Jersey's Clean Energy Program (NJCEP)**. After the state hit its statutory solar milestone and retired the legacy SREC and Transition Incentive (TI) programs, **SuSI** became the current framework, paying **SREC-IIs** (1 per MWh generated) at an **administratively set, locked value** for residential and small commercial systems.

| At a glance | Residential | Commercial |
|-------------|-------------|------------|
| **Primary state incentive** | SuSI — **ADI** track (fixed SREC-II) | SuSI — **ADI** (≤5 MW net-metered) or **CSI** (>5 MW / grid-supply) |
| **SREC-II term** | **15 years**, value locked at registration | 15 years |
| **Export compensation** | **Full retail 1:1 net metering** | Full retail net metering (behind-the-meter) |
| **State income-tax credit** | None (NJ has no state solar income-tax credit) | None |
| **Sales-tax exemption** | **100%** (exempt from 6.625%) | **100%** |
| **Property-tax exemption** | **100%** of added value | **100%** of added value |
| **Program administrator** | NJBPU / NJCEP | NJBPU / NJCEP |

> **2026 status flag:** The **ADI SREC-II value steps down over time** for new registrants (reported around **$76.50/MWh** for systems registered on/after early March 2026, down from ~$85/MWh, with residential and non-residential segments differing). **Values change with each program-year update and by segment — verify the current rate at [NJCEP Solar](https://njcleanenergy.com/renewable-energy/programs/solar) and [DSIRE](https://programs.dsireusa.org/).**

## Net Metering / Net Billing

New Jersey retains **true 1:1 retail-rate net metering** — one of the most valuable export policies remaining in the U.S.

- **Residential and commercial** behind-the-meter systems earn a **full retail-rate credit** (~$0.16–$0.26/kWh depending on utility and rate class) for exported energy.
- **Monthly credit rollover** applies, with an **annual true-up**; at true-up, any net excess is typically reconciled (commonly at avoided-cost/wholesale rate for the residual).
- Utilities: **PSE&G, JCP&L, Atlantic City Electric, and Rockland Electric.**

> **Key point:** Because NJ credits exports at the **full retail rate**, self-consumption plus banked credits can offset nearly the entire energy portion of the bill — making net metering the single largest ongoing value driver, on top of SREC-IIs. See [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md).

## SREC / RECs

New Jersey **replaced tradable SRECs** with **fixed-value SREC-IIs** under SuSI. This is a major distinction from the old market.

| Item | Legacy SREC | SuSI SREC-II |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **Value** | Floating market price | **Fixed / administratively set** ($/MWh) |
| **Term** | 15 years | **15 years**, locked at registration |
| **Risk** | Market price volatility | Price certainty (rate locked at install) |
| **New registrations?** | Closed | **Open** (with periodic step-downs) |

- **1 SREC-II = 1 MWh** of generation.
- The value is **locked on your registration date** and cannot be reduced for the 15-year term — but the **rate for new registrants steps down**, so **registration timing matters**.

See the [New Jersey — SuSI](incentive-nj-susi.md) deep-dive and, for how tradable markets differ elsewhere, [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md).

## State Tax Credits

New Jersey does **not** offer a **state solar income-tax credit** for residential or commercial owners. The state's value case is delivered instead through **SuSI SREC-IIs, full retail net metering, and the sales/property-tax exemptions** below.

> **Bold takeaway:** Unlike New York or Massachusetts, there is **no NJ state income-tax credit** — don't budget for one. The economics come from SuSI + net metering + exemptions.

## Federal Tax Credits

> **Residential (§25D):** The **25% federal Residential Clean Energy Credit ended for systems placed in service after December 31, 2025** under OBBBA. **Cash and loan residential purchases in 2026 receive no §25D credit** in New Jersey.

- **Commercial / business systems** remain eligible for the **federal ITC under §48 / §48E**, plus **MACRS accelerated depreciation** and potential **domestic-content, energy-community, and low-income adders**. Commercial owners can also use **transferability** or **direct pay** to monetize the credit.

See: [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md) · [MACRS / Bonus Depreciation](incentive-macrs-depreciation.md) · [Domestic Content Adder](incentive-domestic-content.md) · [Energy Communities](incentive-energy-communities.md) · [Direct Pay](incentive-direct-pay.md) · [Transferability](incentive-transferability.md).

## Utility Incentives / Rebates

New Jersey's incentive design is **statewide (SuSI)** rather than utility-by-utility rebate-driven, so there is **no standalone upfront utility rebate** for typical residential/commercial PV in the way NY-Sun operates.

- Value flows through **SREC-IIs (SuSI)** and **net-metering credits** rather than a $/W rebate.
- **Community solar** is available through the **NJ Community Solar Energy Program (CSEP)** for subscribers who cannot host their own array — see [Community Solar](residential-community-solar.md) and [Commercial Community Solar](commercial-community-solar.md).
- **Storage:** NJBPU has been developing a **storage incentive program**; verify current status at NJBPU.

> **Verify** current program-year SREC-II values and any storage incentives at [NJBPU](https://www.nj.gov/bpu/) and [NJCEP](https://njcleanenergy.com/renewable-energy/programs/solar).

## Sales Tax Exemption

New Jersey provides a **100% sales-and-use tax exemption** for qualifying solar-energy equipment.

- **Residential and commercial** purchases are **fully exempt from the 6.625% state sales tax** via the **ST-4** exempt-use certificate.
- This applies to solar panels, inverters, racking, and other qualifying components.

> **Verify** eligible equipment and certificate procedures at the [NJ Division of Taxation](https://www.nj.gov/treasury/taxation/) and [DSIRE](https://programs.dsireusa.org/).

---

## Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

New Jersey offers a **100% property-tax exemption** for the added value of a renewable-energy system.

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Benefit** | **100% exemption** from the added assessed value attributable to the solar system |
| **Applies to** | Residential and commercial |
| **Mechanism** | Certification of the renewable-energy system (local application/certificate) |
| **Effect** | Your property taxes **do not increase** because you installed solar |

> **Key point:** New Jersey's exemption is **full (100%)** rather than time-limited — the added value is excluded from assessment for the life of the system, provided certification is maintained. Confirm the current certification procedure locally.

## Permitting & Interconnection notes

- **Permitting:** New Jersey has adopted measures to streamline residential solar permitting; municipal building/electrical permits still apply. See [Residential Permitting](residential-permitting.md) and [Commercial Permitting](commercial-permitting.md).
- **Interconnection:** Governed by **NJBPU net-metering and interconnection rules**, with tiered review (Level 1/2/3) based on system size and grid impact. Larger commercial systems may require **studies and upgrades**. See [Grid Studies & Queues](grid-studies-queues.md) and [IEEE 1547](grid-ieee1547.md).
- **Registration:** Systems must **register with NJBPU** to earn SREC-IIs under SuSI — coordinate registration timing with the step-down schedule.

## Bottom line

New Jersey has a **best-in-class** residential and commercial solar economics package: **fixed, 15-year SREC-II income (value locked at install), true 1:1 retail net metering, a 100% sales-tax exemption, and a 100% property-tax exemption.** There is **no state income-tax credit**, and — as everywhere — the **residential §25D federal credit ended after 2025**. For **commercial** projects, layer the **federal §48E ITC and MACRS depreciation** on top of SuSI and net metering. The key action item is **timing your SuSI registration** ahead of the next SREC-II step-down.
