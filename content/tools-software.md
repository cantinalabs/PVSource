# Tools & Software

The modern solar industry runs on software. From the first satellite-imagery sketch of a roof to a bankable 25-year energy yield report, almost every step of a photovoltaic (PV) project is now mediated by a specialized tool. This guide surveys the platforms that designers, engineers, installers, EPCs, sales teams, and informed DIYers actually use in 2025-2026, organized by workflow stage. For each tool we note whether it skews toward **residential**, **commercial** (commercial-and-industrial, or "C&I"), or serves **both**, along with the platform it runs on and the general shape of its pricing.

A note on pricing: the solar-software market consolidates and re-prices constantly (Aurora's acquisition of HelioScope, Enphase's ownership of Solargraf, Stem's ownership of AlsoEnergy, and steady annual AutoCAD increases are all examples). Treat every figure here as an approximate tier, not a quote. Always verify current pricing directly with the vendor.

## CAD & Drafting

Computer-aided design tools produce the construction drawings, plan sets, single-line diagrams, and stamped engineering documents that permitting offices and EPCs require. Most dedicated solar-design platforms now generate plan sets automatically, but general-purpose CAD remains essential for commercial work, custom structures, and anything an authority having jurisdiction (AHJ) wants in DWG format.

### AutoCAD

**AutoCAD**, from Autodesk, is the long-standing industry-standard 2D/3D drafting application. In solar it is used for site plans, array layouts, civil and structural detailing, and producing the DWG/PDF plan sets larger AHJs and EPCs expect. Its native DWG format is the lingua franca of professional CAD exchange. Pricing has risen steadily year over year.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | CAD & drafting |
| Primary users | Both (heavier in commercial/EPC) |
| Platform | Desktop (Windows/Mac), with web/mobile companions |
| Pricing model | Subscription, annual per-seat (premium tier); verify current pricing |

[AutoCAD](https://www.autodesk.com/products/autocad/)

### AutoCAD Electrical

**AutoCAD Electrical** is a toolset within AutoCAD (bundled in some Autodesk subscriptions) purpose-built for electrical controls design: single-line diagrams, three-line diagrams, panel layouts, and automated wire/component numbering. For larger commercial and utility PV, it streamlines the electrical documentation that string-and-inverter systems demand.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | CAD & drafting (electrical) |
| Primary users | Commercial / utility |
| Platform | Desktop (Windows) |
| Pricing model | Subscription, per-seat (often via AutoCAD toolset bundle); verify current pricing |

[AutoCAD Electrical](https://www.autodesk.com/products/autocad/included-toolsets/autocad-electrical)

### DraftSight

**DraftSight**, from Dassault Systèmes, is a lower-cost professional 2D (and, in higher tiers, 3D) CAD package built around the DWG format, positioned as an AutoCAD alternative. It is popular with smaller solar shops and engineers who need DWG compatibility without AutoCAD's price. It is sold in Professional, Premium, and Network tiers.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | CAD & drafting |
| Primary users | Both |
| Platform | Desktop (Windows/Mac) |
| Pricing model | Subscription, per-seat (mid tier, well below AutoCAD); verify current pricing |

[DraftSight](https://www.draftsight.com/)

### LibreCAD (free option)

**LibreCAD** is a free, open-source 2D CAD application. It is a reasonable no-cost entry point for DIYers and very small operations doing simple layouts, though it works natively in DXF rather than DWG, which limits seamless file exchange with AutoCAD-based partners. **QCAD** (its upstream project) and **NanoCAD** are comparable free/low-cost alternatives worth knowing.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | CAD & drafting (free) |
| Primary users | Residential / DIY |
| Platform | Desktop (Windows/Mac/Linux) |
| Pricing model | Free (open source); verify current pricing |

[LibreCAD](https://librecad.org/)

**Residential vs commercial:** Most residential installers rely on the plan-set output of their solar-design platform and rarely touch full CAD; LibreCAD or DraftSight covers occasional needs. Commercial and EPC teams lean on AutoCAD and AutoCAD Electrical for stamped, DWG-native construction and electrical documentation.

## Solar Design & Sales / Proposal

This is the most crowded and fastest-moving category. These platforms combine remote roof modeling, panel layout, shade analysis, energy estimates, financial proposals, and (increasingly) CRM and e-signature into one sales-to-design workflow. Market consolidation here has been significant.

### Aurora Solar

**Aurora Solar** is a leading cloud-based solar design and sales platform. It offers remote shading analysis (LIDAR-assisted), automated 3D roof modeling, performance simulation, and customer-facing proposals, primarily for residential and small-commercial. Aurora acquired **HelioScope** (via Folsom Labs) to extend into larger C&I simulation.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Solar design / sales proposal |
| Primary users | Residential & small commercial |
| Platform | Web |
| Pricing model | Subscription, quote-based (per-seat tiers, enterprise); verify current pricing |

[Aurora Solar](https://aurorasolar.com/)

### OpenSolar

**OpenSolar** is notable for being genuinely free to use for its core design, proposal, and workflow tools, monetizing instead through hardware/finance partner placements and paid API/connector access. It offers 3D design, proposals, and lead-gen, and in early 2026 launched "OS 3.0" with an AI assistant. A strong choice for cost-conscious residential installers.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Solar design / sales proposal |
| Primary users | Residential (and small commercial) |
| Platform | Web |
| Pricing model | Free for core tools; paid API/connector access; verify current pricing |

[OpenSolar](https://www.opensolar.com/)

### Solargraf

**Solargraf**, owned by **Enphase**, is a cloud-based solar (and storage) design and proposal tool aimed at fast residential sales: aerial-imagery design, instant proposals, permit packages, and e-signature. Being Enphase-owned, it integrates tightly with Enphase microinverter and battery products.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Solar design / sales proposal |
| Primary users | Residential |
| Platform | Web |
| Pricing model | Subscription, per-seat tiers; verify current pricing |

[Solargraf](https://www.solargraf.com/)

### Pylon

**Pylon** is a sales-led, all-in-one platform pairing solar design with CRM, proposals, and e-signature, priced aggressively for residential sales teams. Its engineering is geared toward sales-calculator accuracy rather than lender-grade simulation, so it is typically paired with a dedicated yield tool for engineering sign-off.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Solar design / sales proposal / CRM |
| Primary users | Residential |
| Platform | Web |
| Pricing model | Subscription, per-seat (lower tier); verify current pricing |

[Pylon](https://getpylon.com/)

### Scanifly

**Scanifly** specializes in drone- and imagery-based site capture for "quality-obsessed" contractors: a short drone flight (any GPS-equipped drone) produces an accurate 3D model with structural, electrical, and shade data, feeding PV design, field operations, plan sets, and proposals. It also integrates Nearmap, satellite, and USGS LiDAR data for remote design.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Solar design / drone site capture |
| Primary users | Both |
| Platform | Web + mobile (drone capture) |
| Pricing model | Subscription, quote-based platform tiers; verify current pricing |

[Scanifly](https://scanifly.com/)

### Others worth knowing

- **Solo (Solo by Tigo / formerly SolarNexus-adjacent proposal tools)**, **Sighten**, **Enact**, **Arka 360**, and **Demand IQ** also appear in residential proposal workflows. **EasySolar** and **SolarEdge Designer** (free, manufacturer-tied) round out the field. Verify current ownership and pricing, as this segment churns frequently.

**Residential vs commercial:** OpenSolar, Solargraf, and Pylon are squarely residential sales tools. Aurora spans residential through small commercial. Scanifly and HelioScope (below) carry the most weight on larger commercial roofs where engineering rigor matters.

## Performance Simulation & Yield Modeling

These tools answer the bankability question: how many kilowatt-hours will this array actually produce over its life, with what uncertainty (P50/P90)? They incorporate weather datasets, detailed shading, module/inverter models, and loss factors. This is where engineers and investors live.

### PVsyst

**PVsyst** is the long-standing industry benchmark for detailed PV simulation and the reference tool for bankable energy-yield assessments. It models near and far shading, detailed loss diagrams, bifacial gain, and degradation, and is widely required by lenders and independent engineers for commercial and utility projects.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Performance simulation |
| Primary users | Commercial / utility |
| Platform | Desktop (Windows) |
| Pricing model | Subscription / license, per-seat (mid-high tier); verify current pricing |

[PVsyst](https://www.pvsyst.com/)

### HelioScope

**HelioScope**, now part of **Aurora Solar** (acquired via Folsom Labs), is a web-based design-and-simulation tool focused on the C&I range (roughly 100 kW to multi-MW). It combines fast layout with component-level performance modeling and single-line generation, and has gained recognition as a bankable web-based platform.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Performance simulation / design |
| Primary users | Commercial |
| Platform | Web |
| Pricing model | Subscription, per-seat tiers (Basic/Pro/Enterprise); verify current pricing |

[HelioScope](https://www.helioscope.com/)

### NREL SAM (System Advisor Model)

**SAM (System Advisor Model)**, from the U.S. National Renewable Energy Laboratory, is a free, open-source performance-and-financial model spanning PV, storage, CSP, wind, and more. It excels at detailed financial modeling (LCOE, PPA, tax-equity, P50/P90) and is widely used in research, development, and feasibility work. Source code is open and free for commercial use.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Performance + financial simulation |
| Primary users | Commercial / utility / research |
| Platform | Desktop (Windows/Mac/Linux) + SDK |
| Pricing model | Free (open source); verify current pricing |

[NREL SAM](https://sam.nrel.gov/)

### PVWatts

**PVWatts**, also from NREL, is a free browser-based calculator that gives a quick AC-energy estimate from a handful of inputs (capacity, tilt, azimuth, mounting, losses). It uses SAM's simplified model and requires no download. Ubiquitous for ballpark residential estimates and quick checks.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Quick yield estimate |
| Primary users | Both (especially residential / quick checks) |
| Platform | Web |
| Pricing model | Free; verify current pricing |

[PVWatts](https://pvwatts.nrel.gov/)

### Solargis

**Solargis** provides bankable, high-resolution solar resource and meteorological data plus simulation software (Prospect for assessment, Evaluate for PV simulation, Forecast for production forecasting). It is a go-to for utility-scale developers and financiers needing validated long-term irradiance data.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Solar resource data + simulation |
| Primary users | Commercial / utility |
| Platform | Web (+ data services / API) |
| Pricing model | Subscription / quote (data-tier dependent); verify current pricing |

[Solargis](https://solargis.com/)

### Meteonorm

**Meteonorm** is a meteorological-data tool and reference database that supplies typical-year weather and irradiance data files (e.g., TMY) used as inputs to simulation tools like PVsyst and SAM. Widely used to feed yield models with site-specific climate data.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Meteorological / irradiance data |
| Primary users | Commercial / utility (engineering input) |
| Platform | Desktop / data files (+ web services) |
| Pricing model | License / subscription, per-seat; verify current pricing |

[Meteonorm](https://meteonorm.com/)

### Simulation comparison table

| Tool | Best for | Users | Platform | Pricing |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| PVsyst | Bankable detailed yield, shading | Commercial/utility | Desktop | Paid license/subscription |
| HelioScope | Fast C&I design + simulation | Commercial | Web | Paid subscription |
| NREL SAM | Detailed financial + performance modeling | Commercial/research | Desktop | Free (open source) |
| PVWatts | Quick AC-energy estimates | Both / residential | Web | Free |
| Solargis | Bankable resource data + simulation | Commercial/utility | Web | Paid / quote |
| Meteonorm | Weather/irradiance input data | Commercial/utility | Desktop/data | Paid license |

**Residential vs commercial:** Residential workflows rarely need standalone simulation — PVWatts (or the estimate baked into Aurora/OpenSolar) suffices. PVsyst, HelioScope, SAM, Solargis, and Meteonorm are the engineering-and-finance toolkit for commercial and utility projects where bankability is required.

## Financial, Proposal & Storage Modeling

These tools translate a system design into dollars: utility-rate analysis, savings projections, financing scenarios, and — increasingly — battery dispatch and demand-charge management modeling. Critical for commercial deals and for any project pairing solar with storage.

### Energy Toolbase (ETB)

**Energy Toolbase** (ETB Developer) is a leading financial and utility-rate analysis platform for solar and energy-storage projects, covering both behind-the-meter and front-of-meter cases. It maintains a large library of utility rates and integrates with major battery vendors (e.g., Tesla, Sungrow) for storage modeling. Its output is accepted by some lenders without independent validation. The broader ETB suite also includes controls (ETB Controller) and monitoring (ETB Monitor).

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Financial / storage modeling |
| Primary users | Commercial (and solar-plus-storage) |
| Platform | Web |
| Pricing model | Subscription, per-seat (free trial available); verify current pricing |

[Energy Toolbase](https://www.energytoolbase.com/)

### Others worth knowing

- **NREL SAM** (above) doubles as a powerful free financial model (LCOE/PPA/tax-equity).
- **HOMER** (UL Solutions) for microgrid and hybrid-system optimization and economics.
- **Sunbase** and various **proposal/CRM** tools include savings calculators for residential sales.
- Lender- and TPO-specific portals (e.g., financing providers' own quoting engines) handle residential loan/lease/PPA modeling.

**Residential vs commercial:** Residential financing math is usually handled inside the proposal tool or a lender's quoting portal. Energy Toolbase, SAM, and HOMER are the commercial/storage workhorses where demand charges, tariffs, and dispatch economics get complex.

## Site Assessment & Shading

Before (or instead of) a site visit, these tools establish roof geometry, obstructions, and shading. They range from free machine-learning estimators to high-resolution aerial imagery subscriptions to handheld on-roof shade instruments.

### Nearmap

**Nearmap** provides frequently updated, high-resolution aerial imagery (and derived 3D/AI roof data) used by solar designers for remote measurement and shading inputs. It is integrated into several design platforms (including Scanifly and others) and sold as a content/data subscription.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Aerial imagery / site data |
| Primary users | Both |
| Platform | Web (+ API / integrations) |
| Pricing model | Subscription, quote-based (coverage-dependent); verify current pricing |

[Nearmap](https://www.nearmap.com/)

### Google Project Sunroof

**Google Project Sunroof** is a free, consumer-facing tool that uses satellite imagery and machine learning to estimate a roof's solar potential, including rough shading and sizing. It is best for first-pass feasibility and homeowner education rather than engineering-grade design.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Feasibility / shading estimate |
| Primary users | Residential / consumer |
| Platform | Web |
| Pricing model | Free; verify current pricing |

[Google Project Sunroof](https://sunroof.withgoogle.com/)

### Solmetric SunEye

The **Solmetric SunEye** (SunEye 210 and the newer SunEye 360) is a handheld, on-site instrument that captures a fisheye sky view to measure shading and annual solar access at a specific point on a roof. The classic 210 is the long-standing field reference; note that older models have been phased in and out, so confirm current availability.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | On-site shade measurement (hardware) |
| Primary users | Both (field assessment) |
| Platform | Handheld device (+ software) |
| Pricing model | Hardware purchase (one-time), quote; verify current pricing |

[Solmetric SunEye](https://www.solmetric.com/)

### Drone & LiDAR options

Drone-based capture (e.g., via **Scanifly** or **DroneDeploy**) and publicly available **USGS LiDAR** datasets increasingly substitute for handheld shade tools, producing full 3D site models from a short flight or existing point-cloud data. These deliver accuracy comparable to on-roof instruments without putting a person on the roof.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | 3D capture / shading (drone & LiDAR) |
| Primary users | Both |
| Platform | Drone hardware + web software / open data |
| Pricing model | Subscription (software) + drone hardware; LiDAR data often free; verify current pricing |

**Residential vs commercial:** Project Sunroof and a quick Nearmap/imagery check suit residential pre-sales; SunEye remains a trusted residential field instrument. Commercial and EPC teams favor drone/LiDAR capture and high-resolution imagery subscriptions for large, complex roofs and ground mounts.

## Structural & Racking Design

Solar arrays impose loads on roofs and ground structures, and racking must be engineered for wind, snow, and seismic conditions per code. This category spans full structural-analysis software and free, vendor-specific racking configurators.

### RISA

**RISA** (RISA-3D and related products) is established structural-analysis and design software used by engineers for solar carports, ground mounts, roof-attachment checks, and custom steel structures. It is a desktop-based, US-focused professional engineering package.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Structural analysis |
| Primary users | Commercial / utility (PE engineering) |
| Platform | Desktop (Windows) |
| Pricing model | License / subscription, per-seat, quote; verify current pricing |

[RISA](https://risa.com/)

### SkyCiv

**SkyCiv** is a cloud-based structural-engineering platform with a solar focus: it includes wind/snow load generators, a large library of quick-design calculators, member/connection/foundation design, and APIs — useful for racking verification, carports, and ground mounts. Often cited as easier to use and more affordable than legacy desktop tools.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Structural analysis (cloud) |
| Primary users | Commercial (and engineering services) |
| Platform | Web (+ API) |
| Pricing model | Subscription, per-seat tiers; verify current pricing |

[SkyCiv](https://skyciv.com/)

### IronRidge Design Assistant

The **IronRidge Design Assistant** is a free, vendor-provided configurator that walks installers through designing IronRidge roof and ground-mount racking, generating engineered layouts, bills of materials, quoting, and permitting support. A free account saves and shares projects.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Racking design (vendor tool) |
| Primary users | Both (residential-heavy) |
| Platform | Web |
| Pricing model | Free (vendor tool); verify current pricing |

[IronRidge Design Assistant](https://www.ironridge.com/design-tools/)

### Unirac U-Builder

**Unirac U-Builder** (U-Builder 2.0) is Unirac's free web configurator for designing and documenting Unirac racking systems with automatic bills of materials. A companion Unirac racking app integrates directly into Aurora Solar's design mode.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Racking design (vendor tool) |
| Primary users | Both (residential-heavy) |
| Platform | Web |
| Pricing model | Free (vendor tool); verify current pricing |

[Unirac U-Builder](https://design.unirac.com/)

**Residential vs commercial:** Residential racking is almost always handled inside free vendor configurators (IronRidge, Unirac) that output engineered layouts and BOMs. RISA and SkyCiv come into play for commercial carports, ground mounts, and any structure needing a stamped engineering analysis beyond a vendor's standard span tables.

## Monitoring & Commissioning

Once a system is energized, monitoring software verifies production, flags faults, and supports O&M. Residential monitoring is usually the inverter manufacturer's free portal; commercial and utility fleets use hardware-agnostic platforms that aggregate many sites and vendors.

### Enphase Enlighten

**Enphase** systems report through the **Enlighten** platform, which provides module-level production data (each microinverter reports individually) for homeowners and installer fleet management. The standard for microinverter-based residential systems.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Monitoring (manufacturer portal) |
| Primary users | Residential (and small commercial) |
| Platform | Web + mobile app |
| Pricing model | Free with Enphase hardware; verify current pricing |

[Enphase Enlighten](https://enphase.com/homeowners/enlighten-app)

### SolarEdge Monitoring

**SolarEdge** provides a free monitoring portal giving panel-level visibility through its power optimizers, plus installer fleet tools. Standard for SolarEdge optimizer/inverter systems.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Monitoring (manufacturer portal) |
| Primary users | Residential & commercial |
| Platform | Web + mobile app |
| Pricing model | Free with SolarEdge hardware; verify current pricing |

[SolarEdge Monitoring](https://www.solaredge.com/en/products/software-tools/monitoring)

### AlsoEnergy (PowerTrack)

**AlsoEnergy**, now owned by **Stem, Inc.**, offers the **PowerTrack** platform — a hardware-agnostic, edge-to-cloud monitoring and control system that is an industry standard for commercial and utility-scale fleets, including PV-plus-storage asset optimization.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Monitoring & control (fleet) |
| Primary users | Commercial / utility |
| Platform | Web + edge hardware |
| Pricing model | Subscription / quote (asset-based); verify current pricing |

[AlsoEnergy](https://home.alsoenergy.com/)

### Solar-Log

**Solar-Log** provides data loggers and the cloud monitoring platform **Solar-Log WEB Enerest** (Enerest 4), designed to be device-agnostic and work across loggers from many manufacturers. Common in residential-through-commercial multi-vendor installations, especially in Europe.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Monitoring (hardware + cloud) |
| Primary users | Both |
| Platform | Web + logger hardware |
| Pricing model | Hardware purchase + subscription tiers; verify current pricing |

[Solar-Log](https://www.solar-log.com/)

**Residential vs commercial:** Residential systems almost always use the free manufacturer portal (Enlighten or SolarEdge Monitoring) matching their inverter. Commercial and utility fleets need hardware-agnostic platforms — AlsoEnergy/PowerTrack and Solar-Log — that aggregate mixed equipment across many sites with O&M alerting and SCADA-style control.

## Permitting & Utility / String-Sizing Tools

Permitting and interconnection are often the slowest steps in a residential project. Automation tools and manufacturer string-sizing calculators reduce errors and turnaround time.

### SolarAPP+

**SolarAPP+** (Solar Automated Permit Processing Plus), developed with NREL and the U.S. Department of Energy, is a web platform that lets participating local governments instantly issue code-compliant permits for residential solar and solar-plus-storage. It is free for jurisdictions to adopt, has surpassed 100,000 permits issued, and integrates with platforms like OpenSolar for permit tracking. Primarily residential in scope.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | Automated permitting |
| Primary users | Residential |
| Platform | Web |
| Pricing model | Free for jurisdictions (per-permit fees may apply to applicants); verify current pricing |

[SolarAPP+](https://www.gosolarapp.org/)

### String-sizing & utility tools

Manufacturer string-sizing calculators are essential, free engineering aids that ensure inverter input voltage/current windows are respected across temperature extremes. Common examples:

- **Enphase**, **SolarEdge**, **SMA Sunny Design**, **Fronius Solar.configurator**, and similar vendor calculators size strings and verify compatibility for their own inverters.
- AHJ/utility-specific interconnection portals handle the permit-to-operate (PTO) and interconnection-application steps; these vary by jurisdiction and utility.

| | |
|---|---|
| Category | String sizing / utility |
| Primary users | Both |
| Platform | Web |
| Pricing model | Free (vendor tools); verify current pricing |

**Residential vs commercial:** SolarAPP+ targets fast residential permitting. String-sizing calculators serve both, but matter most on commercial string-inverter systems where voltage windows and temperature design margins are critical. Commercial interconnection is handled through utility-specific portals and engineering review rather than instant-approval tools.
