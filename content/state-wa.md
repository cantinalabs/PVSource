# Washington Solar Incentives & Policy

Washington combines **full 1-for-1 net metering**, a **generous sales-and-use tax exemption** for solar equipment and labor, and **no state income tax** (so no state solar income-tax credit is even possible). The state's marquee production-incentive program has closed to new applicants, so today's value comes from net metering, the sales-tax exemption, and federal treatment. This guide summarizes the current landscape for both residential and commercial solar as of mid-2026. Programs change frequently — confirm live figures with the administering agency and the **Database of State Incentives for Renewables & Efficiency ([DSIRE](https://www.dsireusa.org/))** before modeling a project.

For background on how export compensation works, see [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md). For the federal side, see [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md). For neighboring-state comparisons, see [More State Programs](incentive-more-states.md) and the [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md).

## At-a-Glance Summary

| Policy Lever | Residential | Commercial |
|---|---|---|
| **Net metering** | Full **1:1 retail-rate** (RCW 80.60); 4% cumulative cap | Same framework; successor tariffs possible after cap |
| **SREC / REC market** | None tradable (WREGIS tracking; CETA compliance) | None tradable at retail |
| **State income-tax credit** | **None** (no state income tax) | **None** |
| **Production incentive** | **Closed** — RESIP fully subscribed since 2019 | Closed |
| **Sales-tax exemption** | **100%** for systems ≤100 kW AC (through 2029) | 100% ≤100 kW; **50%** for 101–500 kW AC |
| **Property tax** | No confirmed residential exemption *(verify)* | Utility-scale ≥10 MW exemption (RCW 84.36.680) |

> **Framing:** Washington's old cash **production incentive is closed**. The live levers are **full net metering** and the **sales-tax exemption** — both meaningful, but timing matters because the sales-tax exemption sunsets at the end of 2029.

## Overview

Washington's largest investor-owned utility is **Puget Sound Energy (PSE)**; large consumer-owned utilities include **Seattle City Light** and **Snohomish County PUD**. State policy is set by the **Washington Utilities and Transportation Commission (UTC)** for IOUs, the **Department of Commerce**, and the **Department of Revenue (DOR)** for tax matters; the **WSU Energy Program** administered the former production incentive. Because Washington has **no personal state income tax**, there is **no state solar income-tax credit** — value comes from net metering, the sales-tax exemption, and federal incentives.

## Net Metering / Net Billing

Washington provides **net metering at the full retail rate** under **RCW 80.60**.

- **Full 1-for-1 retail-rate credit**; excess credits roll over month to month and are trued up annually in **March/April**.
- **Cumulative cap:** Net metering is available first-come, first-served until the earlier of **June 30, 2029** or the date a utility's cumulative net-metered capacity reaches **4% of its 1996 peak demand**. Once a utility hits the cap (or 2029), it may adopt a successor rate/tariff (subject to UTC review for IOUs).
- **PSE:** Continues offering 1:1 net metering under **Rate Schedule 150**; a successor tariff was under development. Existing net-metering customers are grandfathered. **Verify current Schedule 150 availability and any successor tariff** directly with PSE and the UTC.
- **Consumer-owned utilities** (Seattle City Light, Snohomish PUD) offer net metering under the same RCW 80.60 framework but set their own tariffs by board action. *Verify each utility's current cap status.*

See [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md) for the mechanics.

## SREC / RECs

**Washington has no tradable SREC market.** RECs are tracked and retired through **WREGIS**; there is no solar carve-out generating per-MWh SREC value like the Mid-Atlantic markets in our [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md). Small residential systems generally do not monetize RECs.

Washington's clean-energy mandate is the **Clean Energy Transformation Act (CETA, RCW 19.405)**: **GHG-neutral electricity by 2030 and 100% carbon-free by 2045**, with coal eliminated by end of 2025. Utilities file Clean Energy Implementation Plans on a four-year cycle. *Verify current compliance-period status.*

## State Tax Credits

**None — and none possible in income-tax form.** Washington has **no personal state income tax**, so there is no state solar income-tax credit for residential or commercial owners. Income-tax-based incentives available to Washington projects are **federal only**.

## Federal Tax Credits

Federal incentives are national, not Washington-specific, but they drive project economics:

- **Residential (§25D):** The federal Residential Clean Energy Credit **ended after December 31, 2025** under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA). Systems placed in service in 2026 and later generally **cannot claim §25D**. Confirm with a tax professional.
- **Commercial (ITC / §48E):** The technology-neutral **Clean Electricity Investment Credit (§48E)** generally remains available for commercial and utility-scale projects, subject to phase-out schedules, wage/apprenticeship rules, and bonus adders. Most commercial systems also use **5-year MACRS** depreciation. See [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md); **verify the current bonus-depreciation percentage** separately.

## Utility Incentives / Rebates

- **Renewable Energy System Incentive Program (RESIP):** Washington's former **production incentive** (administered by the WSU Energy Program) is **closed to new applicants**. The Legislature set a **June 30, 2021** certification deadline and a **$110 million** program cap; funding was fully subscribed as of **February 2019**, and new applicants were waitlisted. Existing certified participants continue receiving payments through their program end dates. **No new funding has been authorized.**
- **Utility rebates:** There is **no broad statewide utility cash rebate** for solar. Utility programs skew toward **low-interest loans and low-income assistance** rather than straight rebates. **Verify per utility** (PSE, Seattle City Light, Snohomish PUD, Tacoma Power, Clark PUD, etc.) at DSIRE — these change frequently, and none were confirmed as a general residential rebate in mid-2026.

## Sales Tax Exemption

Washington offers a **sales-and-use tax exemption** for solar energy systems under **RCW 82.08.962** (and use-tax companion RCW 82.12.962):

| System Size (AC) | Exemption |
|---|---|
| **1 kW – 100 kW** | **100%** of state and local sales/use tax |
| **101 kW – 500 kW** | **50%** |

- Applies to **both residential and commercial**, covering machinery, equipment, **and installation labor** (contractor certification conditions apply).
- **Effective window:** July 1, 2019 **through December 31, 2029** (the statute is written to expire January 1, 2030). *Timing matters — verify the current sunset before relying on it.*

## Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

- **Residential rooftop:** Washington does **not** offer a confirmed dedicated property-tax exemption for the added value of small residential PV in the way some states do. **Verify at DOR/DSIRE** if this is load-bearing for a residential project.
- **Utility-scale (≥10 MW AC):** **RCW 84.36.680** exempts the state portion of personal-property tax for solar/wind facilities of at least **10 MW nameplate AC** (construction beginning on/after July 1, 2023; effective Jan 1, 2025), for **10 or 15 years**. In exchange, the owner pays a **production excise tax (RCW 82.96)** — solar at **$80/MW/month** (10-yr) or **$75/MW/month** (15-yr). This is a large-project provision, not residential.

## Permitting & Interconnection Notes

- **Interconnection (IOUs):** UTC-regulated utilities follow **WAC 480-108**, with simplified/expedited/study tiers by size. *Verify exact kW thresholds and timelines against the current WAC.* Consumer-owned utilities set their own interconnection tariffs by board action.
- **Code:** Renewable provisions appear in the Washington State Energy Code; electrical permitting runs through L&I or the local AHJ.
- **Automated permitting (SolarAPP+):** Adopted by a growing number of jurisdictions for instant residential PV/storage permitting, but adoption is **jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction**, not a statewide mandate — **verify** for the specific city/county.

## Bottom Line

For **Washington homeowners**, economics rest on **full 1:1 net metering**, a **100% sales-tax exemption** on systems ≤100 kW (through 2029), and **no state income tax** — offset by the fact that the old production incentive is closed and §25D federal credit ended after 2025. For **commercial projects**, the same net-metering framework plus a 100% (≤100 kW) or 50% (101–500 kW) sales-tax exemption apply, and the federal §48E ITC and depreciation are the core drivers; utility-scale ≥10 MW facilities can use the RCW 84.36.680 property-tax exemption in exchange for the production excise tax. Watch the **4% net-metering cap / 2029 sunset** and the **2029 sales-tax-exemption expiration** — both are timing risks.
