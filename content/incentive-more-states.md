# More State Programs

This guide profiles commercial-solar incentive structures in several additional states beyond the flagship programs covered elsewhere on the site. Each profile is deliberately concise: it names the primary program, explains the incentive **mechanism**, notes **commercial eligibility**, flags **current status**, and links to the authoritative source. Programs change frequently — always confirm live rates, block levels, and enrollment windows with the administering agency or the **Database of State Incentives for Renewables & Efficiency ([DSIRE](https://www.dsireusa.org/))** before modeling a project.

For federal-plus-state stacking and depreciation interactions, see [State Tax Credits & Incentives](state-tax-credits.md). For a deeper treatment of certificate markets, see [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md).

## Maryland

Maryland pairs a **renewable portfolio standard (RPS) solar carve-out** with tax exemptions. The RPS creates a tradable **SREC** market: `1 SREC = 1 MWh` of solar generation, sold to load-serving entities that must meet the solar carve-out or pay the Solar Alternative Compliance Payment (SACP).

- **SREC market** — Maryland SRECs trade on a spot/contract basis. The **2025 SACP is approximately `$55`** (declining on a statutory schedule), which acts as a soft price ceiling. Standard Maryland SRECs have traded in the **low-`$50s`** range recently *(approximate — verify)*. Under a **Certified SREC multiplier** effective January 1, 2025, qualifying in-state projects placed in service between mid-2024 and 2028 can earn up to **`1.5×`** the market rate, so Certified SRECs have traded meaningfully higher. Verify current values at [DSIRE — Maryland](https://programs.dsireusa.org/system/program/md/solar).
- **Sales & use tax exemption** — Solar energy equipment is exempt from Maryland's `6%` sales and use tax.
- **Property tax exemption** — Solar PV is exempt from state and local real property tax; it may not increase assessed value.
- **Grant status** — The Maryland Energy Administration's legacy **Commercial Clean Energy Rebate Program (C-CERP)** was **not offered in FY25 and is not planned to reopen**. MEA has directed commercial applicants to successor grant offerings that rotate by fiscal year. Confirm current grant availability at [Maryland Energy Administration — Business Incentives](https://energy.maryland.gov/business/pages/incentives/cleanenergygrants.aspx).

> Commercial takeaway: In Maryland the recurring value is the SREC stream plus tax exemptions, not a fixed upfront grant. Model SRECs conservatively given price volatility and the SACP ceiling.

## Minnesota

Minnesota's commercial value comes chiefly from **Xcel Energy programs**, a **value-of-solar / community solar** framework, and a property-tax exemption. Minnesota does **not** run a statewide SREC market.

- **Xcel Solar\*Rewards (and Solar\*Rewards for Business)** — A performance-based incentive paying a per-kWh production payment (historically around **`$0.03/kWh`** for a multi-year term, layered on top of net metering) *(approximate — verify current block/rate)*. Availability is capacity-limited and typically opens in annual application windows. See [DSIRE — Xcel Solar\*Rewards](https://programs.dsireusa.org/system/program/detail/5417/xcel-energy-solar-rewards-program).
- **Community Solar Gardens (Solar\*Rewards Community, now the "Melissa Hortman" program)** — Commercial subscribers or developers participate via a **value-of-solar (VOS) tariff** bill credit rather than an SREC. The program was substantially restructured and is administered through the **Minnesota Department of Commerce**. See [MN Commerce — Community Solar Gardens](https://mn.gov/commerce/energy/consumer/energy-programs/community-solar-gardens.jsp).
- **Property tax exemption** — Solar systems are exempt from property tax on the value the panels add.
- **Sales tax exemption** — Solar equipment qualifies for exemption from Minnesota sales tax.

> Commercial takeaway: A commercial host in Xcel territory typically stacks net metering, a Solar\*Rewards production payment (while blocks last), and tax exemptions; larger off-taker or developer roles run through community solar/VOS rather than a certificate market.

## Connecticut

Connecticut consolidated its net metering and REC payments into the **Non-Residential Renewable Energy Solutions (NRES)** tariff, administered by the utilities (Eversource and United Illuminating) under PURA oversight. NRES replaced the older LREC/ZREC program.

- **NRES mechanism** — Projects **bid** into periodic RFPs (roughly February and August) for a **fixed tariff rate** that then holds for a **20-year contract**. Applicants choose a **Buy-All** (export all generation at the tariff) or **Netting** (offset on-site load) tariff structure. The program is authorized for a multi-year run selecting up to roughly `100 MW` of clean energy annually, with separate carve-outs (e.g., public-school solar). Price caps by project size have been adjusted to reflect federal ITC changes. See [PURA — NRES](https://portal.ct.gov/pura/electric/office-of-technical-and-regulatory-analysis/clean-energy-programs/non-residential-renewable-energy-solutions-program) and [Eversource — NRES](https://www.eversource.com/business/save-money-energy/clean-energy-options/connecticut-non-residential-renewable-energy-solutions).
- **Sales & use tax exemption** — Solar equipment is exempt from Connecticut sales and use tax.
- **Property tax exemption** — Class I renewable systems serving eligible loads are exempt from property tax.

> Commercial takeaway: NRES is a **fixed, long-term, bid-set tariff** — closer to a performance-based incentive than a volatile SREC. Bid timing and price-cap category matter; secure an approved rate to lock 20 years of certainty.

## Colorado

Colorado combines the investor-owned utility's **Solar\*Rewards** incentive with strong statewide tax treatment. There is no statewide SREC spot market comparable to the Mid-Atlantic.

- **Xcel Solar\*Rewards** — Xcel Energy's performance/upfront incentive for qualifying customers, with enhanced amounts for income-qualified and disproportionately-impacted communities. Commercial and small-business tracks are capacity-limited. See [DSIRE — Colorado](https://programs.dsireusa.org/system/program/co/solar).
- **Sales & use tax exemption** — Colorado provides a statewide `100%` sales and use tax exemption for qualifying solar equipment (local jurisdiction treatment may vary).
- **Property tax exemption** — Qualifying renewable systems (commonly systems under `2 MW`) receive a `100%` property tax exemption on the added value.

> Commercial takeaway: The dependable value in Colorado is the tax exemption stack plus utility incentives; verify Xcel commercial block availability before assuming a production payment.

## Oregon

Oregon's incentives run primarily through **Energy Trust of Oregon (ETO)** for customers of Portland General Electric and Pacific Power, complemented by an Oregon Department of Energy (ODOE) rebate program.

- **Energy Trust of Oregon incentives** — ETO offers cash incentives for solar and **solar-plus-storage**. Recent structures included a flat per-system solar incentive and a **storage adder around `$400/kWh` capped near `$5,000`** *(approximate — verify current-year offer)*. ETO also runs trade-ally and custom paths for larger commercial projects. See [Energy Trust — Solar](https://www.energytrust.org/incentives/solar-for-your-home/) and [Energy Trust Insider — Solar Incentives](https://insider.energytrust.org/programs/solar/incentives/).
- **Oregon Solar + Storage Rebate Program (ODOE)** — A separately funded rebate that has cycled through **fully-reserved / temporarily-reopened** periods; commercial eligibility is limited (residential and low-income service providers are the core categories). Confirm whether a funding round is open at [ODOE — Solar + Storage Rebate](https://www.oregon.gov/energy/incentives/pages/solar-storage-rebate-program.aspx).
- **Property tax exemption** — Oregon exempts the added value of qualifying solar systems from property tax.

> Commercial takeaway: For commercial and industrial hosts, ETO custom/production-track incentives plus federal ITC and depreciation typically drive economics; the ODOE rebate is intermittent and residentially focused.

## Summary Comparison

| State | Primary commercial mechanism | Certificate market? | Sales tax | Property tax | Status flag |
|-------|------------------------------|---------------------|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| Maryland | RPS SREC stream (+ Certified SREC multiplier) | Yes — spot/contract SREC | Exempt | Exempt | Legacy commercial grant closed; SRECs active |
| Minnesota | Xcel Solar\*Rewards PBI; VOS community solar | No SREC; uses VOS tariff | Exempt | Exempt | Capacity-limited; community solar restructured |
| Connecticut | NRES fixed 20-yr bid tariff | No spot market (REC folded into tariff) | Exempt | Exempt | Active; periodic RFP bids |
| Colorado | Xcel Solar\*Rewards; tax exemptions | No statewide SREC market | 100% exempt | Exempt (<2 MW) | Active; utility blocks limited |
| Oregon | Energy Trust incentives + storage adder | No SREC market | — (no state sales tax) | Exempt | ETO active; ODOE rebate intermittent |

> All values above are illustrative of the **mechanism**, not guaranteed current pricing. Rates, blocks, caps, and enrollment windows change frequently. **Verify every figure at the official agency site and [DSIRE](https://www.dsireusa.org/) before relying on it.**
