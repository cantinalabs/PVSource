# Virginia Solar Incentives & Policy

Virginia offers **1:1 net metering** (recently reaffirmed by the State Corporation Commission), a **voluntary/limited SREC market** supported by the Virginia Clean Economy Act's RPS, a **100% local-option property-tax exemption**, a **sales-tax exemption** for certified solar equipment, and emerging **shared solar / community energy** programs under Dominion. This guide covers residential and commercial systems and where they diverge.

> **Note (as of 2026-06-30):** Virginia's net-metering rules (NEM 2.0), shared-solar rollout, and SREC pricing are actively evolving. Treat figures below as directional and **verify at the Virginia SCC, Virginia Department of Energy, and [DSIRE](https://www.dsireusa.org/)** before relying on them.

## At-a-Glance Summary

| Policy area | Residential | Commercial |
|---|---|---|
| Net metering | **1:1** bill offset (NEM 2.0 confirmed by SCC in 2026); minor monthly charge / low excess buyback | 1:1 within program caps; larger systems vary — *verify* |
| SREC market | **Voluntary/limited**; VCEA RPS creates utility demand (~$25–$33 recently) | Same market; more volume |
| State tax credit | None | None |
| Federal tax credit | §25D **ended after 12/31/2025** (OBBBA) | §48E ITC — see [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md) |
| Shared / community solar | **Shared Solar** & Dominion community program (rolling out) | Subscriber / host roles |
| Sales tax exemption | Yes — certified solar equipment | Yes (certified pollution-control classification) |
| Property tax exemption | **100% local-option** exemption for added value | Local-option; separate solar-facility assessment rules for large projects |

## Overview

Virginia's solar policy is shaped by the **Virginia Clean Economy Act (VCEA, 2020)**, which set **mandatory RPS** targets (Dominion to 100% renewable by 2045; Appalachian Power by 2050) and created utility demand that underpins the SREC market. Combined with **1:1 net metering**, a **sales-tax exemption**, a **100% local-option property-tax exemption**, and expanding **shared solar**, Virginia is a comparatively strong Southeast market. There is **no state income-tax credit**.

## Net Metering / Net Billing

- Virginia mandates **net metering**, and in **2026 the State Corporation Commission (SCC) reaffirmed** the framework (**NEM 2.0** for Dominion) after review — the 1:1 economics are **essentially preserved**.
- Under NEM 2.0, expect a **small fixed monthly charge** (on the order of ~$1/month reported) and **excess year-end generation** cashed out at a **low avoided-cost rate** (reported near **~$0.058/kWh**). **Verify current charges and buyback rate** with Dominion/SCC.
- **System-size and aggregate-cap** rules apply; **commercial** systems must confirm eligibility and any program caps.

See [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md) for how 1:1 net metering compares to net billing.

## SREC / RECs

- Virginia has an **SREC market**, but it is best described as **voluntary/limited** relative to mature Mid-Atlantic markets. The **VCEA RPS** creates **utility demand**, supporting pricing.
- **1 SREC = 1 MWh.** Owners generally **retain ownership** of their SRECs and may **register and sell** them; residential systems have recently traded around **~$25–$33** each (a few hundred dollars/year), **but pricing varies** — **verify current market rates**.
- **Commercial** systems generate more SRECs; model revenue against current market demand and RPS compliance dynamics.

For how Virginia compares to tradable compliance markets, see [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md).

## State Tax Credits

Virginia has **no state solar income-tax credit** for residential or commercial systems. Value comes from net metering, SRECs, tax exemptions, and (commercial) the federal ITC.

## Federal Tax Credits

- **Residential (§25D):** The 30% Residential Clean Energy Credit **ended after December 31, 2025** under **OBBBA**. Systems placed in service on/after **January 1, 2026** do **not** qualify.
- **Commercial (ITC / §48E):** The **Clean Electricity Investment Credit (§48E)** remains available for businesses/TPO systems, subject to **begin-construction** deadlines and phase-outs, and pairs with **MACRS depreciation**.

Details and timelines: [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md).

## Utility Incentives / Rebates

- **Dominion Energy** and **Appalachian Power** offer efficiency and program options that change periodically; there is **no large statewide cash rebate** for rooftop solar.
- **Shared Solar / Community Energy:** Virginia's **Shared Solar program** and the **2025 Community Energy Act** require Dominion to develop community-solar offerings. Dominion filed its pilot in **December 2025**, with customer enrollment expected to **ramp through late 2026**; **subscription credit amounts were not yet finalized** at the time of writing — **verify current status** with the SCC/Dominion.
- These programs let renters, low-income households, and businesses participate **without a rooftop installation**.

## Sales Tax Exemption

Virginia provides a **sales- and use-tax exemption for certified solar energy equipment** (classified under **certified pollution-control equipment**, effective since **January 1, 2017**, per Va. Code). This benefits **residential and commercial** purchasers; commercial/business-owned systems in particular rely on the certified-pollution-control classification. **Confirm current certification requirements** with the Virginia Department of Taxation.

## Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

- Under **Va. Code § 58.1-3661**, solar energy equipment is a **separate class of property**, and **localities may exempt up to 100%** of the **added value** of solar equipment from property tax. Many localities have adopted a **100% exemption** — but it is a **local option**, so **check your specific county/city**.
- **Large-scale/utility commercial projects** are subject to separate **SCC solar-facility assessment / revenue-share** rules (and possible local revenue-share ordinances) — model these for ground-mount and utility-scale projects.

**Verify local adoption** with your county/city and DSIRE.

## Permitting & Interconnection Notes

- Interconnection follows **SCC / utility interconnection rules**; small residential systems use expedited "Level 1"-style review.
- Local **AHJ permitting** varies by locality.
- SREC participation requires **registering the system** and using a **REC tracking** account (PJM-GATS) to sell into the market.

## Bottom Line

- **Residential:** **1:1 net metering (reaffirmed in 2026)** plus **sales-tax exemption**, widely adopted **100% local property-tax exemption**, and **modest SREC income** make Virginia attractive — though the **federal §25D credit is gone after 2025**. Watch NEM 2.0's small charges and low excess buyback.
- **Commercial:** **§48E ITC + MACRS**, net metering, SRECs (VCEA-supported), and tax exemptions drive economics; large ground-mount projects must model **solar-facility assessment/revenue-share** rules. **Shared solar** is expanding for non-rooftop participation.
