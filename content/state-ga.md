# Georgia Solar Incentives & Policy

Georgia is one of the **least incentive-rich states** for distributed solar: **no state RPS, no state solar tax credit, no SREC market, no statewide net-metering mandate**, and no statewide solar sales- or property-tax exemption. Distributed-solar economics are almost entirely **utility-driven**, and the dominant utility — Georgia Power — buys excess exports at **avoided cost**, well below retail. This guide covers residential and commercial solar as of mid-2026. Confirm live figures with the administering agency and the **Database of State Incentives for Renewables & Efficiency ([DSIRE](https://programs.dsireusa.org/))** before modeling a project.

For export-compensation mechanics, see [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md). For the federal side, see [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md). For comparisons, see [More State Programs](incentive-more-states.md) and [More State Programs (Round 2)](incentive-more-states-2.md).

## At-a-Glance Summary

| Policy Lever | Residential | Commercial |
|---|---|---|
| **Net metering** | Georgia Power **RNR-Instantaneous Netting** at avoided cost (~7.2¢/kWh); monthly-netting cap filled 2021 | Same RNR/avoided-cost framework |
| **SREC / REC market** | **None** (no RPS) | **None** |
| **State income-tax credit** | **None** (credit expired end of 2014) | **None** |
| **Utility rebate** | Community solar; storage/VPP pilots *(verify)*; some EMC rebates | VPP / commercial pilots *(verify)* |
| **Sales-tax exemption** | **None** for rooftop | Narrow manufacturing/economic-development carve-outs only |
| **Property tax** | **No statewide exemption** — local, county-by-county | Same (local) |

> **Bottom line up front:** In Georgia, most of the "incentive" value is federal, not state. New Georgia Power customers export excess at **avoided cost (~7.2¢/kWh)**, not retail, so sizing to on-site consumption matters more here than in net-metering states.

## Overview

**Georgia Power** (a Southern Company subsidiary) is the dominant investor-owned utility, regulated by the **Georgia Public Service Commission (PSC)**. Many customers are instead served by **electric membership cooperatives (EMCs)** or **municipal utilities**, each of which sets its own solar terms. There is **no statewide net-metering mandate and no RPS**, so compensation and policy vary sharply by provider.

## Net Metering / Net Billing

**No statewide net-metering mandate.** Under Georgia Power's **RNR-11 tariff** ("Renewable and Nonrenewable Resources"):

- **RNR-Monthly Netting** (the more favorable option — excess summed monthly, netted, remainder credited at avoided cost) was **capped at ~5,000 residential customers (~32 MW), which filled in 2021.** It is effectively closed to new customers.
- **RNR-Instantaneous Netting** is what **new customers get today.** Exports are netted instantaneously; excess is credited at the **Solar Avoided Cost Rate (≈3.22¢/kWh for 2026) plus a 4¢/kWh adder ≈ 7.2¢/kWh** — well below retail. Availability runs first-come, first-served until cumulative renewable capacity reaches **0.2% of the prior-year annual peak demand.** *Verify remaining headroom against the current RNR-11 tariff.*
- **Residential eligibility:** system ≤ 10 kW-AC. A **minimum basic service charge** applies every month.
- **2025 IRP:** Advocates pushed to expand/lift the monthly-netting cap; **no expansion of monthly netting was adopted.** Georgia Power indicated the program may be revisited in a future rate case / the 2028 IRP. *Verify against the final 2025 IRP order.*

**Commercial:** Same RNR/avoided-cost framework via the commercial tariff and PowerClerk interconnection, with size tiers (systems **>250 kW-AC** incur an added witness-test fee). Larger commercial and utility-scale procurement runs through separate RFP/PPA mechanisms, not net metering.

**EMCs & municipal utilities:** Policies vary — there is **no statewide EMC net-metering policy.** Each co-op sets its own buyback rate, size limits, and any rebates. For example, **Central Georgia EMC** offers a **$450/kW residential rebate** (systems ≤10 kW, max $4,500). *Confirm current terms with the specific co-op or municipal utility.*

> Because most new Georgia Power customers are on **instantaneous avoided-cost netting**, exports are worth roughly half of retail. See [Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures](grid-net-metering.md) for how this affects payback.

## SREC / RECs

**Georgia has no RPS and no SREC market.** There is no state compliance market creating tradable solar-credit value for residential or commercial owners. Owners may sell RECs into voluntary markets, but there is no Georgia-specific SREC value. Contrast with the markets in our [SREC Markets Deep-Dive](incentive-srec-markets.md).

## State Tax Credits

**None currently active.** The former **Clean Energy Property Tax Credit expired at the end of 2014** and was not renewed. Georgia Power's own FAQ states plainly that neither Georgia Power nor the State of Georgia currently offers incentives or rebates for residential solar installations.

## Federal Tax Credits

With no state credit, federal treatment drives Georgia economics:

- **Residential (§25D):** The federal Residential Clean Energy Credit **ended after December 31, 2025** under OBBBA. Systems placed in service in 2026 and later generally cannot claim it — and no Georgia state credit backfills it.
- **Commercial (ITC / §48E):** The technology-neutral Clean Electricity Investment Credit generally remains available for commercial and utility-scale projects, subject to phase-out schedules, wage/apprenticeship rules, and bonus adders. See [Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits](federal-tax-credits.md).

## Utility Incentives / Rebates

- **Georgia Power Community Solar** (subscription to shared solar — you don't own panels): standard residential ~**$24.00 per 1-kW block/month**; income-qualified rate ~**$6.00/block/month** (household ≤200% of Federal Poverty Guidelines). Limited availability; first-come, first-served. See our [Community Solar](residential-community-solar.md) overview.
- **Storage / VPP pilots (from the approved 2025 IRP):** a **Customer-Sited Solar Plus Storage Pilot** (~50 MW target) and a **Virtual Power Plant (VPP) pilot** (~50 MW) reportedly offering upfront incentives around **$750/kW** (standard) to **$1,000/kW** (low-to-moderate income / certain commercial). *Verify current enrollment status and exact levels — pilot details evolve.*
- **EMC rebates:** vary by co-op (e.g., Central Georgia EMC $450/kW).

## Sales Tax Exemption

**No specific residential/rooftop solar sales-tax exemption.** Homeowners pay the standard **4%** state sales tax plus local option taxes on equipment. Narrow exemptions exist for **energy used in manufacturing** and for certain utility-scale/economic-development projects, but these do **not** cover ordinary rooftop solar. *Verify scope; these are not general rooftop exemptions.*

## Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

**No mandatory statewide residential solar property-tax exemption.** Treatment is **local:** Georgia law permits individual cities/counties to grant an exemption for the added value of a solar system, but it is not uniform or guaranteed.

> **Homeowners and commercial owners must confirm treatment with their specific county tax assessor.** Sources conflict on how broadly local exemptions apply — verify locally.

## Permitting & Interconnection Notes

- **Georgia Power interconnection:** Applications run through **PowerClerk**; the interconnection agreement is between Georgia Power and the customer. Most systems require a **Witness Test**, after which a **Permission to Operate (PTO)** letter issues (following bidirectional meter reprogramming). Systems **>250 kW-AC** pay a separate witness-test fee. Grid-tied systems shut off during outages unless battery backup is installed.
- **Statewide rules:** No uniform statewide DER interconnection standard binds all utilities; Georgia Power operates under its own PSC-approved procedures, EMCs under theirs.
- **SolarAPP+ / streamlined permitting:** Adoption is **local and partial** (e.g., Athens-Clarke County expedited permitting; Atlanta SolarAPP+-related documentation). No statewide mandate. *Verify the specific jurisdiction.*

## Bottom Line

Georgia offers **very little state-level solar incentive**: no tax credit, no SREC market, no statewide net metering, and no statewide sales- or property-tax exemption. New Georgia Power customers export excess at **avoided cost (~7.2¢/kWh)**, so systems should be sized to on-site consumption. The best available value is often **community solar**, emerging **storage/VPP pilots**, occasional **EMC rebates**, and — critically — **federal** incentives (note §25D ended after 2025; commercial §48E largely survives). Always confirm your specific utility's buyback rate and your county's property-tax treatment before modeling.
