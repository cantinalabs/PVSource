Colorado Solar Incentives & Policy

Solar in Colorado — statewide net metering, sales- and property-tax exemptions, Xcel Solar*Rewards, a storage credit, and a strong community-solar market.

Net meteringYes
ExemptionsSales & property
XcelSolar*Rewards

Colorado is a strong solar state built on statewide net metering, full sales- and property-tax exemptions, active Xcel Solar*Rewards utility incentives, and one of the nation's most robust community solar markets. It has no general state income-tax credit for solar PV itself, but it does offer a residential battery-storage credit (closing at the end of 2026) and solar-adjacent heat-pump credits. This guide covers residential and commercial solar as of mid-2026. Confirm live figures with the administering agency and the Database of State Incentives for Renewables & Efficiency (DSIRE) before modeling a project.

For export-compensation mechanics, see Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures. For the federal side, see Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits. For comparisons, see More State Programs and More State Programs (Round 2).

Framing: Colorado's dependable value is the tax-exemption stack + net metering + utility incentives + community solar, not a state income-tax credit for panels. The battery-storage credit is a closing window (last year 2026).

Overview

Colorado's investor-owned utilities are Xcel Energy (Public Service Company of Colorado) and Black Hills Energy; cooperatives and municipal utilities set their own rules but must allow systems up to at least 120% of average annual consumption. Policy is set by the Colorado Public Utilities Commission (PUC) within DORA, with the Colorado Energy Office (CEO) and the Division of Property Taxation (DPT) administering programs and exemptions.

Net Metering / Net Billing

Statewide requirement: Colorado law requires IOUs to offer net metering; co-ops and munis must allow systems up to at least 120% of the customer's average annual consumption.

  • Xcel (residential): Full retail-rate credit for exports, rolling forward monthly, trued up annually. At true-up, remaining excess is cashed out at Xcel's much lower average hourly incremental cost (roughly $0.03–$0.05/kWh vs. ~$0.14–$0.17/kWh retail). No move to net billing has been adopted for residential as of mid-2026. Verify the exact residential credit rate and true-up mechanics; Xcel filed a ~10% rate increase for 2026.
  • Xcel (commercial): Net metering applies to larger commercial systems, historically sized around load with defined caps. Verify current commercial size limits against the Xcel/PUC tariff.
  • Black Hills Energy: Two-meter setup (production + net); excess banks as a rolling kWh credit to the next calendar year or can be paid out, at the customer's election.

Full retail-rate net metering is a meaningful advantage vs. avoided-cost states. See Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures.

SREC / RECs

Colorado's Renewable Energy Standard requires 30% renewables by 2020 for large IOUs (still the standing floor); 2019's SB 19-236 set an 80%-carbon-reduction-by-2030 trajectory toward 100% by 2050. There is no open SREC spot market — RECs are monetized through utility programs.

Xcel Solar*Rewards RECs: The program buys the RECs from your system's output. Residential performance-based incentive is small (~$0.005/kWh reported). Commercial "Medium" (25 kW–500 kW) pays around $0.0375/kWh; "Large" (>500 kW) is bid via RFP with no fixed REC price. Treat per-kWh figures as verify — amounts change per program year and can sell out.

State Tax Credits

  • No general state personal income-tax credit for solar PV.
  • Residential Energy Storage (battery) credit (HB22-1382): 10% of the qualifying basis (battery equipment, sales tax, freight; excludes labor/install/permit fees). Expires December 31, 2026 — 2026 is the last year. System must be placed in service before Jan. 1, 2027. Claimed via DR 1307; not refundable; can be assigned to the seller at purchase. Typical credit ~$1,200–$1,500 for a 13.5 kWh battery. No extension announced — treat as a closing window.
  • Heat pump / geothermal credit (HB23-1272): Active and independent of expired federal credits — for 2026, ~$1,000 air-source heat pump, ~$2,000 geothermal, ~$250 heat-pump water heater. Claimed by a CEO-registered contractor who must pass ≥33.33% to the customer. (Solar-adjacent, not solar PV.)

Federal Tax Credits

  • Residential (§25D): The federal Residential Clean Energy Credit ended after December 31, 2025 under OBBBA. Systems placed in service in 2026 and later generally cannot claim it. Installers report a 2026 market slowdown and a pivot toward leases/PPAs as a result.
  • Commercial (ITC / §48E): The technology-neutral Clean Electricity Investment Credit generally remains available for commercial and utility-scale projects, subject to phase-out schedules, wage/apprenticeship rules, and bonus adders. See Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits.

Utility Incentives / Rebates

  • Xcel Solar*Rewards (residential): Small per-kWh REC payment (~$0.005/kWh). Income-Qualified (IQ) and Disproportionately Impacted Community (DIC) customers can receive an upfront rebate of about $1/watt (up to 10 kW-AC). The 2026 cycle opened May 21, 2026. Verify current-year amounts/budget.
  • Xcel Solar*Rewards for Business (commercial): Medium (25–500 kW) ~$0.0375/kWh; Large (>500 kW) RFP-bid. Capacity allocated in tranches; can close mid-year.
  • Xcel storage: Battery/storage incentive programs operate; confirm current per-kWh/upfront terms. (Verify.)
  • Black Hills Energy solar: ~$1/watt rebate for 0.5–25 kW systems; ~3.8¢/kWh income-qualified incentive; ~$100/kW paired-storage rebate (cap ~$1,000/customer). Interconnection fees: $320 (≤10 kW), $620 (>10 kW–500 kW). Allocations released quarterly.

Community Solar (a Colorado strength)

Xcel's Solar*Rewards Community was historically the nation's largest community-solar program. Important change: legacy Solar*Rewards Community capacity closed December 31, 2025. Effective January 1, 2026, a new Inclusive Community Solar program (created by SB 24-207) took over — roughly 50 MW for Xcel and 3.5 MW for Black Hills, available from Jan. 31, 2026 and selectable during interconnection. Subscriptions cap at 120% of average annual usage; residential bill-credit value is roughly 79% of retail; residential termination fees are prohibited beginning 2026. See our Community Solar overview.

Sales Tax Exemption

Colorado provides a 100% state sales/use tax exemption for components used to produce AC electricity from renewable resources (solar), so the 2.9% state sales tax does not apply to qualifying equipment.

Local jurisdiction (city/county/special-district) sales taxes are not automatically exempt — treatment varies locally; some local governments offer their own credits/rebates. Verify with your municipality.

Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

Per the Colorado Division of Property Taxation:

  • Residential solar: Independently owned residential PV is exempt from property tax (§ 39-3-102, C.R.S.) where the system is on residential real property, produces electricity primarily for the residence, and has a capacity of no more than 100 kW-AC.
  • Commercial / larger systems: Solar facilities producing 2 MW or less of AC are locally assessed (favorable renewable personal-property treatment); systems greater than 2 MW are state-assessed as public-utility property. Verify the exact valuation/tax-factor result for a specific commercial system via DPT templates.
  • Community solar gardens: A specific exemption (§ 39-3-118.7) covered tax years 2015 through before Jan. 1, 2021 (that window has lapsed). Verify current community-solar property-tax treatment, especially under SB 24-207.

Permitting & Interconnection Notes

  • SB 21-261 directed the PUC to adopt DER aggregation/interconnection rules, approve customer-owned meter-collar adapters for residential installs, and allow solar-plus-storage sized up to 200% of projected future use.
  • Automated permitting: Colorado promotes automated permit processing (APPS grant program via the CEO) using SolarAPP+ or Symbium. Denver and Bennett have adopted SolarAPP+; Symbium opened to Colorado in April 2024. SolarAPP+ qualifies systems up to 38 kW. The specific statutory permitting-timeline mandate could not be confirmed — verify the exact requirement and enabling bill.

Bottom Line

Colorado stacks up well: full retail-rate net metering, a 100% state sales-tax exemption, a strong property-tax exemption (residential ≤100 kW-AC), Xcel/Black Hills Solar*Rewards incentives (with meaningful IQ/DIC adders at ~$1/W), and a leading community-solar market now running through the new Inclusive Community Solar program. The 10% battery-storage credit is a valuable but closing window (last year 2026). Note the federal §25D residential credit ended after 2025, so 2026 residential economics lean more on state/utility programs and third-party ownership.
