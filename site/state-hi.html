Hawaii Solar Incentives & Policy

Solar in Hawaii — the 35% state credit, the shift from net metering to self-supply and smart export, and the nation's highest electricity prices.

35% creditState
Self-supplySmart export
PricesHighest

Hawaii has the highest electricity prices in the United States (~42¢/kWh residential), which makes solar economics compelling even though the state closed traditional net metering in 2015. Value now comes from a 35% state income-tax credit (RETITC), a set of successor export/self-supply and battery programs run by Hawaiian Electric, and low-cost GEMS financing — all against a backdrop of active 2026 policy change. This guide summarizes the current landscape for both residential and commercial solar as of mid-2026. Programs change frequently — confirm live figures with the administering agency and the Database of State Incentives for Renewables & Efficiency (DSIRE) before modeling a project.

For background on how export compensation works, see Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures. For the federal side, see Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits. For cross-state comparisons, see More State Programs and the SREC Markets Deep-Dive.

Framing: Two big 2026 shifts: Act 24 overhauls the RETITC for 2027+ (with a $40M annual cap and a 2030 sunset), and Hawaiian Electric folded its old export tariffs into a new "Smart Renewable Energy" (Smart DER) framework. Several legacy program names are now closed to new customers.

Overview

Hawaiian Electric (HECO) serves Oahu, Maui County, and Hawaii Island; Kauai is served by the Kauai Island Utility Cooperative (KIUC). State policy runs through the Hawaii State Energy Office (HSEO), the Department of Taxation (DOTAX), the Public Utilities Commission (PUC), and the Hawaiʻi Green Infrastructure Authority (HGIA) for financing. The dominant economic driver is simply avoided cost: at ~42¢/kWh, self-consumed solar offsets extremely expensive grid power.

Net Metering / Net Billing

Traditional Net Energy Metering (NEM) closed to new customers in October 2015. Existing NEM customers are grandfathered.

  • The legacy successor tariffs — Customer Grid-Supply (CGS), CGS Plus, Smart Export, and Customer Self-Supply — are now closed to new enrollment (early 2024).
  • New HECO customers use the consolidated "Smart Renewable Energy" (Smart DER) framework, with two tracks:
  • Smart Renewable Energy Export — credits for energy exported to the grid.
  • Smart Renewable Energy Non-Export — no grid export; can include battery storage (often approved faster where circuits are constrained).
  • Legacy CGS/Smart Export customers transition into Smart Renewable Energy Export 7 years after original enrollment.
  • Community-Based Renewable Energy (CBRE / "Shared Solar") is active for renters and those who cannot install rooftop.

Because export capacity is circuit-limited under HECO's Rule 14H, non-export + battery configurations are common. Verify current program terms and circuit availability. See Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures.

SREC / RECs

Hawaii has no traditional SREC market. Its RPS — 100% renewable by 2045 (the nation's first such mandate) — has no solar carve-out, which is the mechanism that would create tradable SRECs. Solar is instead compensated through the tariff/export programs plus the RETITC. Contrast with the compliance markets in our SREC Markets Deep-Dive.

State Tax Credits

Renewable Energy Technologies Income Tax Credit (RETITC) — HRS §235-12.5. For 2026, the traditional structure applies:

Technology Credit Cap
Solar PV (residential, single-family) 35% of cost $5,000 per 5 kW system
Solar thermal water heating (residential) 35% $2,250 per system
Wind (residential) 20% $1,500 per system
Commercial solar 35% $500,000 per array

Major 2026 change — Act 24 (signed May 21, 2026): Beginning with tax years after December 31, 2026, the RETITC gains new eligibility/certification rules, an annual $40 million statewide aggregate cap, and a sunset in 2030. Executive Order 26-02 (June 2026) shields systems placed in service in calendar year 2026 from the aggregate cap where the taxpayer reasonably relied on the credit before Act 24. So the 35%/$5,000 structure holds for 2026 installs, but 2027+ rules are not yet finalized — HSEO guidance is pending. Verify before advising on 2027+.

Federal Tax Credits

Federal incentives are national, not Hawaii-specific, but they drive project economics:

  • Residential (§25D): The federal Residential Clean Energy Credit ended after December 31, 2025 under OBBBA. Systems placed in service in 2026 and later generally cannot claim §25D. Confirm with a tax professional.
  • Commercial (ITC / §48E): The technology-neutral Clean Electricity Investment Credit (§48E) generally remains available for commercial and utility-scale projects, subject to phase-out schedules, wage/apprenticeship rules, and bonus adders. See Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits.

Utility Incentives / Rebates

  • Battery Bonus — CLOSED. HECO's Battery Bonus program (Oahu and Maui) is fully closed; validation documents were no longer accepted after September 30, 2025.
  • Bring Your Own Device Plus (BYOD Plus) — launched May 15, 2025, is the current battery-incentive successor. It pays for a new battery plus a customer-selected daily 2-hour export window, with monthly export credits at the customer's retail rate. Verify current terms and any island-specific caps.

Sales Tax Exemption

Hawaii has no sales tax; instead it levies the General Excise Tax (GET) at a general rate of 4% plus county surcharges (effective ~4.0–4.712% depending on island). Solar installations and services are subject to GET — there is no statewide GET exemption for solar. (The federal residential credit, when it applied, was calculated on the full cost including GET.)

Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

Property tax is county-administered, so treatment varies:

  • Honolulu (City & County): Offers a Real Property Exemption for Alternative Energy Improvements — solar PV is exempt from assessment (commonly cited as a 25-year exemption), so adding solar does not raise assessed value.
  • Maui, Hawaii (Big Island), and Kauai counties: Comparable PV exemptions were not clearly confirmed — do not assume Honolulu's exemption applies statewide. Verify directly with each county Real Property Tax office and DSIRE.

Permitting & Interconnection Notes

  • New rooftop systems require a county building permit, then interconnection review under HECO/PUC. HECO's Rule 14H technical review applies to every application; on constrained circuits, export applications may be denied or require an expensive study, while non-export configurations are often approved faster.
  • "Quick Connect" (Oahu, Maui, Hawaii Island): on eligible circuits, systems ≤25 kW meeting Rule 14H can be energized before final utility approval, with review completed afterward — dramatically faster than the traditional wait.
  • GEMS financing: The Hawaiʻi Green Infrastructure Authority administers GEMS (Green Energy Market Securitization) low-cost financing, including an on-bill program (repayment tied to the electric meter, enabling renters to participate; minimums ~$5,000 residential / $50,000 commercial). A 2026 release of ~$18 million in new loan capital targets nonprofits, small business, and underserved ratepayers as federal credits phase out. Verify current availability.

Bottom Line

For Hawaii homeowners, the extraordinary ~42¢/kWh grid price is the core driver, paired with the 35% state credit (up to $5,000 per 5 kW system) and a non-export + battery configuration under Smart DER (traditional net metering is gone; Battery Bonus is closed; BYOD Plus is the current battery incentive). Note that §25D federal credit ended after 2025, and the RETITC itself changes materially in 2027 under Act 24. For commercial projects, the 35% state credit up to $500,000 per array applies alongside the federal §48E ITC and depreciation, plus GEMS financing for qualifying entities. Everything here is subject to GET, and property-tax relief depends on your county. Confirm the 2027 RETITC rules, current Smart DER/BYOD Plus terms, and your county's property-tax treatment before committing.
