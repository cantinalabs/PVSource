Illinois Shines & Commercial Solar

Illinois Shines (the Adjustable Block Program): how REC contracts work, 2025–26 prices and block capacities, and what commercial developers must navigate.

Overview

Illinois Shines (legislatively known as the Adjustable Block Program or ABP) is Illinois' primary state-administered solar incentive program. Established by the Future Energy Jobs Act (FEJA, 2016, P.A. 99-0906) and significantly expanded by the Climate and Equitable Jobs Act (CEJA, 2021, P.A. 102-0662), it provides financial incentives for solar development through Renewable Energy Credit (REC) contracts between Approved Vendors and utilities (ComEd, Ameren, MidAmerican).

Program Administrator: Illinois Power Agency (IPA) Program Year: June 1 – May 31 (annual cycle, new capacity released each June) Website: illinoisshines.com

Core Mechanism: REC Contracts

How It Works

  1. Solar project generates electricity → creates RECs (1 REC = 1 MWh generated)
  2. Approved Vendor (AV) contracts with IPA to sell RECs at fixed price
  3. Utilities (ComEd/Ameren/MidAmerican) purchase RECs to meet Illinois RPS requirements
  4. AV passes value to customer via reduced project cost, lease/PPA savings, or direct payment

Key Point

Illinois Shines does NOT pay customers directly. Incentives flow through Approved Vendors who monetize REC contracts and pass savings to customers.

Revenue Calculation Example

Commercial 500 kW DC system (Group B, >200-500 kW tier)
- REC Price: $53.40/REC
- Annual Production: ~650,000 kWh = 650 MWh = 650 RECs/year
- Annual REC Revenue: 650 × $53.40 = $34,710/year
- 15-Year Contract Value: ~$520,650 (nominal, no escalation)
- Present Value (5% disc.): ~$360,000

This REC value is monetized by the AV and passed to customer via reduced EPC cost or PPA rate.

Commercial Project Eligibility & Requirements

System Requirements

  • New construction or existing system (energized after June 1, 2017)
  • Interconnected to ComEd, Ameren, MidAmerican, or eligible muni/co-op
  • Net metering enrollment (supply-only net metering for post-1/1/2025 installs)
  • Qualified Person installation (per 220 ILCS 5/16-128A, 83 Ill. Adm. Code 468)

Commercial-Specific Requirements

Prevailing Wage (CEJA Requirement)

  • All Illinois Shines projects must pay prevailing wage (IL Dept. of Labor rates)
  • Applies to: AVs, subcontractors, installers
  • Certified payroll records required
  • Major cost adder for commercial EPC — budget accordingly

Minimum Equity Standard (MES)

  • EEC categories only: Growing % of workforce must be Equity Eligible Persons
  • Standard categories: No MES requirement, but demographic reporting required
  • AVs submit annual MES Compliance Plan (due ~June) and Year-End Report

Project Labor Agreement (PLA)

  • Community Solar >3 MW (CRGA 2025): PLA required
  • DG projects: No PLA requirement

REC Contract Structure (2025-26 Changes per CRGA)

Category Contract Type
Small DG Advance payment (lump sum at energization)
Public Schools DG Advance payment
Large DG Annual payments (15 years, per REC delivered)
All Community Solar Annual payments (15 years)

Advance Payment (Small DG/Public Schools): AV receives ~15 years of REC value upfront at energization — passes as upfront discount to customer. Improves project economics significantly for commercial <2 MW.

Application Process (Commercial EPC Workflow)

1. Become or Partner with an Approved Vendor (AV)

  • AV Application: illinoisshines.com/become-an-approved-vendor/
  • Requirements: Insurance, financial standing, consumer protection compliance, qualified staff
  • Single Project AV: Available for projects ≥100 kW (simplified path)
  • Most commercial EPCs partner with existing AVs rather than becoming one

2. Project Development & Application

4. Required Documentation

  • Single-line diagram, site plan, equipment specs
  • Interconnection agreement / proof of application
  • Net metering enrollment confirmation
  • Prevailing wage compliance plan
  • Certified payroll records (during construction)
  • Disclosure Form (customer-facing, AV provides)
  • MES Compliance Plan (if EEC category)

Net Metering in Illinois (Critical for DG Economics)

Supply-Only Net Metering (Post-1/1/2025 Installs)

  • Export credited at: Supply rate only (~$0.04-0.07/kWh)
  • Delivery charges: Paid on ALL consumption (no netting of delivery)
  • Result: Longer payback, lower export value vs. legacy net metering
  • Applies to: ComEd, Ameren, MidAmerican territories

Legacy Net Metering (Pre-1/1/2025)

  • Full retail rate netting (supply + delivery)
  • 1:1 kWh credit for exports
  • Grandfathered for systems interconnected before 1/1/2025

Municipal / Co-op Territories

  • Set own net metering policies
  • Often more favorable than investor-owned utilities
  • Check specific utility tariff

Consumer Protection & Disclosure Requirements

AV/Designee Obligations (Consumer Protection Handbook)

  • Standardized Disclosure Form for every customer
  • No deceptive marketing, no pressure tactics
  • Clear explanation of: REC ownership, net metering, savings estimates, contract terms
  • Complaint process: illinoisshines.com/consumer-complaints/

Standard Disclosure Form Includes:

  • Estimated vs. guaranteed production
  • REC price & contract term
  • Net metering rate & policy
  • Escalation assumptions
  • Termination / transfer provisions
  • Contact info for IPA/Program Admin

Equity Eligible Contractor (EEC) Program

EEC Certification

  • ≥51% owned by Equity Eligible Persons (EEP) OR
  • ≥51% workforce = EEP OR
  • Nonprofit/community-based org serving EEP communities
  • Certification via IPA: illinoisshines.com/equity-eligible-contractors/

EEC Benefits

  • Dedicated capacity blocks (DG + CS)
  • Advances of Capital: Upfront funding for project development (reduces working capital needs)
  • Higher REC prices (CDCS/EEC CS tiers)
  • MES Compliance: Workforce equity reporting

For Commercial EPCs: Partnering with or becoming an EEC unlocks dedicated capacity and advance capital.

Illinois Solar for All (ILSFA) — Low-Income Program

  • Separate but related program (also IPA-administered)
  • No upfront cost for income-qualified customers
  • Carve-outs: Single-family, multi-family, community solar, public facilities
  • Commercial angle: EPCs can bid on ILSFA projects (public housing, nonprofits, etc.)

Timing Apply early in Program Year (June) for Group A pricing & capacity
AV Strategy Partner with established AV (faster) vs. become Single Project AV (≥100 kW)

REC Revenue Group B DG: ~$42-53/REC → ~$30-45k/MW/year for 15 years

Common Pitfalls for Commercial EPCs

  1. Assuming Group A pricing → Most capacity fills at Group A; budget for Group B
  2. Ignoring prevailing wage → Major cost impact; non-compliance = contract termination
  3. Late interconnection application → Queue delays push past 18-month energization deadline
  4. Supply-only net metering modeling → Export value ~60-70% lower than legacy NEM
  5. Not verifying AV status → Only AVs can submit applications; verify on IPA list
  6. Missing MES reports (if EEC) → Annual compliance plan + year-end report mandatory
  7. Assuming REC price escalation → Prices fixed for 15 years; no inflation adjustment
  8. Overlooking advance payment (Small DG) → Can reduce customer cost by 20-30% upfront
