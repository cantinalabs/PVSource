New York Solar Incentives & Policy

Solar in New York — NY-Sun and the VDER value stack, the 25% ($5,000) state credit, the 15-year RPTL §487 property exemption, and the sales-tax exemption.

NY-Sun · VDERPrograms
25% / $5kState credit
§48715-yr exempt

New York remains one of the strongest solar markets in the United States, backed by a layered set of state programs: the NY-Sun upfront incentive (delivered through the MW Block structure), the Value of Distributed Energy Resources (VDER) "Value Stack" that has replaced traditional net metering for most projects, a 25% state income-tax credit for residential systems, and a 15-year property-tax exemption under RPTL §487. This page gives the full state picture and links out to the dedicated program deep-dive for the mechanics of the incentive itself.

For the mechanics of NY-Sun and the VDER Value Stack, see the dedicated page: New York — NY-Sun & VDER. This page covers the surrounding tax, permitting, and interconnection landscape and how the pieces fit together for residential and commercial projects.

Overview

New York's clean-energy policy is administered largely by the New York State Energy Research and Development Authority (NYSERDA) under the state's Climate Leadership and Community Protection Act (CLCPA) goals. The New York State Public Service Commission (PSC) sets the compensation rules — most importantly the transition from retail net metering to the VDER Value Stack.

2026 status flag: As of early 2026, standard-income NY-Sun MW Block allocations were closed or nearly exhausted in both the Con Edison and Upstate regions, with remaining rebates targeted at low-income (≤80% AMI) customers. Block availability changes frequently — verify current block status at NYSERDA NY-Sun and DSIRE.

Net Metering / Net Billing

New York has largely retired traditional retail net metering in favor of the VDER Value Stack, which compensates exported energy based on when and where it is delivered to the grid rather than at a flat retail rate.

  • Mass-market residential and small non-residential systems generally receive credits under the Value Stack or a hybrid arrangement. Many residential customers are billed under a Net Energy Metering + Customer Benefit Contribution (CBC) structure, where a small monthly per-kW CBC charge applies.
  • Commercial and larger projects are compensated through the full VDER Value Stack, which layers several components: Energy Value, Capacity Value, Environmental Value (tied to the REC/social cost of carbon), Demand Reduction Value (DRV), and Locational System Relief Value (LSRV).

Key point: Under VDER, the value of your exports depends on your utility zone, injection timing, and project type — it is not a simple 1:1 retail credit. Model it carefully. See Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures and the New York — NY-Sun & VDER deep-dive.

SREC / RECs

New York does not operate a tradable SREC spot market the way New Jersey, Massachusetts (legacy), or Maryland do.

  • The environmental attributes (RECs) from your system are effectively monetized through the NY-Sun incentive and the Environmental Value component of the VDER Value Stack rather than sold separately.
  • This means New York homeowners and businesses do not receive a separate annual SREC check — the REC value is bundled into the upfront/ongoing compensation.

For contrast with true certificate markets, see SREC Markets Deep-Dive.

State Tax Credits

New York offers one of the most valuable residential state solar income-tax credits in the country.

Feature Detail
Credit 25% of qualified solar-energy-system equipment expenditures
Cap $5,000 maximum
Applies to Purchased systems and leases/PPAs (credit based on payments, subject to rules)
Basis Net cost after the NY-Sun rebate is subtracted
Carryforward Unused credit may generally be carried forward (up to 5 years)
Form NYS Form IT-255

Bold takeaway: On a system after NY-Sun, the 25% credit up to $5,000 frequently offsets a large share of a residential owner's remaining out-of-pocket cost.

Commercial taxpayers do not use IT-255; the value case for businesses comes from the federal ITC and MACRS depreciation instead — see below and MACRS / Bonus Depreciation.

Verify the current credit percentage and cap at the NYS Department of Taxation and Finance and DSIRE before relying on it.

Federal Tax Credits

Residential (§25D): The 25% federal Residential Clean Energy Credit ended for systems placed in service after December 31, 2025 under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA). Cash and loan residential purchases in 2026 receive no §25D credit. This is a federal change and applies in New York as everywhere else.

  • Commercial / business systems continue to be eligible for the federal investment tax credit under §48 / the tech-neutral §48E, subject to the OBBBA timelines, plus bonus/accelerated depreciation (MACRS). Commercial projects may also qualify for domestic-content, energy-community, and low-income adders, and can use transferability or direct pay to monetize credits.

See the dedicated federal pages: Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits · MACRS / Bonus Depreciation · Domestic Content Adder · Energy Communities · Direct Pay · Transferability.

Utility Incentives / Rebates

The primary utility-linked incentive is the NY-Sun MW Block rebate, paid as a declining $/W incentive that steps down as each regional "block" of capacity fills.

  • Residential: Historically strongest in the Con Edison and Upstate regions; standard-income blocks are largely closed as of 2026, with low-income (Affordable Solar / EmPower+) adders remaining.
  • Commercial / non-residential and community solar: Have their own MW Block allocations and adder structures.

Utilities involved include Con Edison, National Grid, NYSEG, RG&E, Central Hudson, and Orange & Rockland.

Verify current block $/W values and remaining capacity at NYSERDA NY-Sun — these change block-by-block.

Sales Tax Exemption

New York provides a state sales-and-use tax exemption for residential solar-energy-system equipment, and permits local jurisdictions to also exempt the local portion of sales tax (many, including New York City, have opted in).

  • Residential: Exempt from the 4% state sales tax; local exemption depends on your jurisdiction.
  • Commercial: A commercial solar sales-tax exemption is also available under state law (with local opt-in provisions).

Verify local opt-in status for your county/city at the NYS Department of Taxation and Finance and DSIRE.

Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

New York's flagship property-tax benefit is Real Property Tax Law (RPTL) §487.

Feature Detail
Benefit Exemption from the increase in assessed value attributable to an eligible solar/wind/storage system
Term 15 years
Applies to Residential, commercial, institutional, industrial
Application Form RP-487, filed with the local assessor
Deadline On or before taxable status date (e.g., January 5 in New York City) for the following tax year
Local opt-out Municipalities/counties/school districts may opt out of §487 — confirm your locality participates

Important: RPTL §487 is not universal — some localities have opted out or negotiate a PILOT (Payment In Lieu Of Taxes) for larger commercial/utility projects instead. Confirm with your local assessor.

New York City additionally offers a Solar Electric Generating System Property Tax Abatement (PTA) — a multi-year abatement of NYC real-property tax based on system cost — for qualifying systems. Verify the current PTA percentage and window at the NYC Department of Finance.

Permitting & Interconnection notes

  • Unified Solar Permit: New York promotes a standardized "NY Unified Solar Permit" adopted by many municipalities to streamline residential/small-commercial permitting and reduce soft costs. See Residential Permitting and Commercial Permitting.
  • Interconnection: Governed by the NY Standardized Interconnection Requirements (SIR) administered through the Joint Utilities' interconnection portal. Larger commercial systems may trigger CESIR (Coordinated Electric System Interconnection Review) studies and cost allocations. See Grid Studies & Queues and IEEE 1547.
  • Storage: NY has active storage targets; interconnection of paired storage follows SIR plus storage-specific requirements — see Storage Interconnection.

Bottom line

New York offers a deep but complex incentive stack. For residential owners, the 25% / $5,000 state credit plus NY-Sun (where blocks remain) and the §487 property-tax exemption are the headline benefits — but with §25D federal credit gone after 2025, timing and total economics have shifted. For commercial projects, the value case now rests on the federal §48E ITC, MACRS depreciation, adders, and VDER Value Stack compensation rather than a flat net-metering credit. The single most important step is to model VDER for your specific utility zone and confirm NY-Sun block availability before committing.
