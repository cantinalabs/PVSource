Use your site's ASHRAE extreme-minimum design temperature. Also confirm the string's cold Voc stays within the inverter and conductor/OCPD ratings. Min string length (MPPT start) is a separate check.

Voltage Drop

One-way run, uncoated copper. Vd = k × I × R × L ÷ 1000 (k = 2 for 1-phase, √3 for 3-phase)

Common targets: ≤ 2% on feeders, ≤ 3% total. Copper resistance at ~75°C; aluminum and conduit temperature will differ — verify per NEC Chapter 9.

Inverter loading ratio. ILR = DC kW ÷ AC kW

Typical residential 1.1–1.3; utility fixed-tilt often 1.3+. Higher ratios recover more morning/evening energy but clip more at midday.

Inter-Row Spacing & GCR

Minimum row pitch to avoid shading at a worst-case solar elevation. Pitch = L·cos(tilt) + L·sin(tilt)/tan(elev)

Worst-case elevation ≈ 90 − latitude − 23.5 (winter-solstice noon), or use your 9am design angle. Azimuth offsets and terrain change this — model for final design.
