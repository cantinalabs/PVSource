Illinois Solar Incentives & Policy

Solar in Illinois — the Illinois Shines SREC market, net metering, a sales-tax exemption, and the special solar property-tax assessment.

Illinois ShinesSREC
Net meteringYes
PropertySpecial assessment

Illinois runs one of the most generous distributed-solar programs in the Midwest through Illinois Shines (the Adjustable Block Program / ABP), which monetizes SRECs as up-front payments, alongside Illinois Solar for All for income-eligible households, a statewide sales-tax exemption, and a special property-tax assessment that protects owners from tax increases. This guide covers residential and commercial systems and flags where they differ.

Note (as of 2026-06-30): Illinois REC block prices, program budgets, and net-metering rules have changed recently. Treat all figures below as directional and verify at Illinois Shines, the Illinois Power Agency, and DSIRE before relying on them.

Overview

Illinois solar economics are anchored by Illinois Shines, which pays owners the value of their Solar Renewable Energy Credits (SRECs) as a large up-front lump sum representing roughly 15 years of expected production, rather than annual open-market sales. This is administered by the Illinois Power Agency (IPA). For a detailed walkthrough of program blocks, categories, and commercial participation, see the dedicated page: Illinois Shines & Commercial Solar.

The other pillars are net metering, Illinois Solar for All (income-qualified), a sales-tax exemption, and a favorable special property-tax assessment.

Net Metering / Net Billing

Illinois net metering changed materially:

  • Systems interconnected on or after January 1, 2025 receive supply-only net metering — bill credits apply to the supply (energy) portion of the bill, not the delivery/transmission charges or fixed fees. This is less valuable than legacy full net metering.
  • Systems interconnected before January 1, 2025 are grandfathered into the prior full-retail net metering for the life of the system (for ComEd, Ameren, MidAmerican customers).
  • Commercial customers follow the same supply-only structure; larger systems and specific tariffs should be confirmed with the utility.

The shift to supply-only net billing makes the Illinois Shines up-front SREC payment an even more central part of the value stack. See Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures for how supply-only credit differs from full 1:1.

SREC / RECs

Illinois SRECs are monetized through Illinois Shines / ABP:

  • One REC = 1 MWh of generation; the program pre-pays the estimated value of ~15 years of RECs.
  • Prices vary by block, group (A/B), and system-size category. Reported 2025–26 distributed-generation REC prices ran roughly $57–$76 per REC for small residential/small-commercial tiers (values differ by group and category — verify current block pricing).
  • Larger commercial, community solar, and public/nonprofit projects fall into distinct categories with their own pricing and waitlists.

Full mechanics, block status, and commercial categories: Illinois Shines & Commercial Solar. For how Illinois compares to Mid-Atlantic tradable markets, see SREC Markets Deep-Dive.

State Tax Credits

Illinois has no state solar income-tax credit for residential or commercial systems. The value is delivered through Illinois Shines, tax exemptions, and (commercial) the federal ITC.

Federal Tax Credits

  • Residential (§25D): The 30% Residential Clean Energy Credit ended after December 31, 2025 under OBBBA. Installs placed in service on/after January 1, 2026 do not qualify.
  • Commercial (ITC / §48E): The Clean Electricity Investment Credit (§48E) remains available for businesses and third-party-owned (TPO) systems, subject to begin-construction deadlines and phase-outs. Many commercial projects target the begin-construction window to lock in the full credit, and pair it with MACRS depreciation.

Details, deadlines, and depreciation interaction: Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits.

Utility Incentives / Rebates & Illinois Solar for All

  • Illinois Solar for All (ILSFA): A state program for income-eligible households (commonly ≤80% Area Median Income) offering $0 upfront cost and guaranteed bill savings, plus tracks for nonprofits and public facilities. This is separate from Illinois Shines but part of the same policy framework.
  • Utilities (ComEd, Ameren) run energy-efficiency and occasional storage/demand programs; check current offerings.

Sales Tax Exemption

Solar energy equipment is exempt from Illinois state sales tax, applicable to residential and commercial purchases. Confirm scope (including storage) with the Illinois Department of Revenue.

Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

Illinois uses a special property-tax assessment for solar energy systems: qualifying owners can elect an assessment method so the added value of the solar system is effectively not taxed (the property is assessed as if the system were a conventional energy system). This applies to residential and commercial property. The owner generally must claim the special assessment. Verify the current filing procedure with the local assessor and IPA/DSIRE.

Permitting & Interconnection Notes

  • Interconnection runs through the utility (ComEd, Ameren, MidAmerican) under Illinois interconnection rules; smaller residential systems use expedited review.
  • Illinois Shines participation requires working with an Approved Vendor and meeting program documentation and consumer-protection requirements.
  • Local AHJ permitting applies and varies by municipality.

Bottom Line

  • Residential: The up-front Illinois Shines SREC payment is the headline incentive and partly offsets the shift to supply-only net metering and the loss of the federal §25D credit after 2025. Income-eligible households should look at Illinois Solar for All.
  • Commercial: Illinois Shines (larger DG + community categories) + §48E ITC + MACRS + special property-tax assessment + sales-tax exemption make Illinois strong — but watch block availability, waitlists, and begin-construction deadlines.
