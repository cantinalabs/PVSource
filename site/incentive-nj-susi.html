New Jersey — SuSI / SREC-II

New Jersey's successor incentive — the SuSI program's Administratively Determined vs Competitive tracks, SREC-II values and 15-year term, registration and eligibility.

SuSIADI vs CSI
SREC-II15-yr term
NJBPURegistration

The Successor Solar Incentive (SuSI) program is New Jersey's current solar incentive framework. It replaced the legacy SREC and Transition Incentive (TI) programs after the state hit its statutory solar milestone, and it pays projects in SREC-IIs — a fixed-value certificate (1 SREC-II per 1,000 kWh / 1 MWh generated) earned over a 15-year term. SuSI splits the market into two tracks, and commercial developers typically stack it with the Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits and any State Tax Credits & Incentives.

Program administrator: New Jersey Board of Public Utilities (NJBPU), with registration/administration through New Jersey's Clean Energy Program (NJCEP). Official sites: NJCEP — Solar · NJ Competitive Solar Incentive (CSI)

Two Tracks: ADI vs. CSI

Key point: SuSI is an umbrella with two distinct incentive pathways — which one a project uses is determined by size and type, not by choice.

Track Who it covers How the SREC-II value is set
ADI — Administratively Determined Incentive Residential; net-metered non-residential ≤ 5 MW; community solar Fixed SREC-II value set administratively by the NJBPU ($/MWh), locked at registration
CSI — Competitive Solar Incentive Grid-supply solar; net-metered non-residential > 5 MW; grid-supply + storage SREC-II value bid competitively in periodic NJBPU solicitations, subject to confidential price caps

For most rooftop and behind-the-meter commercial projects (≤ 5 MW), the relevant track is ADI. Large grid-supply and >5 MW non-residential projects fall under CSI.

ADI — Administratively Determined Incentive

How it works

A qualifying system registers with the NJBPU and is assigned a fixed SREC-II value in $/MWh. The project then earns one SREC-II per MWh generated, at that locked value, for 15 years.

Rate locks at registration. The SREC-II value is fixed on your registration date and cannot be reduced afterward for the life of the 15-year term — but the administratively set rate for new registrants steps down over time, so registration timing matters.

ADI market segments

The ADI program covers three segments, each with its own SREC-II value:

Segment Eligibility
Net-metered residential Behind-the-meter residential
Net-metered non-residential (≤ 5 MW) Commercial, industrial, institutional rooftop/ground behind-the-meter
Community solar Subscriber-based projects under the NJ Community Solar Energy Program (CSEP)

Confirm the live rate before pricing at the NJCEP Solar page and the relevant NJBPU board order.

Sizing & eligibility

  • ADI ceiling: net-metered systems up to 5 MW.
  • Above 5 MW non-residential or any grid-supply project → CSI (below), not ADI.
  • Must be interconnected in NJ and meet NJBPU registration/consumer-protection requirements.

CSI — Competitive Solar Incentive

The CSI program serves grid-supply solar (selling into wholesale markets), net-metered non-residential projects > 5 MW, and grid-supply + energy storage. Instead of a fixed administrative rate, developers bid an SREC-II price ($/MWh) into periodic NJBPU solicitations, evaluated against confidential price caps.

How CSI awards work: The NJBPU prequalifies projects, then awards SREC-II contracts to bids at or below confidential per-tranche price caps (with limited discretion up to ~10% above cap). Bids above the caps are typically rejected. Example: the third CSI solicitation (bids closed Sept 30, 2025; awards announced March 4, 2026) drew 18 bids totaling ~143.63 MW but awarded only ~24.12 MW across three projects, because most bids exceeded the caps.

VERIFY current solicitation: CSI runs in discrete rounds with their own schedules, tranches, and caps. Check the CSI program site for the open solicitation, eligibility tranches, and current price-cap structure.

Registration (NJBPU)

Design → Interconnection application → SuSI registration (NJBPU/NJCEP)
      → Reserve incentive / lock SREC-II value (ADI) or win solicitation (CSI)
      → Build → Energize → Begin earning SREC-IIs (15-year term)
  • ADI: the installer/registrant submits the SuSI registration to the NJBPU; the SREC-II value locks on the registration date. (Practical turnaround is commonly cited at a few weeks.)
  • CSI: the developer prequalifies and submits a competitive bid during an open solicitation window.

Net Metering

New Jersey retains net metering for behind-the-meter systems, and it operates alongside the SuSI incentive:

Two stacked revenue streams. A net-metered commercial project (a) offsets its bill via net metering and (b) separately earns SREC-IIs on its production for 15 years. The SREC-II is a production incentive, not a substitute for net-metering bill savings.

  • Net-metering eligibility/credit terms depend on system size and the customer's utility tariff — confirm with the serving EDC (PSE&G, JCP&L, Atlantic City Electric, Rockland Electric).
  • Grid-supply / CSI projects export to the wholesale market rather than net-metering behind a retail meter.

How a Commercial Project Monetizes SuSI

Net metering:   Offsets the utility bill (behind-the-meter)
SREC-II:        1 SREC-II per MWh × fixed $/MWh × 15 years (ADI) or bid value (CSI)
Federal:        ITC + bonus depreciation  →  see federal-tax-credits.md

Illustrative ADI math (verify the rate): a 1 MW-DC commercial array producing ~1,300 MWh/year earns ~1,300 SREC-IIs/year; at an administratively set value of, say, $X/MWh, that is 1,300 × $X per year for 15 years — plus net-metering savings, plus the federal ITC and depreciation. Use the current non-residential ADI value, not the residential figure.

Commercial Cheat Sheet — New Jersey

Decision point Guidance
Which track? ≤ 5 MW net-metered or community → ADI (fixed value). > 5 MW non-residential or grid-supply → CSI (competitive bid).
Value basis ADI = administratively set $/MWh, locked at registration for 15 yrs. CSI = your bid vs. confidential caps.
Timing (ADI) Administrative rate steps down over time; register before the next reduction to lock a higher value.
Term 15 years of SREC-IIs (1 SREC-II = 1 MWh).
Net metering Runs alongside SREC-IIs for behind-the-meter projects.
Registration Via NJBPU / NJCEP (ADI) or via competitive solicitation (CSI).
Stacking SuSI + net metering + federal ITC/depreciation.
