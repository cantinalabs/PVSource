The “Big Beautiful Bill” (OBBBA)

How the One Big Beautiful Bill Act accelerates the phaseout of clean-energy credits for wind and solar, the new begin-construction deadlines, and the FEOC supply-chain rules.

Jul 4, 2026Begin-construction deadline
Dec 31, 2027Credit termination (PIS)
FEOCNew supply-chain rules

Overview

Official Name: One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA) — H.R. 1, Public Law 119-21 Enacted: July 4, 2025 (signed by President Trump) Primary Impact: Accelerated phaseout/termination of IRA clean energy tax credits for wind and solar, new Foreign Entity of Concern (FEOC) restrictions, and modifications to domestic content rules.

Bottom Line for Commercial Solar: The technology-neutral credits (Section 48E ITC / Section 45Y PTC) for wind and solar are terminated for projects placed in service after Dec 31, 2027, unless construction began by July 4, 2026. Pre-IRA credits (Section 48 ITC / Section 45 PTC) for projects beginning construction before Jan 1, 2025 are largely unaffected.

Key Provisions Affecting Commercial Solar

1. Termination of Section 48E ITC & Section 45Y PTC for Wind & Solar

Provision Pre-OBBBA (IRA) Post-OBBBA
Credit Type Technology-neutral (zero-emission) Same, but terminated for wind/solar
Phaseout Trigger 2034 or 75% sector emissions reduction Dec 31, 2027 placed-in-service deadline
Exception 4-year continuity safe harbor Construction began ≤ July 4, 2026

IRS Notice 2025-42 (Aug 2025) — Beginning of Construction Rules

  • Five Percent Safe Harbor ELIMINATED for wind/solar for OBBBA BOC deadline
  • Only Physical Work Test qualifies for July 4, 2026 deadline
  • Continuity Requirement: Must maintain continuous construction program
  • Continuity Safe Harbor: 4 calendar years from BOC to placed-in-service
  • Effective: Facilities where construction did NOT begin under old rules before Sept 2, 2025
  • Small Project Exception (≤1.5 MW AC): 5% Safe Harbor still available

Practical Impact: Commercial solar developers must perform physical work of significant nature (not just incur costs) by July 4, 2026 to qualify for Section 48E/45Y.

2. Pre-IRA Credits (Section 48 ITC / Section 45 PTC) — Largely Preserved

Credit Status Under OBBBA
Section 48 ITC (solar, storage, etc.) Unchanged for construction beginning before Jan 1, 2025
Section 45 PTC (wind, solar) Unchanged for construction beginning before Jan 1, 2025
5-Year MACRS Preserved (House/Senate proposals to eliminate were dropped)
Bonus Depreciation Unchanged (80% in 2025, 60% in 2026, 40% in 2027, 20% in 2028)

Strategic Window: Projects that began construction in 2024 (or earlier) under Section 48/45 are grandfathered and unaffected by OBBBA termination provisions.

3. Foreign Entity of Concern (FEOC) Rules — Broad New Restrictions

Three interlocking prohibitions apply to Section 48E, 45Y, 45X, 48C, and others:

A. Specified Foreign Entity (SFE) / Foreign-Influenced Entity (FIE) Prohibition

Category Definition
SFE Governments of China, Iran, Russia, North Korea; their citizens/nationals; entities organized in or with principal place of business in those countries; >50% owned subsidiaries
FIE Entity where SFE: (i) appoints covered officers, (ii) owns ≥25% equity (or ≥40% aggregate), (iii) holds ≥15% debt, (iv) receives "effective control" payments

Effect: No credits allowed to SFEs, FIEs, or facilities they construct.

B. Effective Control Rule

  • Prohibits payments to SFEs under contracts giving SFE "specific authority over key aspects" of energy generation
  • IP Licenses: Any license entered after July 4, 2025 = deemed effective control (except bona fide sales)
  • Oftake/Data/Operations contracts scrutinized

C. Material Assistance Rule

  • Facility cannot incorporate manufactured products where SFE/FIE content exceeds threshold
  • Threshold varies by credit type and construction start year
  • Interim Safe Harbor (until Dec 31, 2026): Rely on supplier certifications + IRS Notice 2025-08 domestic content tables
  • Penalties: Stricter penalties + extended statute of limitations for violations

FEOC Effective Dates

Rule Effective For
SFE/FIE Prohibition Tax years beginning after July 4, 2025
Effective Control Tax years beginning after July 4, 2025
Material Assistance Construction beginning after Dec 31, 2025

Supply Chain Impact: Commercial solar EPCs must audit supply chains for Chinese-origin content (modules, inverters, racking, batteries, critical minerals). Target: ≥40-55% domestic content depending on year.

Construction Begins Domestic Content Threshold (General) Offshore Wind

Note: OBBBA corrected IRA drafting error — Section 48E thresholds now align with Section 45Y.

5. Technologies Unaffected / Extended (Continue Under Section 48E/45Y)

Technology Phaseout Timeline
Energy Storage (standalone) 2034+ (original IRA schedule)
Nuclear (advanced) 2034+; New 10% energy community adder
Hydropower / Marine / Hydrokinetic 2034+
Geothermal Heat Pumps Construction through 2034
Fuel Cells (qualified) New: 30% ITC w/o PWA requirements (post-2025 construction); no adders
Other Zero-Emission 2034+

6. Transferability & Direct Pay — Retained

Provision Status
Credit Transferability (Section 6418) Retained — but transfers to FEOCs prohibited
Direct Pay / Elective Payment (Section 6417) Retained — tax-exempt entities can still monetize
Carryforward Unchanged (20 years)

7. Section 45Z Clean Fuel Production Credit — Extended

  • Extended through 2029 (was expiring 2027)
  • US/Canada/Mexico feedstock only
  • Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF) credit rate reduced
  • Small Producer Biodiesel credit extended through 2026, rate increased

8. Section 48C Advanced Energy Manufacturing Credit

  • FEOC restrictions apply
  • Material assistance rules apply
  • $10B total allocation (IRA) — competitive awards continue

Physical Work Test — What Qualifies (Per Notice 2025-42 / Notice 2013-29)

Qualifying Physical Work Likely NOT Qualifying
Site grading/clearing for array Land purchase/option payments
Pile driving / foundation installation Permitting/engineering fees
Racking installation Interconnection application fees
Module/inverter delivery to site (if binding contract) Developer fees
Trenching for electrical "Paper" expenditures
Substation/switchgear construction
Binding contracts for above work (pre-manufacture)

Key: Work must be of a significant nature and integral to the facility. No minimum dollar threshold, but must be more than "preparatory."

Conclusion: Loss of Section 48E dramatically changes project economics. Most commercial solar PPAs/leases become unviable without federal credit unless state incentives (IL Shines, CA SGIP, NY VDER) or high retail rates compensate.

Summary: What Commercial Solar EPCs Need to Know

Topic Key Takeaway
Section 48E ITC for Solar Dead for new starts after July 4, 2026; must PIS by 2027 (or 2030 if BOC by 7/4/2026)
Section 48 ITC (Pre-IRA) Still available for construction started before Jan 1, 2025
Physical Work Test Only way to establish BOC for 48E — no 5% safe harbor
FEOC / China Supply Chain Major new compliance burden — audit modules, inverters, batteries, steel
Domestic Content 50% (2026), 55% (2027+) for bonus — harder to achieve with FEOC rules
Storage / Nuclear / Other Unaffected — continue under 48E/45Y with 2034+ phaseout
Transferability / Direct Pay Retained — but no transfers to FEOCs
MACRS / Bonus Depreciation Preserved — 5-year MACRS + bonus depreciation unchanged
Immediate Action Start physical work by July 4, 2026 or pivot to storage/other tech
