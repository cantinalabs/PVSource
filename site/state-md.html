Maryland Solar Incentives & Policy

Solar in Maryland — a tradable SREC market, residential and commercial rebates and grants, net metering, and sales- and property-tax exemptions.

SRECTradable
Rebates$1,000+
ExemptionsSales & property

Maryland combines a tradable SREC market with a residential rebate/grant, commercial and canopy solar grants, full retail net metering, a 100% sales-tax exemption, and a property-tax exemption — a broad, business-friendly package administered by the Maryland Energy Administration (MEA). This page gives the full state picture and links to the site's certificate-market deep-dive for SREC mechanics.

For how tradable SREC markets work in detail, see: SREC Markets Deep-Dive. Maryland is one of the states that still operates a live SREC certificate market, so that page complements this one.

Overview

Maryland's solar programs are administered primarily by the Maryland Energy Administration (MEA), with net-metering and interconnection rules under the Maryland Public Service Commission (PSC). Maryland's Renewable Portfolio Standard (RPS) carries a solar carve-out, which sustains a tradable SREC market — distinguishing it from NY (VDER) and MA (SMART).

2026 status flag: MEA program budgets, per-fiscal-year funding, and SREC prices change annually and can exhaust mid-year. The Residential Clean Energy Rebate (flat $1,000) and the low-/moderate-income Maryland Solar Access Program (MSAP) are administered separately. Verify current programs, funding, and SREC prices at MEA and DSIRE.

Net Metering / Net Billing

Maryland maintains full retail-rate net metering.

  • Residential and commercial systems earn a full retail credit (~$0.15–$0.16/kWh, utility-dependent) for exported energy.
  • Utilities: BGE, Pepco, Delmarva Power, and Potomac Edison.
  • Credits roll over with an annual true-up; excess is reconciled per PSC rules.

Key point: Maryland's retail net metering plus a tradable SREC stream means owners can earn on both the energy value (net metering) and the environmental attribute (SREC) — a distinctive double stream. See Net Metering, Net Billing & Rate Structures.

SREC / RECs

Maryland operates an active, tradable SREC market tied to its RPS solar carve-out.

Feature Detail
Certificate 1 SREC = 1 MWh of solar generation
Value Market-driven — reported roughly $60–$90 per SREC (verify; volatile)
Certified SRECs Systems ≤5 MW, placed in service 7/1/2024–1/1/2028, on rooftop/canopy/eligible sites earn 1.5× (Certified) SRECs
Who earns Residential and commercial owners
How sold Via aggregators/brokers or an SREC platform

Bold takeaway: The 1.5× Certified SREC multiplier is a significant boost for qualifying rooftop and canopy systems placed in service before 2028 — confirm eligibility and the current market price.

Because prices are market-driven and volatile, owners often sell through an aggregator for price certainty. See SREC Markets Deep-Dive.

State Tax Credits

Maryland does not offer a general state solar income-tax credit for the PV system itself. Instead, the state uses direct rebates/grants (below) and the SREC market.

  • A separate energy-storage income-tax credit has existed in Maryland in prior years (subject to annual funding caps) — verify current-year availability and cap at MEA and the Comptroller of Maryland.

Bold takeaway: Don't budget for a Maryland income-tax credit on the panels themselves — value comes from the $1,000 rebate, SRECs, net metering, and exemptions (plus a storage credit if funded).

Federal Tax Credits

Residential (§25D): The 25% federal Residential Clean Energy Credit ended for systems placed in service after December 31, 2025 under OBBBA. Cash and loan residential purchases in 2026 receive no §25D credit in Maryland.

  • Commercial / business systems remain eligible for the federal ITC under §48 / §48E, plus MACRS accelerated depreciation and potential domestic-content, energy-community, and low-income adders, with transferability and direct-pay monetization options.

See: Commercial Solar Federal Tax Credits · MACRS / Bonus Depreciation · Domestic Content Adder · Energy Communities · Direct Pay · Transferability.

Utility Incentives / Rebates

Maryland's rebates/grants run through MEA, not standalone utility rebates:

  • Residential Clean Energy Rebate: A flat $1,000 grant for qualifying residential PV systems (subject to annual funding).
  • Maryland Solar Access Program (MSAP): Grants of $750/kW up to $7,500 for income-eligible homeowners (≤150% of statewide median income; owner-occupied primary residence; MEA-approved contractor).
  • Commercial / Canopy Solar Program (CCSP): MEA's commercial and canopy solar grant (reported ~$13 million available in FY26) supporting nonprofits, small businesses, veteran-owned entities, and eligible facilities for rooftop, ground-mount, and canopy projects. Note: the older Commercial Clean Energy Rebate Program (C-CERP) solar portion has been replaced by newer commercial solar offerings — verify the current program name and status.
  • Resilient Maryland / Energy Storage (RCES): Storage incentives up to $5,000 (residential) and $150,000 (commercial).

Verify current-fiscal-year programs, funding availability, and eligibility at MEA — Incentives. Programs frequently exhaust funding mid-year.

Sales Tax Exemption

Maryland provides a 100% sales-and-use tax exemption for solar equipment.

  • Residential and commercial purchases are fully exempt from the 6% state sales tax.
  • Covers solar panels, inverters, racking, and qualifying components.

Verify eligible equipment at the Comptroller of Maryland and DSIRE.

Property Tax Exemption / Abatement

Maryland exempts the added value of a solar-energy system from state and local property tax.

Feature Detail
Benefit Solar system does not increase the property's assessed value for tax
Applies to Residential and commercial
Coverage State property tax; local/county participation applies
  • Several counties (e.g., Anne Arundel, Baltimore, Harford, Prince George's) offer additional local property-tax credits for solar/energy-conservation devices — check with your county tax authority.

Key point: The base state exemption prevents solar from raising your assessment; county-level credits can add further savings. Confirm county specifics locally.

Permitting & Interconnection notes

  • Permitting: Municipal/county building and electrical permits apply under the Maryland building/electrical code. See Residential Permitting and Commercial Permitting.
  • Interconnection: Governed by Maryland PSC net-metering and interconnection rules (COMAR), with tiered review by system size. Larger commercial systems may require studies and upgrades. See Grid Studies & Queues and IEEE 1547.
  • SREC registration: Systems must be certified/registered (e.g., via the PJM-GATS environment) to generate and sell SRECs — coordinate this with your installer/aggregator.

Bottom line

Maryland offers a diversified value stack: a tradable SREC market (with a 1.5× Certified SREC boost for qualifying systems through 2028), full retail net metering, a flat $1,000 residential rebate (plus MSAP for income-eligible homeowners), MEA commercial/canopy grants, storage incentives, and 100% sales- and property-tax exemptions. With §25D federal residential credit gone after 2025, residential owners lean on the state rebate, SRECs, and net metering. For commercial projects, combine MEA grants and SRECs with the federal §48E ITC and MACRS. The key action is to check MEA funding availability early (programs run out) and confirm Certified SREC eligibility for qualifying systems.
