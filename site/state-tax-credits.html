Overview

State-level incentives for commercial solar vary significantly by state. While the federal ITC/PTC provides the baseline incentive, many states offer additional tax credits, rebates, performance-based incentives (PBIs), property tax exemptions, sales tax exemptions, and SREC markets that significantly improve project economics.

Key Considerations for Commercial Solar EPC

  1. Stacking Rules: Most state incentives stack with federal ITC, but check "double-dipping" rules
  2. Tax Appetite: State tax credits require state tax liability; consider transferability
  3. Interconnection: Queue position and costs vary wildly by utility/state
  4. SREC/PBI Revenue: Model as revenue stream (not cost reduction) for pro forma
  5. Property Tax: Exemptions vary by assessor; confirm locally
  6. Sales Tax: Exemptions often require exemption certificates at purchase
  7. Interconnection Timelines: Vary from 30 days to 2+ years by utility/state
  8. Program Caps/Waitlists: Many PBI/SREC programs have capacity caps (NY-Sun, SMART, Illinois Shines)
  9. Depreciation: Most states conform to federal MACRS/bonus depreciation; confirm state conformity
  10. Local Incentives: Cities/counties/utility districts often have additional programs
